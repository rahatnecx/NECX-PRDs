2. Executive Summary

The Notes Center is the agency-scoped, cross-case surface through which caseworkers (VSMS staff) record, browse, search, and manage internal case notes across every case their agency owns. A note is always a private, caseworker-only annotation attached to exactly one case (service claim). Notes capture the operational reality of victim service delivery — safety concerns, follow-up actions, court updates, intake details, and service documentation — that staff must retain for coordination and continuity of care but that is never shown to the victim/survivor.

NECX serves victims and survivors of crime and the agencies and advocates who help them. Advocates work cases over long periods, often hand off between colleagues, and must keep a defensible, confidential record of what was observed, decided, and planned. Individual case detail pages already expose a per-case notes panel; the Notes Center elevates that same underlying data (claim_notes) into an agency-wide management view — a single place to see all notes across all cases, filter by note type, staff author, date, and free-text search, and act on any note without navigating case-by-case.

This document specifies the Notes Center as a distinct product surface: its note taxonomy (seven note types), full note lifecycle (create, edit, delete, and re-link to a different case), the cross-case browse/search/stat experience, and the permission model (notes_center.{view,add,edit,delete}) that gates it. It draws a firm boundary between internal notes (this feature — caseworker-only, never victim-facing) and published claim updates (a separate feature that is deliberately surfaced to victims). Confidentiality and strict per-agency (CJIS-style) isolation are the defining non-functional constraints.

Critically, this PRD also records known implementation risks flagged during discovery — including controller-to-service argument-mismatch defects in the notes-center controllers — so that the baseline is honest about current state and the hardening work is scoped explicitly.

3. Product Overview & Vision

3.1 Problem statement

Caseworkers accumulate a large volume of internal notes across many cases. When notes are only reachable one case at a time, staff cannot answer agency-level questions: *Which cases have open safety concerns? What did we log this week? Which follow-up notes did a particular advocate write before going on leave?* Supervisors have no consolidated view for oversight, quality assurance, or hand-off. At the same time, these notes are among the most sensitive data in the platform — they must remain caseworker-only and strictly scoped to the owning agency, and must never leak into victim-facing surfaces.

3.2 Product vision

Provide agency staff a single, fast, permission-gated Notes Center that lists every case note the agency owns, with rich filtering (type, staff, date, highlight), full-text search, at-a-glance stat cards (Total / Today / This Week / This Month), and inline create/edit/delete — while guaranteeing confidentiality, per-agency isolation, and clear attribution (who created and who last edited each note). The Notes Center is a *management lens* over the same claim_notes records used on case detail pages; a note created in either place is the same record.

3.3 Where this sits in the NECX ecosystem

  • Victim App (Flutter mobile): Never sees Notes Center content. Notes are internal-only; victims see published claim updates, not notes.
  • Workspace / VS360 web (staff): Hosts the Notes Center page and consumes the /web/notes API. Primary and only surface for this feature.
  • NECX Admin: Not a consumer of the Notes Center routes; NECX super-admin bypasses fine-grained permission checks at the middleware level but the surface remains agency-scoped in practice.
  • Unified backend (necx-backend_v2): Owns claim_notes, the notes-center app (its own routes/controller/service/validator/repository), and the shared ClaimNotes model that the per-case victim-services notes endpoints also use.

3.4 Relationship to the per-case notes panel

The victim-services module exposes per-case note endpoints (POST new-note/:claimUid, GET get-notes/:claimUid, PATCH update-note/:noteUid, DELETE delete-note/:noteUid). The Notes Center is a different surface over the same table — it adds agency-wide listing, filtering, stat cards, staff-filter options, and cross-case management. Both write to claim_notes. This PRD scopes the cross-case Notes Center surface (/web/notes); the per-case panel is referenced only at its integration points.

4. Goals, Objectives & Success Metrics

4.1 Goals

  • Give agency staff one consolidated, searchable view of all internal case notes their agency owns.
  • Support the full note lifecycle — create, edit, delete, and re-link a note to a different in-agency case — with clear author/editor attribution.
  • Enforce a strict confidentiality boundary: notes are caseworker-only and never appear on any victim-facing surface.
  • Enforce strict per-agency (cross-agency isolation) scoping on every read and write.
  • Gate every action behind granular permissions (notes_center.view/add/edit/delete).
  • Preserve the taxonomy of seven note types so notes are consistently classified and filterable.
  • Provide lightweight operational insight via stat cards and a highlight filter.

5. Scope

5.1 In Scope

  • The agency-scoped, cross-case Notes Center surface served at mount /web/notes (VS360 web only).
  • Note taxonomy: the seven note types — General Note, Case Update, Safety Concern, Follow-up Note, Service Note, Court Update, Intake Note.
  • Create a note on a case in the agency (POST /web/notes).
  • Edit a note — partial update of title / description / note type, and re-link (reassign) the note to another in-agency case (PATCH /web/notes/:id).
  • Delete a note — hard delete (DELETE /web/notes/:id).
  • Cross-case browse/search/list with pagination, sorting, date-range, note-type multi-filter, staff multi-filter, free-text search, and a "has highlight" filter (GET /web/notes).
  • Stat cards (Total / Today / This Week / This Month) and the staff-filter option list derived from the current result page.
  • Permission gating via checkPermission('notes_center', <action>) plus the allowOnly('VS360') context gate.
  • Strict per-agency scoping on every read and write via the claim_notes → service_claims → services → services.serviceCenterId join chain.
  • Author (createdBy) and editor (updatedBy) attribution semantics.
  • Audit-trail expectations for note mutations.
  • Client-side single-note and all-listed-notes PDF export (VS360 web utility).

5.2 Out of Scope

  • The per-case notes panel endpoints in victim-services (.../service-claims/*-note/*) — referenced only as an integration point; they share the claim_notes table but are a separate surface.
  • Published claim updates (claim_updates, isPublished) — the victim-facing progress-message feature that is deliberately distinct from internal notes (see §7.5 and §16).
  • Victim/APP access of any kind to notes (explicitly excluded — confidentiality boundary).
  • NECX Admin management of notes (no /admin notes routes exist).
  • Cross-agency sharing of notes as first-class objects. (Note content may be copied into a referral case snapshot when the canShareCaseNotes sharing flag is set on a consented referral; that is a one-time sanitized copy handled by the Referrals module, not live Notes Center access.)
  • Soft-delete / trash / restore for notes (current behavior is hard delete).
  • Real-time socket updates for the Notes Center (the VS360 page relies on query invalidation, not sockets).
  • Rich collaboration features (comments on notes, @-mentions, note threading, versioned history/diff) — not present.

7. System Context & Architecture

7.1 Tech stack

  • Backend: Node.js + Express (ESM), Objection.js ORM over Knex/PostgreSQL, with knexSnakeCaseMappers (DB snake_case ⇄ JS camelCase). The notes-center app is its own Express router mounted at /web/notes (API version prefix v2, e.g. /v2/web/notes).
  • Frontend: VS360 web app — React + Vite + Mantine UI + TanStack Query + Redux. Notes Center lives at src/pages/NotesCenter/ (NotesCenter.jsx, useNotesCenter.js, constants.js); API calls via src/services/notes.js against /notes. Response envelope { status, msg, data } (payload at response.data.data).
  • Key backend files: routes src/apps/notes-center/notes-center.route.js; controller notes-center.controller.js; service notes-center.service.js; validator notes-center.validator.js; repository src/db/repositories/claimNotesRepository.js; model src/model/v2/ClaimNotes.js.

7.2 How it talks to the backend

The VS360 Notes Center calls four REST endpoints under /web/notes (list, create, update, delete). Every request passes the standard middleware chain: rate-limiter → auth (populates req.issuer) → context gate allowOnly('VS360') → fine-grained checkPermission('notes_center', <action>) → validator (@tsed/exceptions-based validation, throwing BadRequest) → controller → service → claimNotesRepository. The service resolves the agency from the issuer and enforces scoping.

7.3 Real-time / notifications

The Notes Center has no socket events and issues no victim or staff notifications on note CRUD. The VS360 page keeps its data fresh purely via TanStack Query invalidation of the ["notes-center"] query key after a successful mutation. (Contrast with published claim updates, which *do* notify the victim.)

7.4 Tenancy & isolation

The Notes Center is strictly agency-scoped. Every read and write resolves the issuer's agency (requireAgencyId(issuer) / issuer.serviceCenterInfo.id) and constrains data through the join chain claim_notes → service_claims (claimId) → services (serviceId) filtered by services.serviceCenterId = agencyId. Creating or editing a note verifies the target case belongs to the issuer's agency (resolveAgencyScopedClaim / resolveAgencyScopedNote); scoping violations throw NotFound/Forbidden. This enforces CJIS-style cross-agency isolation: a caseworker can only ever see and touch notes on cases their own agency owns.

7.5 The internal-note vs published-update boundary (architectural)

Two separate tables underpin two separate features:

  • claim_notes (this feature) — internal, caseworker-only, never victim-visible.
  • claim_updates — progress messages with an isPublished flag; mobile filters to isPublished = true, and publishing triggers a victim notification.

They are distinct entities, distinct endpoints, distinct audiences. The Notes Center must never render or expose claim_updates, and the victim app must never receive claim_notes. This boundary is a core architectural invariant, restated in Scope (§5.2), NFRs (§13), and the Glossary (§16).

8. Functional Requirements

The Notes Center exposes four capabilities behind the /web/notes mount, each gated by allowOnly('VS360') and a specific notes_center permission. id in the path is the note's public uid (not the numeric PK).

8.1 Note taxonomy (note types)

Every note carries a noteType drawn from ClaimNotes.NOTE_TYPES. The enum (and its VS360 mirror in constants.js) is: General Note, Case Update, Safety Concern, Follow-up Note, Service Note, Court Update, Intake Note. Default is General Note. Note type is a first-class filter and sort key in the Notes Center.

Suggested semantics (for consistent staff usage; not enforced by the backend beyond the enum):

  • General Note — catch-all annotation; the default.
  • Case Update — internal progress/status commentary (distinct from a *published* victim-facing update).
  • Safety Concern — risk, threat, or safety-planning observation; the highest-sensitivity type.
  • Follow-up Note — planned or required follow-up action.
  • Service Note — documentation of a service delivered or coordinated.
  • Court Update — legal/court-process developments.
  • Intake Note — information captured at or about intake.

8.2 Cross-case browse, filter, search & stats (GET /web/notes)

Returns a paged, filtered list of the agency's notes plus stat counts and the staff-filter options. Query parameters: page, pageSize (≤ 200), startDate, endDate (ISO), types[], staffIds[], search, hasHighlight (boolean), sortField, sortDirection.

Behavior (fetchNotesCenter → findNotesForAgency + getAgencyNoteStats, run in parallel):

  • Agency scoping: requireAgencyId(issuer); the list join surfaces display fields — client name (via service_claims.victimId → app_users), services.title, and the creator/updater names (users aliased creator/updater).
  • Sorting: by createdAt, title, noteType, client name, case id, or createdBy (staff), in the requested direction.
  • Search: search matches note title, description, and client name.
  • Highlight filter: hasHighlight=true filters to notes whose description ILIKE '%<mark%' (i.e., contain highlighted/marked rich-text spans).
  • Stats: getAgencyNoteStats returns counts for total, today, this week, and this month (via date_trunc FILTER aggregations).
  • Staff filter options: availableStaff is the distinct set of authors present on the returned page (there is no separate endpoint for staff options).
  • Response shape: { notes, stats, pagination, availableStaff }.

9. Detailed User & System Flows

9.1 Add a note to a case

  1. A caseworker with notes_center.add opens the Notes Center and clicks "Add Note" (or adds from a case context that pre-fills the case).
  2. The create modal collects: Case (select an in-agency case → serviceClaimUid/claimUid), Title (≤ 200), Note Type (one of the seven), and a rich-text Description (≤ ~5,000 visible chars). Client-side validation (yup noteFormSchema) requires serviceClaimUid, title, note type, and description.
  3. On submit, the client calls POST /web/notes with { claimUid, title, description, noteType }.
  4. Middleware enforces allowOnly('VS360') and checkPermission('notes_center','add').
  5. The service validates the body, then resolveAgencyScopedClaim({claimUid, agencyId}) confirms the case exists and belongs to the issuer's agency. If not → 404/403.
  6. The service inserts the claim_notes row (new v7 uid, createdBy = issuer.id, updatedBy = null, claimId, title, description, noteType) via claimNotesRepository.createOne.
  7. An ActionLogs audit row is written for the create (see §13.5).
  8. The service returns the created note in the standard envelope; the client invalidates ["notes-center"] and the new note appears in the list and updates the stat cards.

Edge cases: invalid/blank required field → validation error surfaced inline; claimUid for a case in another agency → 403/404 (isolation); over-cap title/description → validation error.

9.2 Browse all notes in the Notes Center

  1. A caseworker with notes_center.view opens the Notes Center page.
  2. useNotesCenter.js issues GET /web/notes with defaults (page 1, pageSize 50, default sort by date desc).
  3. Middleware enforces allowOnly('VS360') + checkPermission('notes_center','view').
  4. The service resolves the agency (requireAgencyId), then runs findNotesForAgency (paged, joined list) and getAgencyNoteStats in parallel.
  5. The response { notes, stats, pagination, availableStaff } renders as: four stat cards (Total / Today / This Week / This Month), a filter bar, and a sortable table (columns: date, title, type, client, case, staff).
  6. The staff-filter dropdown is populated from availableStaff (distinct authors on the page).
  7. Clicking a row opens the detail drawer showing the full note (title, type, rich description, case/client, created-by, and — if updatedBy is set — edited-by).

9.3 Filter by type and/or case, search, and sort

  1. The user opens the filter bar and selects one or more note types → client sends types[].
  2. To narrow by case/client, the user types into search (matches title, description, and client name) → search=.
  3. To scope by author, the user selects one or more staff in the staff filter → staffIds[].
  4. The user optionally sets a date range (startDate/endDate) and/or toggles has highlight (hasHighlight=true, matching notes containing marked/highlighted text).
  5. The user clicks a sortable column header → client sends sortField + sortDirection (date/title/type/client/case/staff).
  6. The client re-issues GET /web/notes with the combined parameters; the service applies all filters within the agency scope and returns the filtered page + refreshed pagination. (Stat cards reflect agency totals by period, independent of the active filter.)
  7. Pagination controls (50/page on the client; server caps pageSize at 200) move between pages.

Note: filtering strictly to a single case is achieved via search / the case column; the primary agency-wide use is multi-case triage by type, staff, date, and keyword.

9.4 Edit a note

  1. From the list, the user opens a note's detail drawer and clicks "Edit" (requires notes_center.edit).
  2. The user changes any of: title, note type, description; and/or selects a different in-agency case to reassign the note.
  3. On save, the client calls PATCH /web/notes/:id (note uid) with only the changed fields (must be ≥ 1).
  4. Middleware enforces allowOnly('VS360') + checkPermission('notes_center','edit').
  5. The service resolveAgencyScopedNote verifies the note and its case are in-agency (else 404/403), then partial-patches the provided fields and always sets updatedBy = issuer.id.
  6. If claimUid was supplied, the service re-scopes the target case (in-agency check) and updates claimId to reassign the note.
  7. An ActionLogs update row is written. The client invalidates ["notes-center"]; the drawer now shows "edited by <user>".

Edge cases: empty patch → rejected; reassign to an out-of-agency case → 403/404; over-cap or bad noteType → validation error.

9.5 Delete a note

  1. From the detail drawer (or a row action), a user with notes_center.delete clicks "Delete".
  2. The client shows a confirmation prompt.
  3. On confirm, the client calls DELETE /web/notes/:id (note uid).
  4. Middleware enforces allowOnly('VS360') + checkPermission('notes_center','delete').
  5. The service verifies the note is in-agency (else 404/403), then hard-deletes the row.
  6. An ActionLogs delete row is written. The client invalidates ["notes-center"]; the note disappears and stat cards refresh.

Edge case: deleting a note in another agency → 403/404; there is no undo/restore (hard delete).

9.6 Export a note (or the current list) to PDF

  1. From a note's drawer, the user selects "Export" → the client renders that note to PDF (utils/noteExport.js).
  2. Alternatively, from the list toolbar the user selects "Export all listed" → the client renders the current result set to PDF.
  3. The export is a client-side operation; the confidentiality boundary applies (internal use only).

9.7 Cross-surface consistency (per-case panel ↔ Notes Center)

  1. A note created on a case detail page (via victim-services POST new-note/:claimUid) writes to the same claim_notes table.
  2. When the caseworker opens the Notes Center, that note appears in the agency-wide list (same record, same uid).
  3. Editing/deleting via either surface affects the single underlying record. (Guardrail: both surfaces must apply the same agency-scope checks; see §15 risks.)

9.8 Permission-denied and isolation branches (system flow)

  1. A staff user lacking the required notes_center action calls a Notes Center endpoint → checkPermission returns 403 (unless the issuer is NECX super-admin, who bypasses the fine-grained check).
  2. A non-VS360 issuer (e.g., APP victim, ADMIN) hitting /web/notes → allowOnly('VS360') returns 403.
  3. Any read/write referencing a case or note outside the issuer's agency → the service throws NotFound/Forbidden (isolation preserved; existence not confirmed to the caller).

11.3 External integrations

The Notes Center itself has no external integrations: no Twilio, no FCM, no email, no OpenAI/Anthropic, no Azure Blob usage on note CRUD. Indirect touchpoints:

  • Audit: ActionLogs.logAction(...) writes to action_logs (internal audit store) — see §13.5.
  • Referral snapshot (one-way copy): when a referral is consented with canShareCaseNotes = true, the Referrals module copies a sanitized subset of the case's notes into metadata.caseSnapshot. This is a point-in-time copy performed by the Referrals module — not live cross-agency Notes Center access.
  • AI report context: agency notes may be included in AI report context aggregation. This is a downstream read by the Reports subsystem, not a Notes Center feature.

12. RBAC & Permissions

Two layers gate every Notes Center action: the context gate allowOnly('VS360') (403 unless the issuer's roleInfo.context = VS360) and the fine-grained checkPermission('notes_center', <action>). The notes_center module actions are view, add, edit, delete, granted on the VSMS_ADMIN role template and assignable to other agency roles via Role Management (subject to the agency plan's permission catalog). NECX super-admin bypasses the fine-grained check inside the middleware; the allowOnly('VS360') context gate still applies to /web routes.

13. Non-Functional Requirements

13.1 Confidentiality & victim safety (paramount)

  • NFR-CONF-01: Notes (claim_notes) are caseworker-only and must never be returned to an APP (victim) issuer or rendered on any victim-facing surface. This is a hard invariant.
  • NFR-CONF-02: The Notes Center is the internal counterpart to victim-facing published updates; the two data sets must never be mixed in either direction.
  • NFR-CONF-03: Safety Concern notes (and all notes) must be treated as high-sensitivity data; UI and exports must reinforce internal-only handling.

13.2 Privacy & tenancy isolation

  • NFR-ISO-01: Every read and write must be constrained to the issuer's agency via the service_centers-scoped join; cross-agency access must be impossible (CJIS-style isolation).
  • NFR-ISO-02: Scoping violations must fail closed (NotFound/Forbidden) without confirming existence of out-of-agency records.
  • NFR-ISO-03: Any cross-agency propagation of note content occurs only via an explicit, consented referral snapshot (sanitized, one-way) — never via the Notes Center.

13.3 Performance & scalability

  • NFR-PERF-01: GET /web/notes P95 < 800 ms at default page size; list and stats computed in parallel.
  • NFR-PERF-02: pageSize is capped at 200 to bound query cost; client default is 50/page.
  • NFR-PERF-03: Filter/sort/search operations must remain performant on the agency's full note volume (indexed on claimId, createdBy, created_at, noteType recommended).

13.4 Availability & reliability

  • NFR-REL-01: Write operations must be atomic per note; a failed create/update/delete must not leave partial state.
  • NFR-REL-02: Endpoints are rate-limited (rateLimiters.standard, ~10/5s on writes) to protect the service.

13.5 Auditability

  • NFR-AUD-01: Create, update, and delete of a note must write an ActionLogs row (action, referenceType, actionDescription{source,ip,url,context}, referenceId = note) for a complete compliance audit trail.
  • NFR-AUD-02: Attribution must be preserved: createdBy on creation and updatedBy on every human edit (null updatedBy ⇒ never edited).

13.6 Localization & accessibility

  • NFR-LOC-01: Notes Center UI strings should be localizable via the VS360 client's i18n. (Note content itself is free text authored by staff.)
  • NFR-A11Y-01: The Notes Center list, filters, drawers, and rich-text editor must meet WCAG 2.1 AA (keyboard navigation, labels, contrast).

13.7 Data integrity & validation

  • NFR-VAL-01: Enforce noteType enum and size caps (title 200 / description 50,000) server-side; do not rely on client validation alone.
  • NFR-VAL-02: Rich HTML descriptions must be stored safely and rendered without XSS (sanitize on render).

13.8 Compliance

  • NFR-CMP-01: Because notes may inform grant/PMT narratives and legal proceedings, retention and audit of notes must satisfy the agency's record-keeping obligations; hard deletion is irreversible and must be a permissioned action.

14. Analytics, Reporting & Notifications

14.1 In-product analytics (stat cards)

The Notes Center surfaces four period counts derived from getAgencyNoteStats: Total, Today, This Week, This Month (via date_trunc FILTER aggregations, agency-scoped). These give supervisors an at-a-glance sense of note activity.

14.2 Operational insight via filters

  • The staff filter (availableStaff) enables per-caseworker review (quality assurance, hand-off checks).
  • The note-type filter enables risk triage (e.g., surfacing all Safety Concern notes) and process review (e.g., Court Update, Intake Note).
  • The has-highlight filter surfaces notes with emphasized content.

14.3 Notifications

  • None from the Notes Center. Note CRUD emits no socket events and no victim/staff notifications. Freshness on the VS360 client is handled by TanStack Query invalidation (["notes-center"]), not real-time push.
  • Contrast: published claim updates *do* notify the victim (victim_notifications + FCM + socket update-<receiverUid>); this is deliberately outside the Notes Center.

14.4 Downstream reporting

  • Notes may be incorporated into AI report context aggregation at the agency level (Reports subsystem), and note content may be copied into a referral snapshot when sharing is consented. Both are downstream consumers, not Notes Center capabilities.
