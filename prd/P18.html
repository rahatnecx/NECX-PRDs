2. Executive Summary

NECX serves victims and survivors of crime and the agencies, advocates, and caseworkers who help them. Before a victim ever becomes a formal "case," they typically reach out — asking a question about a published service, tapping "request to connect," calling a hotline, or walking into an agency office. Inquiries Management is the intake-and-triage layer that receives those first contacts, keeps them safe and anonymous, routes them to the right staff, and lets an advocate decide whether to engage, decline, or close the conversation.

An inquiry in NECX is a lightweight, victim-initiated contact that exists before a service claim (case). The system models it primarily as an anonymous inquiry thread — an INQUIRY-type chat thread paired with an InquiryLifecycle state row (pending → approved / rejected / resolved). A second, lighter intake signal — a request-to-connect — lets a victim express interest in a specific service without opening a chat; it surfaces to staff as a synthetic "requested" item alongside their cases. Offline contacts (hotline calls, walk-ins) are captured by staff and seeded directly into case creation.

This PRD documents Inquiries Management end to end: where inquiries come from, how staff triage them (approve/self-assign, reject, or close/resolve), how they are routed and notified, and — importantly — how an inquiry becomes a case. NECX deliberately has no one-click "convert inquiry to case" action. When an advocate decides an inquiry warrants formal service delivery, they open Create Case and seed the form from the inquiry's known details (name, phone, email carried through a prefill). The inquiry and the resulting service claim remain separate records, linked only implicitly once the client's contact information is linked to an account.

The goal is a fast, safe, low-friction front door: victims can ask for help anonymously; advocates can respond quickly and only escalate real cases into the case-management workflow; and the agency retains a clean audit trail and privacy posture throughout.

3. Product Overview & Vision

3.1 Problem Statement

Victims rarely arrive as fully-formed cases. They send tentative first messages, ask whether an agency can help, or request a callback — often anonymously and often at high emotional stakes. Without a dedicated intake surface, these first contacts either get lost in a general inbox, get prematurely turned into formal cases (polluting case metrics and PMT/grant data), or force victims to disclose identifying information before they are ready. Agencies need a way to receive, route, and triage these contacts safely, and to promote only the genuine ones into case management.

3.2 Product Vision

Inquiries Management is NECX's safe front door. It accepts anonymous, victim-initiated contact about a specific service or agency; routes each inquiry to the caseworkers responsible for that service category; and gives staff a simple triage decision — engage (approve and open a real-time chat), decline (reject and archive), or wrap up (resolve/close). It preserves victim anonymity by default, keeps inquiries out of formal case and reporting pipelines until an advocate consciously promotes them, and provides a clean, prefill-assisted bridge into Create Case when escalation is warranted.

3.3 Where This Sits in the NECX Ecosystem

Inquiries Management spans several NECX surfaces that share one unified backend:

  • Victim App (Flutter mobile) — where most inquiries originate: victims start an inquiry chat about a service or send a request-to-connect.
  • VS360 Web (React 18 + Vite + Mantine) — the primary staff triage surface: the "My Inquiries" screen (two-pane master/detail) where advocates approve, reject, close, and chat.
  • Workspace App (Flutter mobile, staff) — a mobile companion where caseworkers can view inquiry threads and perform the same approve/reject/resolve moderation.
  • NECX Admin — platform administration; not a routine inquiry surface.
  • Unified Backend (Node.js + Express, Objection/Knex over PostgreSQL, Socket.IO, Redis, BullMQ, FCM, Twilio) — owns the chat_threads, inquiry_lifecycle, chat_messages, and request_to_connect data and the triage endpoints.

Inquiries Management is upstream of Case Management (Service Claims): an inquiry may lead a staff member to create a case, but the two are distinct records. It shares the Chat & Messaging backbone (the inquiry conversation is an INQUIRY thread) and feeds the Dashboard inquiry KPIs (new/closed/total inquiries, open-inquiries count).

4. Goals, Objectives & Success Metrics

4.1 Goals & Objectives

  • Provide a safe, anonymous front door for victim-initiated first contact, with no requirement to disclose identity to start an inquiry.
  • Route every new inquiry to the caseworkers responsible for the relevant service category within the correct agency.
  • Give staff a fast, unambiguous triage decision: approve (engage), reject (decline), or resolve/close (wrap up).
  • Keep inquiries out of formal case metrics and PMT/grant reporting until an advocate consciously promotes them to a case.
  • Support a clean, low-friction bridge from an inquiry to a new case via prefill-seeded Create Case, while keeping the two records distinct.
  • Notify both parties reliably and in real time (in-app socket, FCM push, email/SMS) at each lifecycle transition.
  • Preserve a complete audit trail of triage actions for compliance and cross-agency isolation.

5. Scope

5.1 In Scope

  • Inquiry sources / intake: victim-initiated inquiry chat (start-inquiry), victim request-to-connect from a published service, and staff-captured offline contacts (hotline call wrap-up, walk-in) seeded into Create Case.
  • Inquiry lifecycle & triage: the InquiryLifecycle state machine (pending → approved / rejected / resolved) and the staff actions Approve (self-assign + open chat), Reject (archive), and Close/Resolve.
  • VS360 "My Inquiries" surface: two-pane list/detail, status filtering (All / Pending / Approved / Resolved / Rejected), unread-message-first sorting, and the triage/chat detail panes.
  • Inquiry chat: real-time messaging on approved/resolved inquiry threads, file attachments, read receipts, message translation (shared chat backbone).
  • The manual path to a case: opening Create Case with prefill seeded from an inquiry's known details, and the explicit fact that there is no one-click convert-to-case.
  • Inquiry↔service-claim relationship: how a request-to-connect surfaces alongside service claims, and how an inquiry and any resulting case remain separate records linked only via the client.
  • Routing, RBAC, notifications, data model, endpoints, NFRs, and risks for the above.

5.2 Out of Scope

  • Case Management / Service Claims internals (approve/reject/resolve of cases, notes, updates, files, referrals, PMT questionnaire capture) — covered by the Case Management PRD; referenced here only where an inquiry hands off to it.
  • Hotline call handling and telephony (Twilio call flows, callbacks, transcripts) — covered by the Hotline PRD; only the wrap-up-to-Create-Case seam is referenced.
  • General chat thread types other than INQUIRY (SERVICE, INTERNAL, AGENCY_CHAT) — covered by the Chat & Messaging PRD.
  • Referrals and partnerships — covered by their own PRD.
  • Reporting/PMT/grants beyond the inquiry KPIs surfaced on the dashboard.
  • An automated "convert inquiry → case" feature — explicitly not built today; noted as an Open Question / future enhancement.
  • A dedicated request_to_connect triage screen — request-to-connect items currently surface within the cases/claims list as synthetic "requested" entries, not on the Inquiries screen.

When she opens VS360, the Inquiries screen shows new anonymous inquiries about those services first (unread messages float to the top). She reads the victim's opening message, and if it looks like a genuine request she taps Approve Inquiry — which self-assigns it to her and opens a live chat. If it is spam or clearly misdirected, she taps Reject Inquiry. When she and the victim have resolved the question, she taps Close Inquiry.

7. System Context & Architecture

7.1 Tech Stack

  • VS360 Web (staff triage): React 18 + Vite, Mantine v7, Redux Toolkit + redux-persist (auth/user, notifications, refetch triggers, threads.inquiryThreads), TanStack React Query v4, react-router-dom v6, socket.io-client for realtime. Inquiry services live in src/services/threads.js, src/services/chat.js, src/services/notification.js.
  • Victim App (intake): Flutter; InquiryChatController / InboxController over a REST + Socket.IO chat service; request-to-connect via the victim-services API.
  • Workspace App (staff mobile): Flutter; inquiry moderation via the same chat-services endpoints.
  • Backend: Node.js + Express (ESM), Objection/Knex over PostgreSQL, Socket.IO, Redis, BullMQ (email/SMS jobs), FCM (push), Twilio (SMS/hotline). Inquiry logic lives in the chat app (src/apps/chat/chat-{routes,controllers,services}.js) and the victim-services module (request-to-connect).

7.2 How the Frontend Talks to the Backend

  • Axios factory (src/services/axios.js), base URL from VITE_APP_BACKEND_BASE_URL. Every request carries Authorization: Bearer <accessToken>, x-refresh-token, X-Netlify-Secret, X-Environment.
  • Responses are AES-encrypted; the response interceptor decrypts string payloads. Refresh tokens rotate via the x-refresh-token response header. A 401 triggers logout; a 403 renders UnauthorizedAccess.
  • API version prefix v2; staff web routes are mounted under /web/* and gated by allowOnly('VS360'); victim routes under /mobile/* (auth-only, no context guard).

7.3 Real-Time & Notifications

  • Socket.IO rooms: per-user vs360-user-<uid>, victim app-notification-room / notification-room, and per-thread thread-<threadUid>-<userUid>.
  • New-inquiry creation emits thread-creation-inquiry; new messages emit message-inquiry with DB notification type INQUIRY_NEW_MESSAGE. Approval emits inquiry-approve-for-<victimUid> and an app-side INQUIRY_APPROVED notification (FCM). VS360 uses Redux refetch.chatRefetch / specificChatRefetch and setInquiryThreadsRefetch triggers, fed by these socket events, to re-fetch live.
  • Notification delivery combines a DB row (service_center_notifications for staff, victim_notifications for victims) + socket emit + FCM push + optional templated email/SMS via BullMQ. All notification calls are wrapped defensively (failures are logged, never break the main transaction).

7.4 Tenancy & Isolation

Every agency-scoped read is filtered by issuer.serviceCenterInfo.id; a VS360 user only ever sees their own agency's inquiries. Inquiry routing further narrows by the staff member's assigned service categories (getInquiryThreadsForVSMSUser). CJIS-style cross-agency isolation is enforced server-side.

8. Functional Requirements

8.1 Inquiry Sources (Intake Channels)

An inquiry is any victim-initiated first contact that exists before a formal case. NECX has three intake paths, of which the first is the canonical "inquiry."

(a) Victim inquiry chat (canonical inquiry). From a service detail screen in the Victim App, a victim starts an inquiry via POST /mobile/chat-services/start-inquiry with inquiryData { serviceCenterId, serviceId }. The backend dedups an existing ACTIVE INQUIRY thread on (victimId, serviceId); if none exists it creates a new chat_threads row (type='INQUIRY', status='ACTIVE') and inserts an inquiry_lifecycle row with status='pending'. Victims may be anonymous (identified by a code) at this stage.

(b) Request-to-connect (lightweight interest signal). From a service, a victim can express interest without opening a chat via PUT /mobile/victim-services/:uid/request-to-connect. This creates a RequestToConnect record, emails the service center, dedupes (returns 409 if the victim has already requested to connect for that service), and surfaces to staff as a synthetic "requested" item in the service-claims list response (requestsToConnect[]) rather than on the Inquiries screen.

(c) Offline contacts (hotline call, walk-in). Inquiries also arrive by phone or in person. These are captured by staff at the point of contact. There is no server-side "offline inquiry" record; instead the caller/visitor details are carried into Create Case via the prefill mechanism (e.g., the hotline wrap-up flow seeds caller name/email/phone into the Create Case modal).

8.2 Inquiry Lifecycle & Triage

An inquiry's state lives in inquiry_lifecycle.status, one of pending, approved, rejected, resolved. The lifecycle is driven by staff triage actions on VS360 (or the Workspace app), all mounted under /web/chat-services:

  • Approve — PATCH /web/chat-services/inquiry/approve/:id. Sets lifecycle approved, self-assigns the inquiry to the acting staff member (vsmsId = issuer.id), marks thread notifications read, sends the victim an INQUIRY_APPROVED FCM notification and socket inquiry-approve-for-<victimUid>, and writes an ActionLogs entry. The inquiry thread becomes a live chat.
  • Reject — PATCH /web/chat-services/inquiry/reject/:id. Sets lifecycle rejected and archives the thread (chat_threads.status='ARCHIVED'). Notifies the victim (INQUIRY_REJECTED).
  • Resolve / Close — PATCH /web/chat-services/inquiry/resolve/:id. Sets lifecycle resolved (the VS360 "Close Inquiry" button maps to this). Notifies the victim (INQUIRY_RESOLVED).

The VS360 detail pane switches on the inquiry's status: pending shows a triage card (last message + Approve/Reject); rejected shows a read-only "This inquiry is rejected" card; approved/resolved shows the active inquiry chat.

8.5 Promoting an Inquiry to a Case (No One-Click Convert)

NECX intentionally provides no one-click "convert inquiry → case" action. When an advocate determines that an inquiry warrants formal service delivery, they open the Create Case flow (CreateCaseModal, submitting POST /service-claims) and seed it via the prefill prop — carrying the victim's name/phone/email as known from the inquiry (or hotline/walk-in). The advocate selects the service, completes required intake, and submits.

Because Create Case is a separate action, the inquiry and the resulting service claim are distinct records. They are not linked by a foreign key at creation. They become associated only implicitly, once the client's contact information is linked to a user account (via the Case Management "Link Client Contact" flow / login-code activation), so that the client's app shows both their inquiry thread and their case.

9. Detailed User & System Flows

9.1 An Inquiry Arrives (Victim Starts an Inquiry Chat)

  1. In the Victim App, the victim opens a service detail screen and taps to start an inquiry (InquiryChatController.handleChat()).
  2. The app calls POST /mobile/chat-services/start-inquiry with inquiryData { serviceCenterId, serviceId }.
  3. The backend checks for an existing ACTIVE INQUIRY thread on (victimId, serviceId); if one exists it returns that thread (dedup) and opens it — no new record.
  4. Otherwise it creates a chat_threads row (type='INQUIRY', status='ACTIVE', victimId, serviceId) and an inquiry_lifecycle row with status='pending'.
  5. The backend routes the new inquiry: it emits thread-creation-inquiry to the agency's staff whose service categories match the service, sends an agency email + SMS, and creates staff notification rows.
  6. VS360 receives the socket event, fires setInquiryThreadsRefetch, and the new pending inquiry appears at (or near) the top of the "My Inquiries" list (unread-first sort).
  7. The victim can send an opening message immediately; it is delivered via message-inquiry / INQUIRY_NEW_MESSAGE and previewed in the staff list row.

9.2 Triage It — Approve & Engage (Happy Path)

  1. Maya opens VS360 → "My Inquiries"; the pending inquiry is surfaced (unread badge).
  2. She selects it; the detail pane shows the triage card with the victim's last message and Approve Inquiry / Reject Inquiry buttons.
  3. She taps Approve Inquiry → approveInquiry(id) → PATCH /web/chat-services/inquiry/approve/:id.
  4. The backend sets inquiry_lifecycle.status='approved', assigns vsmsId = issuer.id (self-assign), marks thread notifications read, and writes an ActionLogs row.
  5. The backend notifies the victim: INQUIRY_APPROVED FCM push + socket inquiry-approve-for-<victimUid> (to notification-room).
  6. On success, VS360 marks thread notifications read (MarkThreadNotificationsRead) and triggers a targeted refetch; the detail pane switches to the ActiveInquiry chat.
  7. Maya and the victim exchange messages in real time (POST /chat-services/send-message/:threadUid, live via message-inquiry), with file attachments and translation as needed.

9.3 Triage It — Reject (Spam / Misdirected)

  1. In the pending triage card, Maya taps Reject Inquiry → rejectInquiry(id) → PATCH /web/chat-services/inquiry/reject/:id.
  2. The backend sets inquiry_lifecycle.status='rejected' and archives the thread (chat_threads.status='ARCHIVED').
  3. The victim is notified (INQUIRY_REJECTED); an ActionLogs row is written.
  4. VS360 marks thread notifications read and refetches; the detail pane now shows the read-only "This inquiry is rejected" card. No further messaging is possible on the archived thread.

9.4 Close / Resolve an Active Inquiry

  1. After the conversation has served its purpose, Maya opens the active inquiry chat and taps Close Inquiry → closeInquiry(id) → PATCH /web/chat-services/inquiry/resolve/:id.
  2. The backend sets inquiry_lifecycle.status='resolved' and notifies the victim (INQUIRY_RESOLVED).
  3. The inquiry moves under the "Resolved" filter; the chat history remains viewable read-only/archived per thread rules. The inquiry KPI "closed inquiries" increments.

9.5 Open Create Case Prefilled From the Inquiry (Promotion to a Case)

  1. During or after an approved inquiry, Maya decides the victim needs formal service delivery.
  2. She opens Create Case (CreateCaseModal), which receives a prefill seeded from the inquiry's known details (name/phone/email).
  3. She selects the Service (limited to her assigned categories), confirms/edits the prefilled contact fields, completes optional demographics and any required service intake questions.
  4. She submits → POST /service-claims (CreateCaseWithUser). Because she is an advocate creating the case, the claim may auto-approve and, if the client is new with an OTP channel, a login code is generated and displayed for the client to activate on the mobile app.
  5. The new case appears in Case Management as a distinct record. The inquiry remains in Inquiries Management (typically resolved/closed by Maya separately). The two are linked only once the client's contact is linked to an account, after which the client's app shows both.

9.6 Request-to-Connect Intake (Lightweight Signal)

  1. From a service in the Victim App, the victim taps "request to connect" → PUT /mobile/victim-services/:uid/request-to-connect.
  2. The backend creates a RequestToConnect record, emails the service center, and dedups — returning 409 if the victim has already requested to connect for that service.
  3. The request surfaces to staff as a synthetic "requested" entry in the service-claims list response (requestsToConnect[]) — on the cases/claims surface, not the Inquiries screen.
  4. A staff member follows up (e.g., by opening Create Case with prefill, or reaching out), converting interest into a case or a conversation as appropriate.

9.7 Offline Contact (Hotline / Walk-In) Triage

  1. A victim calls the hotline or walks into the agency office.
  2. Staff capture the caller/visitor details during the interaction (hotline wrap-up flow, or manual entry).
  3. There is no server-side standalone inquiry record; staff open Create Case with prefill carrying the captured name/email/phone.
  4. Staff select the service, complete intake, and submit — creating the case directly. (If the victim only wanted information, staff may instead answer inline with no record, or start an inquiry on the victim's behalf where the victim has the app.)

9.8 Edge Cases & Branches

  1. Duplicate inquiry: a victim starting a second inquiry for the same (victimId, serviceId) while one is ACTIVE is returned the existing thread (no duplicate).
  2. Duplicate request-to-connect: a repeated request for the same service returns 409 and creates no new record.
  3. Anonymous throughout: an inquiry can complete its entire lifecycle without the victim ever disclosing identity; only an anonymous code is shown to staff.
  4. Category mismatch: an inquiry whose service maps to no assigned caseworker still belongs to the agency; an Agency Admin/Super Admin can triage it (they are not restricted by category).
  5. Rejected then re-contact: after a rejection the thread is archived; a victim who reaches out again starts a fresh inquiry (subject to the active-thread dedup rule).
  6. Notification failure: if socket/FCM/email delivery fails, the triage transaction still commits (notifications are best-effort, wrapped defensively).

13. Non-Functional Requirements

13.1 Security & Privacy (Victim Safety)

  • Anonymity by default: inquiries can be created and triaged without the victim disclosing identity; staff see only an anonymous code until contact is linked. Identity minimization is a first-class requirement.
  • Encrypted transport & payloads: all API responses are AES-encrypted and decrypted client-side; mobile access is additionally gated by the app-secret header (X-Netlify-Secret / x-mobile-app-secret).
  • Cross-agency isolation (CJIS-style): every inquiry read/write is scoped to issuer.serviceCenterInfo.id; staff can never see or act on another agency's inquiries.
  • Authorization on every thread: message send/read is authorized per thread (isAuthorizedForThread).
  • Auditability: triage actions (approve/reject/resolve) write ActionLogs rows (action, referenceId, referenceType, source/IP/URL/context) for a complete compliance trail.

13.2 Performance & Scalability

  • Inquiry list and message history are paginated; VS360 uses infinite-scroll for chat.
  • Real-time updates ride Socket.IO with Redis pub/sub for horizontal scaling; email/SMS are offloaded to BullMQ queues so triage responses are not blocked by delivery latency.
  • Rate limiting: rateLimiters.chatApi on the chat/inquiry tree; standard on victim-services routes.

13.3 Availability & Reliability

  • Notification delivery is best-effort and defensively wrapped: a failed socket/FCM/email send is logged and never rolls back or blocks the triage transaction.
  • Dedup guards (active-thread, request-to-connect 409) prevent duplicate intake records under retries or double-taps.

13.4 Localization & Accessibility

  • Inquiry messages support server-side translation to the recipient's preferred language (translatedContent).
  • Victim App static strings are localized (platform static translations); the emergency-exit affordance remains available over inquiry screens for victim safety.
  • VS360 uses Mantine components with standard keyboard/focus semantics.

13.5 Compliance & Reporting

  • Inquiries are deliberately kept out of case metrics and PMT/grant reporting until promoted to a case, preventing intake noise from distorting funded-service reporting.
  • Inquiry KPIs (new/closed/total, open inquiries) are reported at the aggregate/dashboard level only.

14. Analytics, Reporting & Notifications

14.1 Dashboards & KPIs

  • The VS360 dashboard KPI feed (GET /dashboard/kpi-data) includes new inquiries, closed inquiries, and total inquiries; the admin dashboard variant surfaces an open inquiries count. These are computed by the backend Dashboard model (getInquiriesCountByStatus) from inquiry_lifecycle.
  • Inquiries are excluded from case/claim counts and from PMT demographic aggregations, which draw from pmt_report (created only when a case is created).

16. Glossary

TermMeaning
InquiryA victim-initiated first contact about a service that exists before a formal case; modeled as an INQUIRY chat thread + an InquiryLifecycle state row.
InquiryLifecycleThe DB record (inquiry_lifecycle) holding an inquiry's triage status (pending/approved/rejected/resolved).
Inquiry ThreadThe chat_threads row of type='INQUIRY' carrying the inquiry conversation; status is ACTIVE or ARCHIVED.
TriageThe staff act of deciding an inquiry's fate: approve, reject, or resolve/close.
Request-to-ConnectA lightweight victim signal of interest in a service (RequestToConnect), surfaced to staff as a synthetic "requested" item, distinct from an inquiry thread.
Requested (status)Synthetic status applied to request-to-connect entries shown alongside service claims (requestsToConnect[]).
Case / Service ClaimA formal request for a service (service_claims, uid = CL<yy><month><uuid6>); the entity an inquiry may be promoted into.
PrefillData (name/phone/email) carried from an inquiry or offline contact into the Create Case modal to seed a new case.
Convert-to-CaseThe (deliberately manual) act of creating a case from an inquiry via prefill; NECX has no one-click convert action.
Self-AssignOn approval, an inquiry is assigned to the approving staff member (vsmsId = issuer.id).
Anonymous CodeThe non-identifying code by which a victim appears to staff until contact info is linked.
Advocate / Case WorkerFront-line agency staff (issuer.type = VSMS) who triage inquiries and manage cases.
Service Center / AgencyAn organization (service_centers) publishing services and triaging its own inquiries; the tenancy boundary.
VSMSVictim Services Management System — the staff identity/context (issuer.type = VSMS, roleInfo.context = VS360).
Login CodeA one-time code issued when an advocate creates a case for a victim, letting the victim claim the case on the mobile app.
Service CategoryTaxonomy grouping services; also the routing key that decides which caseworkers see a given inquiry.
PMTPerformance Measurement Tool — grant/federal reporting data created with a case (not with an inquiry).
