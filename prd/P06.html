2. Executive Summary

NECX Shelter Management is the module through which victim-services agencies model, operate, and share their emergency-shelter capacity, and through which victims/survivors of crime discover shelters and request a bed. It serves two distinct audiences from a single unified backend: agency staff (VSMS caseworkers and shelter coordinators) working in the VS360 web application, and victims/survivors working in the NECX mobile app.

For agency staff the module provides a complete physical model of shelter inventory — a hierarchy of shelter location → floor → room → bed — plus the operational tooling to keep that inventory accurate in real time: checking clients in and out of beds, running a prioritized waitlist, handling inter-agency bed-transfer requests, triaging bed requests submitted from the mobile app, tracking maintenance, and recording an auditable history of every event (check-in, check-out, transfer, incident, note). Because shelter is frequently the most time-critical service a victim needs, the module is engineered around live availability: every structural or occupancy change re-computes availability and is pushed over Socket.IO to open staff and mobile clients, and mobile-facing events are additionally delivered by FCM push.

A defining behavior of the module is that assigning a walk-in client to a bed can create that client's NECX account and issue a one-time login code, immediately connecting a person in crisis to the platform (their case, chat with the agency, documents, and future services) without requiring them to self-register. This is the same advocate-created login-code pattern used elsewhere in NECX, applied at the shelter intake point.

For victims the module exposes a safe, privacy-aware discovery surface: search and filter shelters, view them on a map with directions, inspect live room/bed layout, submit a bed request or join a waitlist, and track request status live. Because the physical safety of survivors depends on it, shelters may be marked confidential, in which case their exact address and coordinates are withheld from the mobile surface until after an intake interview.

3. Product Overview & Vision

3.1 Problem Statement

Emergency shelter is scarce, time-sensitive, and safety-critical. Agencies typically track beds in spreadsheets or on paper, which cannot represent real-time occupancy, cannot be safely shared with partner agencies, and give victims no self-service way to find an open bed. The result is wasted capacity (empty beds while people wait), unsafe disclosure of shelter locations, duplicated intake effort, and no auditable record of who was where and when. Victims in crisis — often at night, often on a phone — have no reliable way to find a shelter that fits their needs (family, pets, accessibility, LGBTQ-affirming) and confirm a bed is available before travelling to it.

3.2 Product Vision

Shelter Management makes every bed in the NECX network visible, assignable, and safely shareable in real time. Staff maintain an accurate physical model of their shelters and move clients through it (waitlist → assigned → checked-in → checked-out) with a single source of truth. Victims discover appropriate shelters and request beds directly from their phone, with confidential locations protected until intake. Availability is always live: a bed freed in one place, a request submitted from a phone, or a transfer accepted between agencies is reflected across every connected surface within moments, and the entire lifecycle is auditable for compliance and grant reporting.

3.3 Position in the NECX Ecosystem

Shelter Management is one feature module of the unified NECX platform, which serves four clients from one backend (Express + Objection/Knex over PostgreSQL, Socket.IO, Redis, Twilio, FCM):

  • VS360 web (agency staff) — the primary management surface for shelter inventory and operations, mounted under /web/shelters/* and guarded by allowOnly('VS360').
  • NECX mobile app (victims/survivors) — the discovery and request surface, mounted under /mobile/* (auth-only, no context guard).
  • Unified backend — hosts the shelter data model, business logic, real-time emitters, and notification fan-out; ties shelter check-in into the platform's identity (app_users), case (service_claims), login-code, and notification subsystems.
  • NECX Admin — the platform super-admin console does not directly operate shelters, but platform-level RBAC, plan limits, and reference data (states/cities, service categories) apply.

Shelter Management shares cross-cutting platform machinery documented in the NECX Backend Core: identity and issuer, RBAC (allowOnly + checkPermission), the login-code service, the notification delivery pipeline (DB row + Socket.IO + FCM + optional email), and the ActionLogs audit trail.

4. Goals, Objectives & Success Metrics

4.1 Goals & Objectives

  • Provide agencies an accurate, real-time physical model of shelter capacity (location → floor → room → bed) whose true capacity is governed by actual bed rows, not a static number.
  • Make bed assignment fast enough for crisis intake, including one-step creation of a victim account and login code for walk-ins.
  • Reduce unused capacity by matching waiting clients to freed beds via a prioritized waitlist and available-bed detection.
  • Enable safe inter-agency collaboration by sharing bed availability (not bed-level detail) with accepted partner agencies and supporting bed-transfer requests.
  • Give victims a safe, self-service way to find shelters and request beds, while protecting confidential shelter locations until intake.
  • Keep every surface live via Socket.IO + FCM, and keep every action auditable for compliance and grant reporting.

5. Scope

5.1 In Scope

  • Shelter physical hierarchy CRUD: locations, floors, rooms, beds — with structural constraints (room capacity, location-unique bed numbers, delete guards for occupied/active children).
  • Bed availability and occupancy computation and real-time propagation.
  • Client-to-bed assignment (check-in) including optional creation of an app_user, an approved shelter service_claim, and issuance of a one-time login code to the victim.
  • Check-out and bed-to-bed transfer of active assignments.
  • Waitlist: staff walk-in add, update/cancel, and promotion to a bed (with auto bed selection).
  • Mobile bed-request lifecycle (submit → triage: accept/reject/cancel/waitlist) with cross-notification.
  • Inter-agency transfer requests (outgoing/incoming), accept (auto check-in) and decline, gated by shelters.manage_transfers.
  • Maintenance items CRUD and a server-rendered maintenance PDF/report.
  • Shelter history/event log and incident logging.
  • Stats/rollups: system-wide and per-location bed/occupancy/waitlist.
  • Partner-agency shelter availability visibility (accepted partnerships only).
  • Client search over app_users for assignment.
  • Victim-facing shelter discovery: search/filter/sort/distance, detail, layout, directions (with confidential-location protection), join-waitlist, submit request, "My Shelters"/"My requests" tracking, recent searches.
  • Real-time delivery via the /shelters and /me Socket.IO namespaces and the service-center-<id> room; mobile FCM + in-app notifications.

5.2 Out of Scope

  • Billing, funding, or bed-night cost accounting (grant/PMT reporting is handled by the Reports module; time tracking by Staff Time).
  • Case management beyond the automatic shelter service_claim created at check-in (full casework lives in the Claims/Cases module).
  • Chat/messaging with the client (handled by the Chat & Inquiries module; shelter check-in only creates the account/claim that those modules can then use).
  • In-app payments, reservations with holds/deposits, or occupancy forecasting/analytics beyond the stats rollups described here.
  • Hotline call handling, communities, referrals, and partnership management workflows (own modules; this PRD consumes only partnership acceptance state and partner shelter availability).
  • Native map rendering internals of the mobile client (Google Maps/Directions integration is the app's concern; the backend supplies coordinates and a directions payload).

Dana — Shelter Coordinator. Dana sets up a new emergency shelter in VS360: she creates the location, adds two floors, several rooms with capacities, and the beds within them. Throughout the day she watches the bed board update live as walk-ins arrive and clients leave, promotes waitlisted families when family rooms open, and answers bed requests coming in from the mobile app.

Miguel — Intake Advocate. A survivor arrives at the shelter at 11 p.m. with no NECX account. Miguel opens the assign-client modal, enters the person's name and phone, sets the expected stay, and checks them into an available bed. Sam — Survivor (app user). Sam, fleeing an unsafe home late at night, opens the NECX app, filters for a shelter that accepts pets and is accessible, sees which have open beds nearby, and submits a bed request. Sam gets a push notification when staff accept and assign a bed, with the check-in window and (for non-confidential shelters) directions.

7. System Context & Architecture

7.1 Technology & Placement

Shelter Management is implemented in the NECX backend (necx-backend_v2, Express ESM + Objection/Knex over PostgreSQL, Socket.IO, Redis, Twilio, FCM). Web endpoints live in shelter-management/shelter.{routes,controller,services,validators}.js; mobile endpoints in shelter-mobile.{routes,controller,services,validators}.js. All routers register in src/apps/index.js under the global /v2 API prefix. Data lives in the Shelter* Objection models under src/model/v2/.

  • VS360 web client (vs360-webapp, React + Vite + Mantine + TanStack Query + Redux) calls the web endpoints via src/services/shelters.js (all under /shelters/...). Screens: ShelterLocations.jsx (route /shelter) and BedManagement.jsx (route /shelter/{locationId}).
  • Mobile client (necx-app_v2, Flutter + GetX) calls the mobile endpoints via the Shelters API service and ShelterController; realtime via ShelterSocketService.

7.2 Backend Communication

Every request flows through the platform middleware chain: rate-limiter → auth (populates req.issuer) → context gate (allowOnly) → permission gate (checkPermission) → validator (Joi) → controller → service → repository/model. Web shelter routes are guarded by allowOnly('VS360') and rate-limited with the dedicated rateLimiters.shelter bucket; mobile shelter routes are auth-only (app user) and use rateLimiters.standard. Services return { status, msg?, data? }; controllers echo the status. Agency data is multi-tenant: web reads/writes are scoped to issuer.serviceCenterInfo.id so an agency only ever sees/edits its own shelters.

7.3 Real-Time & Notifications

Two dedicated Socket.IO namespaces (both authenticated by socketAuthentication) carry shelter events:

  • /shelters — clients emit('subscribe', { shelter_uid, channels:['layout','availability'] }); the server joins rooms shelter:<uid>:layout and shelter:<uid>:availability and replies subscribed.
  • /me — auto-joins user:<appUserId>; carries per-user request updates and directory broadcasts.

Internal service emits funnel through _emitShelterRealtime(io, locationRef, { bed?, structural? }), which recomputes availability totals and fires availability.changed plus bed.status_changed and/or layout.changed. Web-staff notifications about mobile-originated activity go to the agency room service-center-<id> (event shelter-request). Mobile users receive both Socket.IO events (on /me and shelter-status-<uid>) and FCM + in-app notifications for status changes.

7.4 Tenancy & Identity

Staff callers carry issuer.type='VSMS', issuer.serviceCenterInfo.{id,uid}, and issuer.vsmsUserInfo[0].id. App users carry issuer.id = app_users.id. Shelter data is owned by a service center via shelter_locations.serviceCenterId; all agency-scoped reads filter on the caller's service center. Waitlist rows distinguish origin by appUserId (NULL = staff walk-in; non-null = mobile request). Assignments record assignedBy/performedBy as the vsms_users row id for audit.

8.1 Shelter Hierarchy (Location → Floor → Room → Bed)

Agencies model physical inventory as a four-level hierarchy. True capacity is governed by actual shelter_beds rows, not a location's initial totalBeds figure. Structural constraints are enforced server-side, and every structural change emits realtime updates.

9. Detailed User & System Flows

9.1 Create Shelter Structure (Location → Floor → Room → Bed)

  1. A Shelter Coordinator with shelters.add opens Shelter Locations in VS360 and clicks "Add Location".
  2. They complete the location form (name, type, address/neighborhood/zip, contact + mobile-visibility toggles, lat/lng, feature switches incl. confidentialLocation, services/languages, policies such as max_stay_nights/id_required, description/notes) and submit → POST /shelters/locations/create.
  3. (Optional) They upload up to 5 images → POST /shelters/locations/{uid}/upload-images; images merge into imageUrls.
  4. The backend inserts the shelter_locations row, emits shelter.directory_changed (created) and availability.changed, and returns the location.
  5. In Bed Management → Structure, the coordinator adds a floor → POST /shelters/floors/create (floor number auto-increments if omitted).
  6. Within the floor, they add a room with a capacity → POST /shelters/rooms/create.
  7. Within the room, they add beds → POST /shelters/beds/create; each bed creation enforces the room capacity and a location-unique bedNumber.
  8. Each structural insert triggers _emitShelterRealtime(..., { structural:true }) → availability.changed + layout.changed; subscribed mobile clients refetch layout.
  9. Edge cases: reducing a room's capacity below its bed count is rejected; deleting a floor/room with active beds beneath is blocked; deleting an occupied bed is blocked. Effective capacity always reflects actual bed rows.

9.2 Check Availability

  1. Staff open Shelter Locations; KPI cards show Locations, Total Beds, Occupancy Rate, and Waitlist total via GET /shelters/stats.
  2. Each shelter card shows an occupancy progress bar (occupied/reserved/maintenance) and bed counts from GET /shelters/locations (per-shelter stats).
  3. Opening a shelter loads GET /shelters/locations/{uid}/stats, .../beds, .../beds/available, and .../assignments/active.
  4. The Bed Board groups beds by status (Available, Occupied, Reserved, Maintenance, Out of Service); occupancy rate = occupied ÷ total; waitlist count = waitlisted OR (waiting AND appUserId IS NULL).
  5. While the screen is open, availability.changed and bed.status_changed events keep counts and the board live without a manual refresh.
  6. On mobile, GET /mobile/shelters returns per-shelter open-bed counts and distance so a victim sees availability before travelling.

9.3 Assign a Client to a Bed (End-to-End, with Login-Code Issuance)

  1. From an Available bed on the Bed Board, an Intake Advocate clicks "Assign".
  2. They choose a mode: New Client, Existing Client (GET /shelters/clients/search?q=), or From Waitlist (GET /shelters/locations/{uid}/waitlist).
  3. They enter details — New: first/last name (required) + optional phone/email; Existing: pick a searched client; Waitlist: pick an entry — set Expected Length of Stay (capped at policies.max_stay_nights), and for family rooms add family members up to capacity.
  4. Submit:
  • New/Existing → POST /shelters/assignments/check-in with bedUid, clientName, contact, appUserId (existing), familyMembers, expectedStayDays, notes, and createLoginCode:true for new clients.
  • From Waitlist → POST /shelters/waitlist/{uid}/promote with bedUid, expectedStayDays.
  1. The backend runs _createAssignmentInTrx in a transaction: verify max_stay_nights; load the bed and require status='available'.
  2. If no appUserId (walk-in): set createLoginCode=true, normalize phone/email, find-or-create an app_users row (isVerified=false, temp password, otpChannel).
  3. Resolve the location's serviceCenterId, find-or-create the "Shelter Services" Services row (category 15), and create an approved service_claims row + claim stats.
  4. If the user is unverified, generate a one-time login code, store it on the claim, and send it via SMS/EMAIL. Verified users get no code.
  5. Insert the shelter_assignments row (status active, expectedCheckOutDate = now + expectedStayDays||30), flip the bed to occupied, and write history check_in.
  6. On commit, emit realtime (bed=occupied) and return { assignment, loginCode }.
  7. The web modal shows the one-time login-code screen (copyable), indicates whether it was auto-sent (SMS/email) or must be shared manually, and gives the client mobile sign-in instructions.
  8. Edge cases: bed no longer available → error and staff pick another bed; expected stay exceeds max_stay_nights → capped/rejected; already-verified existing user → assignment created without a code.

9.4 Waitlist Add & Promote

  1. Add: a coordinator opens the Waitlist panel, searches for an existing app user or enters a client manually, sets bed preference, party adults/children, needs, preferred languages, priority, urgency, referral source, and notes → POST /shelters/waitlist/add (row with appUserId=null for staff walk-ins).
  2. The entry appears on the location and agency waitlists; the waitlist count updates via availability.changed.
  3. When a bed frees (check-out or new bed), a green banner signals available beds while clients wait.
  4. Promote: staff click Promote/Accept & Assign → POST /shelters/waitlist/{uid}/promote with expectedStayDays (and optional bedUid).
  5. If bedUid is omitted, the backend auto-resolves a matching available bed by bedTypePreference.
  6. Promotion checks max_stay_nights, sets the entry assigned, runs _createAssignmentInTrx (creating account/claim/login code as needed), notifies SHELTER_BED_ASSIGNED, and emits realtime.
  7. Edge cases: no matching bed → promotion fails and the entry stays waitlisted; a mobile-originated entry transitioning to a terminal state notifies the linked app user.

9.5 Request & Approve an Inter-Agency Transfer

  1. A Transfer Liaison (with shelters.manage_transfers) opens Partner Shelters and views partner availability via GET /shelters/partners (accepted partnerships; availability totals only).
  2. They open Request Bed Transfer, choose an existing client (from service claims) or a new client, set bed preference (individual/family/any) + family members, urgency, and a message (≥20 chars) → POST /shelters/transfers/request with targetServiceCenterUid, targetLocationUid?, bed prefs, urgency, message.
  3. The backend writes an outgoing row for the requesting SC and a mirrored incoming row for the target SC. The request appears in the requester's Sent list.
  4. Target-agency staff see the request in GET /shelters/transfers/incoming; a badge/banner reflects GET /shelters/transfers/pending-count.
  5. To accept, target staff open Accept Transfer, which filters available beds across their locations by requested bed type/family size, pick a bed → PATCH /shelters/transfers/{uid}/accept with assignedBedUid, notes.
  6. Accept runs _createAssignmentInTrx to auto check-in the client to the assigned bed (creating account/claim/login code as needed), sets the transfer accepted, and emits realtime.
  7. To decline, target staff → PATCH /shelters/transfers/{uid}/decline with declineReason; the transfer is marked declined.
  8. Edge cases: no suitable bed at the target → staff decline with reason; a caller without shelters.manage_transfers is blocked at the permission gate.

9.6 Log Maintenance

  1. From Bed Management, a coordinator opens Add Maintenance and enters title, category (light/toilet/door/…/other), locationDesc, priority, and notes → POST /shelters/locations/{uid}/maintenance-items.
  2. (Optional) They set an affected bed to maintenance status via PATCH /shelters/beds/{uid}, which fires bed.status_changed and updates availability, plus a POST /shelters/history/log entry.
  3. Staff review open items via GET /shelters/locations/{uid}/maintenance-items and delete resolved items via DELETE /shelters/locations/{uid}/maintenance-items/{itemUid}.
  4. To produce a work list, staff export the maintenance report GET /shelters/locations/{uid}/beds/maintenance-pdf (beds in maintenance + open items).
  5. When repairs finish, staff return the bed to available, restoring capacity and emitting realtime.

9.7 Mobile Bed Request → Staff Triage

  1. In the app, a victim opens a shelter, optionally uses the room/bed picker, and submits a bed request → POST /mobile/shelter-requests with party size, urgency, bed-type preference, needs, contact, notes, bed_preference, and max_stay_nights.
  2. The backend dedupes an active request, normalizes bed_preference, derives bed type, sets status waiting (beds free) or waitlisted, inserts a shelter_waitlist row with appUserId, writes history, and notifies web staff (SHELTER_REQUEST, socket shelter-request to service-center-<id>). The response includes waitlist position + check-in window.
  3. Web staff see the request in the Mobile Requests queue (GET /shelters/mobile-requests); the queue and badges live-refresh on the shelter-request event and a 30s poll.
  4. Staff Accept & Assign → PATCH /shelters/mobile-requests/{uid}/accept: if the victim requested specific beds, staff choose which (bedUids), else the system auto-assigns; set expected stay (≤ max_stay_nights) + notes. The backend runs one _createAssignmentInTrx per bed and notifies SHELTER_BED_ASSIGNED.
  5. Alternatively staff Send to Waitlist → PATCH .../waitlist (SHELTER_WAITLISTED), Reject → PATCH .../reject (SHELTER_REQUEST_REJECTED), or Cancel → PATCH .../cancel.
  6. The victim's app reflects the outcome live via /me request.status_changed and receives an FCM + in-app notification; GET /mobile/me/shelter-requests/{uid} shows assigned beds, days remaining, and history.
  7. Edge cases: accepting a pending request with no available bed returns 409 → the UI offers to waitlist instead; the victim cancelling before assignment → POST /mobile/me/shelter-requests/{uid}/cancel (blocked once assigned).

9.8 Victim Searches for a Shelter

  1. The victim opens the Shelter tab; the app requests location permission and records/loads recent searches (GET /mobile/me/shelter-searches/recent).
  2. They enter a query or zip and set filters (type, 24/7, accessible, pet-friendly, families, min open beds) → GET /mobile/shelters with q/zipcode/filters/sort/lat/lng/radius_km.
  3. The backend filters shelter_locations, computes per-shelter bed counts and Haversine distance, sorts, and paginates; for confidentialLocation shelters it hides coordinates/address ("Address shared after intake interview").
  4. The search term is recorded via POST /mobile/me/shelter-searches (upsert per user+query).
  5. The victim opens a shelter → GET /mobile/shelters/{uid} (floors→rooms→beds, availability, my_assignment, bed_req_status) and GET .../layout (ETag/304). ShelterSocketService subscribes for live bed.status_changed/availability.changed/layout.changed.
  6. For a non-confidential shelter, they open directions → GET /mobile/shelters/{uid}/directions (coordinates + polyline). For a confidential shelter, directions return 403 and the UI explains the address is shared after intake.
  7. From detail, the victim can submit a bed request (§9.7) or join the waitlist (POST /mobile/shelter/:sid/join-waitlist).
  8. Edge cases: no results → empty state with filter hints; offline → connectivity fallback; a shelter marked confidential never exposes exact location pre-intake.

13. Non-Functional Requirements

13.1 Victim Safety & Confidentiality of Shelter Locations

  • Confidential locations: shelters flagged confidentialLocation shall never expose exact address or coordinates on the mobile surface pre-intake; address renders as "Address shared after intake interview" and GET /mobile/shelters/:uid/directions returns 403.
  • Contact visibility: a shelter's phone/email appear on the mobile surface only when showPhone/showEmail are enabled.
  • Partner exposure: partner agencies see availability totals only, never bed-level detail or client identity.
  • Login codes: one-time codes are single-use, delivered over the victim's chosen channel (SMS/email); verified users never receive a code (they must use regular login).
  • Data minimization: mobile responses expose only the fields a victim needs; client identity and internal notes are never sent to other victims or to partner-availability views.
  • App-side safety: the victim app enforces a NECX PIN + biometric unlock, session lock (423)/lockout (403), an emergency-exit button, and encrypted API responses; shelter surfaces inherit these protections.

13.2 Security & Access Control

  • All web routes require a valid VSMS session (allowOnly('VS360')) and the relevant shelters.* permission; agency data is strictly tenant-scoped to issuer.serviceCenterInfo.id.
  • Mobile routes require app-user auth and the mobile-app secret header; /me/* endpoints are scoped to the authenticated user.
  • Socket connections on /shelters and /me are authenticated (socketAuthentication); users only join their own user:<id> room and explicitly subscribed shelter rooms.

13.3 Performance & Availability

  • Availability recomputation and realtime emission shall complete within < 2s p95 of the triggering change.
  • Shelter listing/detail/layout shall paginate and support ETag/304 caching for layout to minimize mobile data usage.
  • The shelter API uses a dedicated rate-limit bucket (rateLimiters.shelter) to isolate load from other modules.
  • Assignment, promotion, transfer-accept, and check-out run in DB transactions to keep bed status, assignments, and history consistent under concurrency.

13.4 Auditability & Compliance

  • Every occupancy event (check-in/check-out/transfer/incident/note) writes a shelter_history row with actor (performedBy/assignedBy) and metadata; create/update/delete operations additionally write ActionLogs.
  • History and incidents are retained via soft-deletion of locations/floors/rooms/beds so the audit trail is preserved.
  • Occupancy and utilization data support grant/PMT reporting handled by the Reports module.

13.5 Localization & Accessibility

  • Shelter directory fields include languages and services; mobile discovery supports filtering for accessible and affirming shelters (families, pets, LGBTQ+).
  • The mobile app supports en/es/bn/fr/ar with dynamic translations; shelter-facing strings participate in the translation system.

14. Analytics, Reporting & Notifications

14.1 Dashboards & KPIs

  • Agency-level KPI cards: Locations, Total Beds, Occupancy Rate, Waitlist total (GET /shelters/stats).
  • Per-location: bed counts by status, occupancy progress bar, waitlist count, incidents (GET /shelters/locations/:uid/stats, .../history, .../incidents).
  • Bed Board grouped by status (Available/Occupied/Reserved/Maintenance/Out of Service); overdue-stay indicators compare day counter vs expected stay.
  • Maintenance report (.../beds/maintenance-pdf) for facility work lists.
  • The agency dashboard's shelter_overview widget summarizes shelter status alongside other modules.

14.2 Notifications

  • In-app / socket (real-time): availability.changed, bed.status_changed, layout.changed (/shelters); request.status_changed, shelter.directory_changed (/me); shelter-request (staff, service-center-<id>).
  • FCM + in-app to victims: SHELTER_BED_ASSIGNED, SHELTER_REQUEST_ACCEPTED, SHELTER_BED_TRANSFERRED, SHELTER_WAITLISTED, SHELTER_REQUEST_REJECTED, SHELTER_CHECKED_OUT.
  • To staff: SHELTER_REQUEST, SHELTER_WAITLIST_JOIN, SHELTER_REQUEST_CANCELLED on mobile-originated activity.
  • SMS/email: one-time login-code delivery to walk-in clients via the login-code service.
  • All notification calls are wrapped defensively (failures logged, never breaking the underlying transaction).
