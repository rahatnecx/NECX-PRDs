2. Executive Summary

Staff Time Tracking (branded internally as the Staff Timer) is the NECX capability that lets agency staff record the hours they spend on their work and attribute those hours to a specific project, case, or grant. It exists so that agencies can demonstrate how their people's time is deployed — a foundational requirement for grant compliance (particularly PMT-funded grants) and for general program and project accounting.

NECX serves victims and survivors of crime and the agencies, advocates and caseworkers who assist them. Much of that assistance is funded by grants that require the funded agency to report the effort expended against each award. The Staff Timer gives advocates and caseworkers two low-friction ways to capture that effort: a live start/stop timer that lives in the application header and follows them through their workday, and a manual timesheet where they can add or correct entries after the fact. Every entry is attributed to a project — which may be a case/claim or any other selectable project — and, when the work is grant-funded, to a specific PMT grant.

The module is deliberately simple. It enforces exactly one active timer per user, computes duration automatically on clock-out, scopes all data to the staff member and their agency (service center), and exposes completed entries for date-ranged reporting and export. It has no approval workflow, no payroll disbursement, and no cross-agency visibility on the analyzed branch; it is a time-capture and reporting instrument, not a payroll engine.

This document specifies the Staff Timer as it exists in the unified NECX backend (staff-time module) and the VS360 web client (Staff Timer widget and Time Tracking page), and it flags — in Scope and Risks — the capabilities that are commonly expected of time-tracking systems but are not present on the analyzed branch.

3. Product Overview & Vision

3.1 Problem Statement

Agencies funded by grants must account for how staff time is spent, both to satisfy funder reporting (e.g., PMT grant reports) and to manage their own programs. Without an integrated tool, staff resort to spreadsheets or memory, producing inaccurate, hard-to-aggregate records that are difficult to defend in a grant audit. Advocates also work across many cases in a single day, so any tool that demands heavy data entry will not be used consistently. The problem is therefore twofold: capture must be effortless in the moment, and the resulting data must be clean enough to roll up for grant and project reporting.

3.2 Product Vision

Staff Time Tracking makes recording effort a background activity: a single click starts a timer attributed to whatever the staff member is working on, the timer persists across page loads and follows them in the app header, and a single click stops it and records the duration. For work that was not timed live, a manual timesheet lets staff add or correct entries. The captured hours become a defensible, agency-scoped record that can be filtered by date range, summarized (today / this week / this month), exported to CSV/XLS, and attributed to PMT grants for grant reporting.

3.3 Position in the NECX Ecosystem

NECX is a single unified backend serving four clients: the Victim mobile app, the Workspace mobile app, the VS360 web staff application, and NECX Admin. Staff Time Tracking is a VS360 web capability. Its backend module (staff-time) exposes a web router guarded by allowOnly('VS360'), so only agency staff (VSMS identities operating in the VS360 context) interact with it. It relates outward to two other parts of the ecosystem:

  • Cases/Claims — a time entry's "project" can be a service claim (case), letting an agency see effort by case.
  • PMT Grants & Reporting — when a project is grant-funded, the entry carries a grantId linking it to a pmt_grants record, feeding grant/PMT reporting.

There is no direct payroll integration on the analyzed branch (see Scope and Risks).

4. Goals, Objectives & Success Metrics

4.1 Goals & Objectives

  • Provide effortless, in-the-moment time capture via a persistent header timer with one active timer per user.
  • Allow after-the-fact capture and correction through a manual timesheet with edit and delete.
  • Attribute every hour to a project, and — where applicable — to a case/claim or a specific PMT grant.
  • Produce clean, agency-scoped, date-rangeable time data suitable for grant/PMT and general project reporting.
  • Enforce strict tenancy and per-user data ownership so staff see only their own agency's, and their own, time.
  • Offer at-a-glance summaries (today / this week / this month) and export (CSV/XLS) for offline reporting.

5. Scope

5.1 In Scope

  • Live start/stop timer in the VS360 header: start against a selected project, running badge (HH:MM:SS), restore on mount, stop with optional notes.
  • One active timer per user: starting a new timer auto-completes any existing active timer for that user.
  • Manual timesheet entries: add a completed entry for a chosen date with hours/minutes and notes.
  • Entry management: edit and delete existing entries.
  • Project attribution: projectType, projectId, projectName; when projectType='PMT_GRANT', the entry records a grantId → pmt_grants.
  • Reporting surface: list completed entries filtered by date range; client-side summary cards (today / this week / this month); CSV/XLS export.
  • Automatic duration: computed on clock-out as max(round(now − startTime in minutes), 1).
  • Tenancy & ownership: all entries scoped to the acting user and their service center (agency).
  • RBAC: gated by allowOnly('VS360') context and the time.view fine-grained permission.

5.2 Out of Scope

The following are either explicitly not built on the analyzed branch or belong to adjacent modules. They are called out so the reader does not assume them.

  • Payroll disbursement / pay calculation. The module records duration only; it does not compute pay, apply pay rates, or integrate with any payroll system. Feeding payroll is a potential downstream use of the exported data, not a built feature.
  • Approval / timesheet sign-off workflow. There is no supervisor review, submission, lock, or approval state — entries are ACTIVE or COMPLETED only, and staff can edit/delete their own completed entries freely.
  • Overtime, breaks, leave, holidays, or scheduling rules. No shift definitions, break tracking, or rounding policies beyond the minimum-one-minute floor.
  • Cross-agency or manager roll-up views. Reporting is scoped to the acting user + their service center; there is no built-in supervisor view of other staff members' timesheets on the analyzed branch.
  • Automatic association of a timer to task/case status changes. Association to a case is via selecting it as the "project"; the timer does not auto-attach based on which case screen the user is viewing.
  • Real-time collaboration events. The module is standard REST with client-side query invalidation; there are no socket events for time entries.
  • Idle detection / auto-stop on inactivity. A running timer continues until explicitly stopped or superseded by a new timer.
⚠ Analyzed-branch caveat
Several time-tracking capabilities that stakeholders may expect — payroll integration, approval workflows, and manager roll-up dashboards — are absent on the analyzed branch. Treat them as future work; see Section 15 (Risks & Open Questions).

7. System Context & Architecture

7.1 Technology Stack

The Staff Timer is part of the unified necx-backend_v2 API: Node.js + Express (ESM), Objection/Knex over PostgreSQL, with the standard cross-cutting middleware chain. The consuming client is the VS360 web staff application (React + TanStack Query).

  • Backend module: src/apps/staff-time/staff-time.{routes,controller,services}.js; model StaffTimeEntries over table staff_time_entries.
  • Frontend: page src/pages/StaffTime/StaffTimeManagement.jsx (the Time Tracking route); global header widget src/components/global/StaffTimer.jsx; project options hook src/hooks/useProjectOptions.js; API client src/services/staffTime.js (base /staff-time).

7.2 How It Talks to the Backend

The frontend calls the web router mounted at /web/staff-time (versioned v2), guarded by allowOnly('VS360'). Requests traverse the typical middleware chain: rate-limiter → auth (populates req.issuer) → context-middleware → allowOnly('VS360') → permission-middleware (time.view) → validator (Joi) → controller → service → model. Responses may be AES-encrypted by the encryption middleware and decrypted client-side, consistent with the rest of the platform.

The acting identity is a VSMS user; req.issuer carries the user id/uid, roleInfo (context + permission map) and serviceCenterInfo (the agency). The service uses these to stamp userId and serviceCenterId on every entry and to scope all reads.

7.3 Real-Time & Notifications

The module is standard REST with no socket events. The header widget keeps the running timer visually live by ticking every second on the client, seeded from the server-provided startTime. After mutations, the client invalidates the relevant TanStack Query caches (active timer, timesheet list) to refresh. There are no in-app, socket, FCM, or email notifications tied to time entries on the analyzed branch.

7.4 Tenancy

Multi-tenancy is enforced at the service layer: entries are always filtered and stamped by issuer.serviceCenterInfo.id (agency) and by the acting userId. A VS360 user only ever sees and edits their own time within their own agency. There is no cross-tenant or cross-user access path in the module.

8. Functional Requirements

8.1 Live Timer (Clock In / Clock Out)

The header widget (StaffTimer.jsx) is the primary capture path. On mount it calls GET /staff-time/active to restore any running timer and, if one exists, ticks a running badge (HH:MM:SS) computed from startTime. From an idle state the user opens a "Start Timer" popover, selects a project (via useProjectOptions), and starts the timer. Stopping opens a modal showing the project and elapsed duration, accepts optional notes, and completes the entry.

Server behavior: start (startTimer) first patches any existing ACTIVE entry for the user to COMPLETED, then inserts a new ACTIVE row with startTime = now; grantId is populated when projectType='PMT_GRANT'. Stop (stopTimer) finds the user's active row by uid, computes durationMinutes = max(round(now − startTime in minutes), 1), sets endTime, saves notes, and marks status COMPLETED.

9. Detailed User & System Flows

9.1 Clock In (Start a Live Timer)

  1. Staff member opens VS360; the Staff Timer widget mounts in the header.
  2. The widget calls GET /staff-time/active; the server returns null (no running timer).
  3. The badge shows an idle state with a "Start Timer" control.
  4. The user opens the "Start Timer" popover and selects a project via useProjectOptions (a case/claim, a PMT grant, or another project type).
  5. The client calls POST /staff-time/start with projectType, projectId, projectName.
  6. The server checks for any existing ACTIVE entry for this user; finding none, it inserts a new ACTIVE row with startTime = now, stamping userId and serviceCenterId. If projectType='PMT_GRANT', it sets grantId.
  7. The client begins ticking the running badge (HH:MM:SS) from startTime.

9.2 Switch / Associate Work to Another Case (Superseding Timer)

  1. While a timer is running against Case A, the staff member begins work on Case B.
  2. The user opens the "Start Timer" popover and selects Case B (or another project).
  3. The client calls POST /staff-time/start for the new project.
  4. The server finds the existing ACTIVE entry (Case A), patches it to COMPLETED (computing its duration), then inserts a new ACTIVE entry for Case B with startTime = now.
  5. The badge continues, now attributed to Case B; the Case A entry appears in the timesheet as completed.
  6. Edge case — associating grant work: if Case B's project is a PMT grant, the new entry records grantId, so its hours feed PMT reporting.

9.3 Clock Out (Stop the Timer)

  1. The staff member clicks Stop on the running badge.
  2. A modal displays the current project and the elapsed duration; the user optionally enters notes.
  3. The client calls PATCH /staff-time/stop/:uid with the running entry's uid and notes.
  4. The server finds the user's active row by uid, computes durationMinutes = max(round(now − startTime), 1), sets endTime, saves notes, and marks status COMPLETED.
  5. The client invalidates the active-timer and timesheet queries; the badge returns to idle and the completed entry appears in the timesheet.
  6. Edge case — very short sessions: any timer stopped in under a minute is recorded as 1 minute.

9.4 Add a Manual Timesheet Entry

  1. The staff member opens the Time Tracking page and clicks Manual Entry.
  2. In the modal they select a project, pick a date, enter hours + minutes, and add notes.
  3. The client converts hours + minutes to durationMinutes and calls POST /staff-time/manual with projectType, projectId, projectName, date, durationMinutes, notes.
  4. The server builds startTime = 09:00 on the given date and endTime = startTime + durationMinutes, stores the entry as COMPLETED, stamped with userId and serviceCenterId (and grantId if a PMT grant).
  5. The client invalidates the timesheet query; the new entry appears in the list and updates the summary cards.

9.5 Edit an Existing Entry

  1. From the timesheet table, the staff member clicks Edit on a row.
  2. The Edit modal preloads the entry's project, date, hours/minutes and notes.
  3. The user changes one or more fields and saves.
  4. The client calls PATCH /staff-time/:uid with the updated fields.
  5. The server updates the entry (validated and scoped to the acting user + agency) and returns the result.
  6. The client invalidates the timesheet query and refreshes the table and summaries.
  7. Edge case — delete: from the same row the user may click Delete, which calls DELETE /staff-time/:uid; the entry is removed and the list refreshed.

9.6 View & Export a Timesheet

  1. The staff member opens the Time Tracking page.
  2. The page loads GET /staff-time?startDate&endDate for the current range and renders the paginated table (Date, Project, Duration, Notes, Actions).
  3. Summary cards for Today / This Week / This Month are computed client-side from the returned entries.
  4. The user adjusts the date-range filter; the client re-queries and re-renders.
  5. The user clicks Export; the client generates a CSV/XLS file (ExportCsvXls) from the current entries for offline reporting or grant reconciliation.

9.7 Restore a Running Timer After Reload / Re-login

  1. The staff member reloads the page or logs back in.
  2. The Staff Timer widget mounts and calls GET /staff-time/active.
  3. Because the running timer's state lives server-side (an ACTIVE row), the server returns it.
  4. The client resumes the running badge from startTime with no loss of elapsed time.

13. Non-Functional Requirements

13.1 Security & Privacy

  • All routes require an authenticated VSMS identity, the VS360 context, and time.view. Responses may be AES-encrypted by the platform encryption middleware.
  • Time data is agency- and user-scoped; there is no path for a user to read another user's or another agency's entries.
  • Notes fields may reference case work; because attribution can point at a case/claim, entries should be treated as sensitive and handled with the same care as other case-adjacent data, honoring NECX's victim-safety and dignity commitments (no victim PII should be entered into free-text notes).

13.2 Performance

  • The header widget must restore the active timer quickly on mount (GET /active) and tick smoothly (1 Hz) without server round-trips.
  • Timesheet reads must be date-range bounded and paginated to keep payloads small; summaries are computed client-side.

13.3 Availability & Resilience

  • Running-timer state is authoritative server-side, so timers survive client reloads, navigation and re-login with no loss of elapsed time.
  • The one-active-timer invariant is enforced on start (prior active row auto-completed), preventing orphaned concurrent timers.

13.4 Data Integrity

  • Duration is computed server-side on stop with a 1-minute floor; manual entries derive endTime from startTime + durationMinutes, keeping start/end/duration consistent.
  • Every entry is stamped with userId and serviceCenterId at write time.

13.5 Auditability

  • Entries carry created_at/updated_at timestamps. Platform-level ActionLogs provide the general audit trail. Note that because staff may freely edit/delete their own completed entries, the module itself does not retain an immutable per-edit history (see Risks) — an important consideration for grant-audit defensibility.

13.6 Localization & Accessibility

  • The VS360 web client follows platform localization and accessibility standards; durations render as HH:MM:SS (live) and hours/minutes (manual), and dates respect the user's locale.

13.7 Compliance (Grant / PMT)

  • Grant-funded hours must be attributable to a specific PMT grant via grantId to support PMT grant reporting. Date-range export supports reconciliation against funder reporting periods.

14. Analytics, Reporting & Notifications

14.1 Dashboards & KPIs

  • Client-side summaries: the Time Tracking page renders Today / This Week / This Month totals computed from the returned entries.
  • Timesheet table: Date, Project, Duration, Notes with date-range filtering and pagination.
  • Grant/project attribution: because entries carry project fields and grantId, hours can be rolled up by project and by PMT grant to support PMT grant reporting and general project accounting.

14.2 Reporting & Export

  • GET /staff-time?startDate&endDate returns the completed-entry corpus for a range (user + agency scoped).
  • CSV/XLS export (ExportCsvXls) enables offline reporting, grant reconciliation, and any manual downstream payroll processing.

14.3 Notifications

  • None. The module has no in-app, socket, FCM, or email notifications; the live badge is kept current purely client-side, and lists refresh via TanStack Query invalidation after mutations.
