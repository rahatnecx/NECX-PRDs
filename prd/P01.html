2. Executive Summary

NECX is a coordinated victim-services platform operated by NECX Inc. It exists to help victims and survivors of crime find, request, and receive services from the agencies and advocates who serve them — safely, privately, and with dignity. The platform connects three constituencies on one shared backbone: victims/survivors, the agencies (service centers) that deliver services, and NECX Inc as the platform operator that provisions and governs the network.

The platform is delivered as a single unified backend serving four purpose-built clients. Victims use the NECX Victim App (Flutter/GetX mobile) to discover services, request help, chat with agencies and an AI assistant, find emergency shelter, complete intake forms, and e-sign documents — all behind a privacy-first PIN/biometric lock with an emergency exit. Agency staff use VS360 (React web dashboard) as their primary caseworker workspace and the NECX Workspace App (Flutter/Riverpod mobile) as its on-the-go companion. NECX Inc super-admins use the NECX Admin console (React web) to onboard agencies, define roles and subscription plans, configure platform-wide localization and AI/telephony providers, and manage shared Twilio hotline infrastructure.

The system is multi-tenant: each agency ("service center") is an isolated tenant. Agency-scoped data is filtered by the caller's service center on virtually every query, and cross-tenant access surfaces as "not found" to avoid existence leaks. Identity separates platform/staff users (users) from victims (app_users), and a fine-grained RBAC layer (context gate + module/action permission map, capped by subscription plan) governs every privileged action. The heart of the domain is the service claim (case) — a victim's request for a specific service — around which the platform coordinates intake questions, caseworker notes, victim-facing updates, chat, referrals between agencies, PMT/grant reporting, documents, and e-signature.

This document specifies the system as a whole: shared architecture, identity and access control, the end-to-end case/service-delivery lifecycle, cross-cutting flows (agency provisioning, victim registration and service request, advocate-created cases with login-code claiming, and notification delivery), and a high-level summary of every major subsystem. Each subsystem also has a dedicated feature PRD; this Core PRD is the authoritative map of how they fit together.

3. Product Overview & Vision

3.1 Problem Statement

Victims of crime navigate a fragmented landscape of agencies, hotlines, shelters, and grant-funded programs, often at their most vulnerable. Agencies, in turn, operate siloed systems that make it hard to coordinate care, refer clients safely across organizations, and produce the demographic and performance reporting that funders (e.g., VOCA/OAG Performance Measurement Tool grants) require. Victims frequently cannot see the status of their own requests; advocates cannot easily hand a case to a partner agency with the victim's consent; and platform operators lack a single control plane to onboard and govern the network.

3.2 Product Vision

NECX unifies the victim, the advocate, and the platform operator on one coordinated system. A victim can discover and request a service in minutes, track it to resolution, and communicate with a real advocate in real time — while remaining anonymous until they choose otherwise. An agency can run its entire caseload, hotline, shelter capacity, communities, and grant reporting from one workspace, and can refer a client to a partner agency with the victim's explicit, per-item consent. NECX Inc can stand up a new agency — with its staff seats, roles, mobile-facing services, and a dedicated hotline number — from a single record, cascading configuration down to every client automatically. Safety and privacy are first-class: victim-safe defaults, encrypted transport, app-level PIN/biometric locks, an emergency exit, and a full audit trail.

3.3 Where This Sits in the NECX Ecosystem

The platform is one unified backend and four clients:

  • NECX Victim App — Flutter (GetX) mobile client for victims/survivors. Consumes /v2/mobile/* routes (issuer type APP).
  • NECX Workspace App — Flutter (Riverpod) mobile client for agency staff, a companion to VS360. Consumes /v2/web/* (issuer type VSMS).
  • VS360 Web Dashboard — React (Vite) web client, the primary agency-staff workspace. Consumes /v2/web/* (issuer type VSMS).
  • NECX Admin — React (Vite) web console for NECX Inc super-admins, the platform control plane. Consumes /v2/admin/* (issuer type NECX).
  • Unified Backend — Node.js/Express (ESM), Objection/Knex over PostgreSQL, Socket.IO, Redis, BullMQ, Azure Blob Storage, Twilio, FCM, and AI providers (OpenAI/Anthropic/ElevenLabs).
ℹ Naming
In code and routes an "Agency" is a "Service Center" (service_centers, /service-centers). The two terms are used interchangeably throughout this document; the client UIs generally say "Agency."

4. Goals, Objectives & Success Metrics

4.1 Goals & Objectives

  • Provide victims a safe, private, low-friction path to discover and request victim services and track them to resolution.
  • Give agency staff a single, coordinated workspace (web + mobile) for the full case lifecycle, hotline, shelter, communities, and grant reporting.
  • Enable a NECX Inc operator to onboard and govern a multi-tenant network of agencies from one control plane, cascading roles, plans, services, and hotline infrastructure to every client.
  • Enforce strict tenant isolation, fine-grained RBAC, and a complete audit trail across every privileged action.
  • Support cross-agency coordination (partnerships and consent-gated referrals) without compromising victim consent or privacy.
  • Produce accurate, funder-grade PMT/grant reporting, augmented (but never fabricated) by AI assistance.
  • Keep victim safety and privacy paramount: victim-safe defaults, PIN/biometric app locks, emergency exit, confidential shelters, and encrypted transport.

5. Scope

5.1 In Scope

  • The unified backend API (tri-router admin/web/mobile pattern under /v2) and its cross-cutting middleware, real-time, jobs, and storage.
  • All four clients at a system level: Victim App, Workspace App, VS360 web, NECX Admin.
  • Multi-tenant agency model, identity (users vs app_users, vsms_users membership), authentication (OTP, refresh, login-code, PIN/step-up), and RBAC (context gate + permission map + plan caps).
  • The case/service-delivery lifecycle end to end: services & categories, service claims (cases), intake questions/answers, notes, updates, files, ratings, PMT snapshots, and statistics.
  • Every major subsystem at a high level (see Section 8): Case Management, Services, Referrals/Partnerships, Inquiries, Shelters, Hotline, Chat, Notes Center, Staff Time, Calendar/Reminders/Appointments, Communities, Reports/AI, NECX Drive, E-Sign/Document Center, and Admin provisioning.
  • Cross-cutting flows (Section 9): agency onboarding/provisioning cascade, victim registration & service request, advocate-created case + login-code claiming, and notification delivery.
  • Notification delivery across DB + Socket.IO + FCM + email/SMS.

5.2 Out of Scope

  • Detailed screen-by-screen UX and per-field specifications of each client and feature (covered by the client and feature PRDs).
  • Non-victim-services verticals present as legacy code (e.g., sales-representative and delivery-man endpoints in Admin) — not part of the victim-services product.
  • The community feature on the Victim App (implemented but currently hidden/commented out) is treated as planned-but-disabled.
  • Features flagged as planned/absent in the knowledgebase (see Risks & Open Questions): Workspace-app shelter, staff timer, FCM push, and PIN/biometric on the Workspace app; the "Case Management AI agent"; NECX Drive on mobile; the Victim App's older simulated e-sign path.
  • Hard storage quota enforcement for NECX Drive (a usage counter is tracked but not enforced).
  • Infrastructure provisioning, billing/collections, and Twilio commercial contracting beyond the in-product cost analytics.

6.2 Persona Narratives

Maria, a survivor downloads the NECX Victim App after a crisis. She registers with demographic/eligibility details, sets a PIN, and enables Face ID. She browses services filtered to her city, opens a domestic-violence advocacy service, answers its pre-assessment questions, and submits a claim. She chats with the assigned advocate in real time, finds an emergency shelter on the map, and requests a bed. Later she e-signs an intake document from within the app. The emergency-exit button is always one tap away.

David, an advocate at a partner agency, works cases all day in VS360 and checks in from the Workspace app between site visits. He creates a case on a walk-in victim's behalf (auto-approved), which mints a login code the victim can later use to claim their account. He adds internal notes (invisible to the victim), publishes victim-facing updates, refers the client to a partner agency after obtaining consent, and takes hotline calls through the browser softphone with live AI transcription.

Priya, a NECX Inc super-admin onboards a new agency: she defines the VS360 roles, bundles them into a subscription plan, creates the agency with a seat count and a plan, provisions the agency's staff logins (roles constrained by the plan), attaches services and pre-assessment questionnaires that surface in the mobile app, and provisions a dedicated Twilio hotline number with SHAKEN/STIR caller trust — all from the Admin console.

7. System Context & Architecture

7.1 Tech Stack

  • Backend (necx-backend_v2): Node.js + Express (ESM), Objection.js/Knex over PostgreSQL, Socket.IO, Redis (scaling/pub-sub/cache and hotline staff availability), BullMQ (email and background jobs), Azure Blob Storage (MinIO/Azurite in dev), Twilio (hotline + OTP-SMS), FCM (push), and AI providers OpenAI/Anthropic/ElevenLabs. Single unified API under version prefix /v2.
  • VS360 web (vs360-webapp): React 18 + Vite, Mantine 7, Redux Toolkit + redux-persist, TanStack React Query, Axios, socket.io-client, Twilio Voice SDK, PostHog.
  • NECX Admin (necx-admin): React 18 + Vite, Mantine 7, Redux Toolkit + redux-persist, React Query, Axios.
  • Workspace App (necx-workspace-mobile-app): Flutter, Riverpod, GoRouter, Dio, socket.io-client, flutter_secure_storage.
  • Victim App (necx-app_v2): Flutter, GetX, Dio, socket.io-client, Firebase (Crashlytics + FCM), local_auth (biometric), google_maps_flutter, secure storage, PostHog.

7.2 Request Model & Middleware

The backend uses a tri-router pattern: nearly every feature module exports adminRouter (/admin/*), webRouter (/web/*), and mobileRouter (/mobile/*), mounted by a loader under /v2. The URL prefix declares the intended client; allowOnly(...contexts) enforces it. A typical middleware chain is: rate-limiter → auth (populates req.issuer) → context-middleware → allowOnly(context) → permission-middleware (fine-grained) → Joi validator → controller → service → repository/model. Cross-cutting middleware includes encryption-middleware (AES response encryption that clients decrypt), file-middleware (multer uploads), twilio-signature.middleware (validates Twilio webhooks), and global-error-middleware.

7.3 Talking to the Backend

All clients send Authorization: Bearer <access> plus x-refresh-token, and gate at the edge with an X-Netlify-Secret shared secret (nginx/edge proxy) and X-Environment. Sliding token refresh occurs by rotating the x-refresh-token response header (web clients) or via POST /auth/refresh (mobile). In non-development environments, response bodies are AES-encrypted and decrypted client-side (crypto-js on web, encrypt on mobile). Web clients target /v2/web (VS360) or /v2/admin (Admin); mobile targets /v2/mobile (Victim) or /v2/web (Workspace, with a /v2/mobile variant for the richer user profile).

7.4 Real-Time & Notifications

Socket.IO (src/utils/socket.js) provides per-user rooms (vs360-user-<uid>), a victim room (app-notification-room), agency rooms (service-center-<id>), per-thread rooms (thread-<threadUid>-<userUid>), and dedicated shelter namespaces (/shelters, /me). Redis backs scaling and pub/sub. Notification delivery is multi-channel: a DB row (service_center_notifications for staff / victim_notifications for victims) + a Socket.IO emit + an FCM push + an optional templated email (BullMQ). All notification calls are wrapped defensively so a delivery failure never breaks the main transaction.

7.5 Tenancy

Two identity tables — users (platform/staff/admin) and app_users (victims) — plus vsms_users, which joins a users row to a service_centers (agency) row to represent staff membership. The authenticated principal req.issuer carries id, uid, type (NECX/VSMS/APP), roleInfo (with context + permission), and, for staff, serviceCenterInfo. Agency-scoped data is filtered by issuer.serviceCenterInfo.id; a VS360 user only ever sees/edits their own agency, and cross-tenant rows surface as "not found."

7.6 Async Jobs & Storage

BullMQ queues handle templated email delivery (Handlebars + provider) and other background jobs. File storage uses Azure Blob in staging/prod (MinIO/Azurite in dev). NECX Drive uses a dedicated Azure adapter with direct-to-blob SAS upload/download; E-Sign and case/claim files proxy bytes through a shared fileService.

8. Functional Requirements

This section summarizes each subsystem at the system level, with requirement IDs and pointers to the dedicated feature PRDs. Endpoint, socket, and status detail is drawn from the backend and client knowledgebases.

8.1 Identity, Authentication & Session

Authentication is a two-step OTP flow shared by all clients (admin/web/mobile variants), rate-limited (auth, authStrict, passwordReset). Credentials validate against user_credentials (bcrypt); OTP is delivered over the user's channel (email or SMS/Twilio); verification issues JWT access + refresh tokens and returns the profile with roleInfo/serviceCenterInfo. Password reset is a channel-check → OTP-trigger → verify → set-new sequence. Mobile adds a login-code flow (advocate-created/anonymous case claiming), PIN lifecycle, biometric unlock, step-up re-auth, and device-token registration for FCM.

FR-AUTH-03The system shall provide password reset via check-reset-channels → reset-password-trigger → verify-reset-password → reset-password.Must

8.5 Referrals & Partnerships

Partnerships are bilateral agency relationships (request → accept/decline; accepted enables referrals and auto-creates a shared agency chat). Referrals move a case to a partner agency for a specific service, gated by explicit per-item victim consent; on consent the case is cloned (with a sanitized snapshot per canShare* flags) into the target agency, which then accepts or declines it. There is a single-active-referral-per-case rule. See Referrals/Partnerships feature PRDs.

9. Detailed User & System Flows

9.1 Agency Onboarding & Provisioning Cascade (Admin → Agency → VS360 users → Mobile services)

  1. A NECX super-admin defines RBAC roles for the VS360 context (and ADMIN context) in Admin → Users → Roles & Permissions (POST /roles), toggling per-module/action permissions (e.g., claims.view_all is only enabled when claims.view is on).
  2. The super-admin creates subscription plans (POST /plans) that bundle a set of VS360 roles; a plan effectively defines the agency's tier and which roles its users may hold.
  3. The super-admin creates an agency (POST /admin/service-centers) with name, contact, address (country/state/city), a seat count (numberOfUsers, NECX-privileged), a plan (planId), serviceType=VICTIM_SERVICE_PROVIDER, and optional Twilio switches (setupHotline, nested setupShakenStir). The server generates a unique 4-char code and uid, writes an ActionLogs row, and — if setupHotline — fires fire-and-forget hotline provisioning (Twilio failure is logged, non-blocking).
  4. If hotline provisioning is requested, setupHotlineForAgency runs step-tracked and idempotent: Twilio subaccount → API key → TwiML app (voice URL /v2/web/hotlines/twilio/voice) → search + buy a US number → create hotline_lines → optional SHAKEN/STIR TrustProduct. A simulate mode can fake SIDs for dry runs.
  5. The super-admin provisions VS360 users for the agency (from the agency detail page or the VS360 Users tab). Assignable roles are fetched by the agency's plan (GET /roles/plan/:planId); service categories and OTP channel are assigned; "Add User" is disabled once users.length >= numberOfUsers. POST /users with type=VSMS creates the login and the vsms_users membership.
  6. The super-admin attaches services + pre-assessment questionnaires to the agency (POST /victim-services), each service carrying intake questions. These become the offerings and pre-assessments that surface to victims in the mobile app (auto-isVerified when created by staff).
  7. The super-admin sends an onboarding email (POST /users/send-onboarding-email/:uid) with credentials.
  8. Result: one agency record fans out into a VS360 tenant with seats/roles, mobile-app services/questionnaires, and (optionally) a dedicated Twilio hotline — all keyed to the agency uid. Edge case: planId = "-1" maps to no plan; agencies without a plan are hidden from directory search.
  9. Bulk path: POST /admin/service-centers/batch imports agencies + services per-agency-transaction, resolving state/city and logging each row.

9.2 Victim Registration & Service Request (End to End)

  1. The victim installs the Victim App and completes the 4-step registration wizard: personal info + preferred OTP channel (EMAIL/SMS) → location (state→city cascading) → demographics/eligibility (race/ethnicity, gender, age, special classification) → set password + accept terms. Submit calls POST /mobile/users.
  2. On success the app resets registration data and auto-logs in (POST /mobile/auth/sign-in), navigating to the OTP screen.
  3. The victim enters the OTP; POST /mobile/auth/verify-profile (type=APP, includes deviceToken) returns {user, accessToken, refreshToken, hasPin, settings.isPinEnabled}. The app persists tokens/user, identifies analytics/crash reporting, initializes the socket, and routes through PIN setup/verify (mandatory on relaunch).
  4. The victim discovers services: the Services tab lists services with filters (category, state, city, debounced search, paginated) via GET /mobile/victim-services. National services bypass geo filtering.
  5. The victim opens a service (GET /mobile/victim-services/{uid}, increments viewCount, adds language when non-English), which returns the per-service claim questions (pre-assessment).
  6. The victim answers questions and submits a claim: POST /mobile/service-claims { serviceId, questionAnswers }. The server (in a transaction) resolves victimId=issuer.id, applies the duplicate guard (rejects an existing ongoing claim for the (service, victim)), creates the claim uid=CL<yy><month><uuid6> with status pending, inserts answers, creates a stats row, computes new-vs-returning client, creates the PMT report with demographics, and emits claim-request to category-assigned caseworkers.
  7. Agency staff (VS360/Workspace) see the new claim, review it, and approve it (PATCH /service-claims/approve/:uid): status → approved, a published "Approved" update is created, a SERVICE chat thread is opened, stats record approved_by/at, and the victim is notified (victim_notifications SERVICE_CLAIM_APPROVED + FCM + socket).
  8. The victim and advocate chat in real time on the SERVICE thread; the victim can view published updates, receive/complete intake forms, e-sign case documents, and (if referred) consent to referrals.
  9. On completion the advocate resolves the case (PATCH /service-claims/resolve/:uid): status → resolved, a "Resolved" update is published, the chat thread is archived, and the victim may rate the service (PATCH /service-claims/rating/:uid).
  10. Alternative branches: the victim may request to connect (PUT /mobile/victim-services/{uid}/request-to-connect, emails the center, 409 if already requested) or start an inquiry chat before claiming; staff may reject a claim (status → rejected, PMT deleted, victim notified).

9.3 Advocate-Created Case + Login-Code Claiming

  1. An advocate creates a case on a victim's behalf in VS360/Workspace: POST /service-claims with shouldCreateUser=true and userData (and/or an existing victimId).
  2. The service finds the user by email/phone or creates a temporary app_user (anonymousUserCode, isVerified=false), then creates the claim with status approved (advocate-created cases auto-approve) and records approved_by/at.
  3. If the new user has an OTP channel (otpChannel ≠ NONE), the system generates a one-time login code (loginCodeService), stores it on the claim (loginCode, codeSentAt), and delivers it to the victim via email/SMS.
  4. Separately, a shelter check-in for an unlinked walk-in can also mint a login code (find-or-create app_user → approved shelter claim → generate + send code), handed to the client by staff.
  5. The advocate may later attach contact info to an anonymous case via PATCH /service-claims/link-client/:claimUid, which validates the channel/contact and issues/reuses a login code (ADVOCATE_LINKED_CLIENT audit).
  6. The victim claims the account in the app: POST /mobile/auth/send-otp-for-code (if needed) → POST /mobile/auth/verify-otp-for-login-code (activates the account).
  7. If the account has no password yet, the victim sets one via POST /mobile/auth/set-password-first-login (with preAuthToken); otherwise POST /mobile/auth/login-with-code completes login.
  8. The now-verified victim is logged in (tokens issued), routed through PIN setup, and sees the previously anonymous case linked to their real account with full case detail, chat, updates, and documents.

9.4 Cross-Agency Referral with Victim Consent

  1. Agency A and Agency B establish a partnership (A requests via POST /web/partnerships; only B may accept). On accept, a shared AGENCY_CHAT thread is auto-created and both agencies are notified.
  2. Both agencies set acceptingReferrals=true (toggle). Agency A, owning an existing case, lists eligible targets (GET /referral-options — accepted partnerships where both accept referrals, enriched with services).
  3. Agency A creates a referral (POST /web/partnerships/referrals) selecting a partner service and setting the six canShare* sharing flags (all default false) and an optional message. Guards: valid partnership (accepted), issuer is a party, original case exists, and no active referral already exists for the case (else 409). The referral is created with consentStatus=pending, referralStatus=pending, consentExpiresAt=now+2d.
  4. The victim is prompted in the app and makes a consent decision (POST /mobile/partnerships/referrals/:uid/decision). Reject → consentStatus=denied (terminal). Approve → consentStatus=granted.
  5. On approve, the system builds a sanitized case snapshot limited to the enabled canShare* subsets (notes, files, published updates only, chat, victim answers, PMT demographics), then clones the case into the target agency (new service_claims uid, status pending, mirrored PMT + stats) and sets referredCaseId. referralStatus stays pending until the target agency acts.
  6. Agency B approves or rejects the cloned claim in its normal case workflow, which flips referralStatus to accepted/declined.
  7. The victim's app gates chat on the referred case (consent granted + agency accepted). Agency A can resend a denied referral (resend), soft-delete its own referral, and see referral stats.

9.5 Notification Delivery (Cross-Cutting)

  1. A domain event fires (claim approved, new message, referral, shelter update, document signature request, community action, etc.).
  2. The owning service inserts a DB notification row — service_center_notifications for staff or victim_notifications for victims — with additionalData (e.g., claimUid, threadUid).
  3. Socket.IO emits to the target room so open clients update live: vs360-user-<uid> / service-center-<id> for staff; app-notification-room (and event names like service-claim-action-for-<victimUid>, update-<receiverUid>, inquiry-approve-for-<victimUid>) for victims; per-thread rooms for chat; and /shelters//me namespaces for shelter events.
  4. FCM push is sent to the recipient's registered deviceToken(s) for background delivery (Victim App). The Workspace app currently has no FCM and instead polls notifications via REST.
  5. An optional templated email (BullMQ Handlebars queue) and/or SMS is sent for select events (e.g., login-code delivery, inquiry/partnership emails, document-signature request to the victim).
  6. Read-state is cleared via helpers (markClaimNotificationsAsRead, markThreadNotificationsAsRead) and per-notification/thread mark-read endpoints.
  7. All notification calls are wrapped defensively: a failure in any channel is logged and never breaks the main transaction. Edge case: a recipient with no device token still receives the DB row + socket event + (where configured) email.

9.6 Hotline Inbound Call (Summary Flow)

  1. A victim dials the agency's hotline number; Twilio hits POST /web/hotlines/twilio/voice (signature-validated). The backend resolves the active hotline_lines by the dialed number.
  2. It collects assigned staff who are Redis-available; if none, it returns TwiML indicating unavailability (recorded). Otherwise it pre-generates a callId + conference name, creates the hotline_calls row (active), and sets the first available staff to on_call.
  3. Every available staff's browser (and phone, if any) is rung into the conference simultaneously; hotline:incoming is emitted to service-center-<id> so all browsers show the incoming dialog. TwiML starts a media stream + <Dial><Conference>; the victim waits until staff joins.
  4. When a primary staff leg joins (POST /web/hotlines/twilio/conference), all other ringing legs are cancelled, answeredByUserId/callAnsweredVia are fixed, and the answering user is notified. Secondary staff can be added/transferred.
  5. Live audio streams over the media WebSocket → transcription → periodic AI insight extraction (when enabled), emitting hotline:transcript/hotline:insights to the answering user.
  6. Ending moves the call to wrap_up (browser-answered) or completed; wrap-up records disposition/category/mood/summary/services and can auto-create a callback. Dispositions tie the call to casework (created_case, created_inquiry, linked_to_existing_case, referred_to_partner, callback_scheduled, informational_only).

9.7 E-Sign a Case Document (Summary Flow)

  1. Staff upload a document to a case (POST /web/case-documents/claim/:claimUid), status draft.
  2. Staff send for signature (POST /:uid/send) with positioned fields[] (role, page, normalized x/y/w/h) or fallback requireVictim/requireStaff; prior pending signature rows are cleared; status → pending_signature; a preview PDF shows dashed placeholders.
  3. The victim is notified (socket + in-app + FCM + email) and views the document in-app; staff sign in VS360.
  4. Each signer signs (POST /:uid/sign) with a drawn/typed/saved signature (victim restricted to their own signer_app_user_id), capturing identity, type, timestamp, and IP address.
  5. The signed PDF is re-stamped from the pristine original on every signature; status transitions to partially_signed then signed (sets completed_at); parties are notified. Cancel soft-deletes the document and drops it from the victim's list.

13. Non-Functional Requirements

  • Security & transport — Bearer JWT with sliding refresh; edge gate via X-Netlify-Secret; AES-encrypted response bodies (non-dev) decrypted client-side; Twilio webhook signature validation; rate limiters (standard, auth, authStrict, passwordReset, chatApi, pmtApi, shelter, tool); Drive MIME anti-spoofing (whitelist + magic-byte sniff), SHA-256 share-link tokens, path-traversal-proof blob paths, and SAS rate limits.
  • Privacy & victim safety — victim-safe defaults; NECX PIN + biometric lock on the Victim App; emergency exit button; session lock (423) and lockout (403); confidential shelters hide coordinates/address until intake; anonymous cases and consent-gated referrals; case-detail screens verify ownership before rendering; sharing preferences default to all-false; referral snapshots share only sanitized, per-flag subsets.
  • Multi-tenancy & isolation — strict service_center_id scoping; cross-tenant existence hidden as not-found. (Open item: E-Sign read paths rely on route guard + signer check rather than an explicit tenant re-check — flagged for security review.)
  • Performance — server-side pagination and filtering; Redis caching; direct-to-Azure SAS uploads (bytes bypass the app tier for Drive); React Query caching (5-min stale/10-min cache) on web; debounced search on mobile.
  • Availability & resilience — Socket.IO auto-reconnect with room re-join; defensive notification wrapping; idempotent, resumable hotline provisioning; terminateStaleCalls on startup; fire-and-forget side effects that never block core transactions.
  • Auditability — nearly all create/update/delete operations write ActionLogs with action, reference type, and actionDescription{source, ip, url, context}; E-Sign captures per-signature IP address.
  • Localization & accessibility — platform-managed mobile supported languages and static translations; Victim App supports en/es/bn/fr/ar (RTL for Arabic) with runtime-fetched dynamic translations and message translation. VS360/Admin/Workspace are English-only today (no global i18n). Accessibility to follow WCAG 2.1 AA as clients mature.
  • Compliance — PMT/OAG grant reporting pipeline with demographic snapshots and a programmatic accuracy validator (no fabrication; file values override system data; human approval for AI edits); full audit trail for compliance review.

14. Analytics, Reporting & Notifications

14.1 Dashboards & KPIs

The Admin dashboard is currently a stub.

14.2 Reporting

The PMT/grants pipeline links grants ↔ questionnaires (by service category) ↔ per-claim answers ↔ PMT demographic rows, feeding both an Excel AI spreadsheet editor (conservative, approval-gated, revertible edits) and the NECX AI Report Builder (structured JSON, live agency data + uploaded files, server-side keys, post-generation accuracy validator with conflict/hallucination flags, PDF export). Staff-time entries feed grant/project accounting.

14.3 Notifications

Multi-channel delivery combines a DB row (service_center_notifications/victim_notifications) + Socket.IO emit + FCM push + optional email/SMS. Staff clients show unread badges (claims, inquiries, internal chat, agency messages, communities, plus live hotline/shelter counts). The Victim App receives FCM push and an in-app overlay; the Workspace app polls notifications via REST (no FCM today). Read-state is managed per notification/claim/thread.
