2. Executive Summary

VS360 is the browser-based control center used by victim-services agency staff — caseworkers, advocates, hotline operators, shelter coordinators, and agency administrators — to deliver and coordinate help for victims and survivors of crime. It is one of four clients on the unified NECX platform: the Victim mobile app (survivors), the Workspace mobile app (staff on the go), the NECX Admin console (NECX Inc. platform operators), and VS360 itself (the agency staff web dashboard). All four share a single NestJS/Express backend and Socket.IO real-time layer.

VS360 turns a fragmented set of manual workflows — intake forms, spreadsheets, phone logs, referral faxes, grant reports — into one coordinated system. Staff work victim cases end-to-end (intake, approval, chat, notes, updates, files, e-signature, referral, resolution), publish the services their agency offers, triage anonymous inquiries, run a Twilio-powered crisis hotline with live AI transcription and insights, manage physical shelter inventory down to individual beds, and produce funder-ready grant and board reports with an AI report builder that draws on live agency data. Cross-agency collaboration (partnerships, referrals, agency chat, network discovery) lets agencies hand cases to each other with the client's consent while preserving safety and data isolation.

The product's design is dominated by NECX's mission context: the people it ultimately serves are victims and survivors of crime. Cases are anonymous by default, contact linking is deliberate and consented, cross-agency data sharing is gated field-by-field, confidential shelter locations are hidden, and hotline transcripts carry PII that must never be logged. Every action is audit-logged for compliance, and reporting is built to satisfy federal/grant Performance Measurement Tool (PMT) requirements (VOCA/VAWA-aligned).

VS360 is a React 18 + Vite single-page application using Mantine v7 for UI, TanStack React Query for server state, Redux Toolkit (persisted) for client state, and Socket.IO for real-time. It authenticates with a two-step email-and-password → OTP flow, enforces a three-layer frontend RBAC model backed by per-module/per-action permissions, and is multi-tenant: a staff user only ever sees and edits their own agency's data.

3. Product Overview & Vision

3.1 Problem Statement

Victim-services agencies operate under acute constraints: limited staff, crisis-driven caseloads, strict grant-reporting obligations, and an overriding duty to protect the safety and privacy of the people they serve. Historically these agencies stitch together intake forms, spreadsheets, phone systems, shelter whiteboards, and manual referral processes. This fragmentation causes lost context between staff, slow response to victims in crisis, error-prone grant reporting, and no safe, consented way to hand a case to a partner agency that can better help.

3.2 Product Vision

VS360 is the single operational surface where an agency runs its entire victim-services practice. It should let any authorized staff member see what needs attention right now, act on it with as few steps as possible, collaborate with colleagues and partner agencies safely, and generate the compliance artifacts funders require — all without leaving the browser, and all while treating victim safety and data minimization as first-class constraints rather than afterthoughts.

3.3 Position in the NECX Ecosystem

NECX is a coordinated victim-services platform with a single unified backend serving four clients:

  • Victim App (mobile, issuer.type = APP) — survivors discover services, submit inquiries and service claims, chat with advocates, request shelter beds, consent to referrals, and claim advocate-created cases via a one-time login code.
  • Workspace App (mobile, issuer.type = VSMS) — the same agency staff identity as VS360, for on-the-go work.
  • VS360 Web (this product, issuer.type = VSMS, roleInfo.context = VS360) — the full agency staff dashboard.
  • NECX Admin (web, issuer.type = NECX, context = ADMIN) — NECX Inc. platform operators who provision agencies, plans, categories, and hotline Twilio accounts.

VS360 consumes the backend's /web/* router tree (guarded by allowOnly('VS360')) and the shared Socket.IO server. It never talks to storage or AI providers directly for privileged data — AI calls and encrypted-data access are backend-routed; only NECX Drive uploads go direct-to-Azure via short-lived SAS URLs.

4. Goals, Objectives & Success Metrics

4.1 Goals & Objectives

  • Give every staff role a role-aware home that surfaces the cases, follow-ups, calls, and shelter needs requiring attention now.
  • Support the full case lifecycle (intake → approve/reject → work → resolve) with chat, notes, updates, files, e-signature, and referral all in one place.
  • Let agencies publish and manage the services they offer, with per-service intake questionnaires that feed both casework and reporting.
  • Provide a real-time, AI-assisted crisis hotline: browser softphone, live transcription, AI insights, wrap-up, callbacks, and analytics.
  • Model and manage physical shelter inventory (locations → floors → rooms → beds) with check-in/out, waitlist, incidents, and inter-agency bed transfers.
  • Automate grant/PMT reporting: collect data via linked questionnaires, and generate funder-ready Excel and publication-quality reports with an AI builder grounded in live agency data.
  • Enable safe cross-agency coordination: partnerships, consented referrals with field-level sharing controls, agency chat, and network discovery.
  • Enforce victim safety and privacy end-to-end: anonymous-by-default cases, deliberate contact linking, confidential shelters, PII-safe logging, full audit trail, and strict tenant isolation.

7. System Context & Architecture

7.1 Technology Stack

VS360 is a React 18.3 (JSX, StrictMode) single-page app built with Vite 6 (@vitejs/plugin-react). It is plain JavaScript/JSX (no TypeScript source). Key libraries:

  • UI: Mantine 7.11 (core, dates, modals, notifications, tiptap, charts, carousel, dropzone, spotlight, form) as the primary design system, with Tailwind CSS 3.4 for layout utilities and a custom Mantine theme (Poppins font, brand primary, TailAdmin-style dark palette).
  • Server state: TanStack React Query 4.26 (global client with staleTime 5 min, cacheTime 10 min, refetchOnWindowFocus: false, retry once with backoff, no mutation retry).
  • Client state: Redux Toolkit 2.2 + react-redux + redux-persist (entire store persisted to localStorage under persist:root, version 2 with a wipe-and-redirect migration on version mismatch).
  • HTTP: Axios 1.7 via a per-request factory (src/services/axios.js).
  • Real-time: socket.io-client 4.8 (src/context/SocketContext.jsx).
  • Telephony: Twilio Voice SDK (@twilio/voice-sdk) for the hotline browser softphone.
  • Charts/maps: ApexCharts, Recharts, Mantine charts, Leaflet + react-leaflet (+ routing machine) for shelter/geo heatmaps.
  • Spreadsheets: Luckysheet (CDN), xlsx, exceljs, luckyexcel for grant report tooling.
  • Rich content: TipTap, react-markdown + remark-gfm, dompurify; PDF rendering via react-pdf/pdf.js for e-sign.
  • DnD: @dnd-kit. Crypto: crypto-js (AES response decryption). Analytics: PostHog. Testing: Vitest + Testing Library + MSW. Lint: ESLint 8 flat config (--max-warnings 0).

Build modes (Vite --mode): development (port 3000), staging, beta, production (port 4000), with env files under configs/. Deploy via Netlify (netlify.toml per context) with a Dockerfile/compose alternative.

7.2 Provider Composition & Routing

main.jsx initializes PostHog and mounts <App/> inside <PostHogProvider> within <StrictMode>. App.jsx composes providers outer→inner: ReduxWrapper (redux-persist PersistGate) → PostHog identity → QueryWrapper (React Query) → ThemeWrapper (Mantine theme, modals, notifications) → SocketProvider → RouteWrapper.

Routing is data-driven: src/constants/AppRoutes.js exports route objects { path, Element, isIndexUrl, isProtected, module, permission?, isForAll? }; RouteWrapper.jsx maps them to react-router-dom v6 <Route> elements. Protected routes render as <PrivateLayout><ProtectedPage module permission isForAll><Element/></ProtectedPage></PrivateLayout>; public routes render inside <PublicLayout>; a catch-all renders NotFoundPage. Client route paths live in src/constants/AppUrls.js; the sidebar model in src/constants/NavItems.js (plus SettingNavItems.js for the Reports/PMT sub-cards).

7.3 API / Data Layer & Security Gateway

Every request from the axios factory attaches: Content-Type: application/json, Authorization: Bearer <accessToken>, x-refresh-token: <refreshToken>, X-Netlify-Secret (shared secret the Netlify edge proxy validates before forwarding to the backend), and X-Environment. The response interceptor rotates a refreshed token from the x-refresh-token response header and decrypts the body: in non-development environments the backend returns AES-CBC-encrypted strings, decrypted via decryptData() (crypto-js, VITE_APP_AES_KEY/VITE_APP_AES_IV); development passes through unencrypted. On HTTP 401 the interceptor dispatches clearAllUserState() (full logout/state wipe); 403/404/5xx are surfaced to friendly toasts via handleErrorResponse / NotificationUtil. Each backend domain has a service module under src/services/ returning axios promises. The API base already includes the /web (and version) prefix.

7.4 Real-Time & Notifications

A single Socket.IO connection (SocketContext) opens when token and appUser exist, passing the JWT in the socket auth handshake (not headers), with transports: [websocket, polling], reconnection (8 attempts, 2–10s backoff), plus app-level reconnect resilience. Rooms (SOCKET_ROOMS): vs360-user-<uid> (personal), service-center-<id> (agency-wide: hotline, shelter, inquiries), global-notifications, and per-thread thread-<threadUid>-<userUid>. Incoming events run through socketEventCallbacks → toast + Redux refetch.* flags that pages watch to re-run queries. Hotline events are registered on demand by the Hotline page and torn down on unmount. Notification badges (claims, inquiries, internal chat, agency messages, communities, notifications, live hotline/shelter counts) are tracked in the Redux notification slice and rendered in the sidebar and header.

7.5 Tenancy & Session

Multi-tenancy is carried on the authenticated user object in the Redux auth slice (agency, serviceCenterInfo, roleInfo, vsmsUserInfo, serviceCategories); socket rooms and RBAC derive tenant scope from it. Backend /web/* routes independently enforce agency scoping via issuer.serviceCenterInfo.id, so a VS360 user only ever sees and edits their own agency's data. Tokens survive reloads through the persisted Redux store; there is no separate token-storage layer.

8. Functional Requirements

8.1 Authentication & Session

Login is a two-step flow: email + password, then OTP verification. SignInApi (POST /auth/sign-in) validates credentials and returns a privateKey, type, and passwordReset flag (not tokens); the app stores a temp blob (~179s validity) and navigates to /verify-user, or to /forgot-password when a password reset is required (including the 403 isPasswordResetRequired branch). verifyOtpApi (POST /auth/verify-profile) exchanges the 6-digit OTP for user, accessToken, refreshToken, and modules.vs360Modules, dispatches authActions.signin, and navigates to the user's computed home route. OTP has a 5-minute countdown and a 30-second resend throttle. Dev builds offer a Quick Login modal with seeded accounts.

8.5 Case Management (Claims)

In VS360 a "case" is a service claim (UI: "My Cases"; API resource service-claims). The list (GET /service-claims, page size 20) shows cases as cards with header stat chips (New, Approved, Referred) and a Create Case button. The query key includes claims.view_all so admins see all agency cases and workers see assigned. Cards are sorted by priority: unread messages first (matched against SERVICE_NEW_MESSAGE notifications), then new/pending, in-progress/approved, resolved, rejected. Filters cover client-name/Case-ID search, category (limited to the user's serviceCategories), and status. A pageless fetch drives CSV/XLS export.

Create Case is a 3-step stepper (POST /service-claims): Required Information (service limited to assigned categories, first/last name; anonymous by default with optional contact + otpChannel), Optional Demographics (age/gender/race/classification/state→city), and dynamic Service Questions (true-false / multiple-choice / fill-in-the-blank, with per-question "Bypass Requirement"). If contact matches an existing account the case links to it; a new anonymous/coded user triggers a CodeDisplayModal with the mobile login code. A prefill prop seeds the modal from hotline/inquiry data. Advocate-created cases auto-approve on the backend.

Case Detail (GET /service-claims/:uid → {claim, claimChatThread}, plus GET /reports/pmt-questionnaires/claim/:uid) shows a Client/Case info right rail (name, email, phone, Login Code with Used/Unused badge, status), status-dependent header actions (Approve/Reject, or Accept/Decline referral; when approved: Refer and Mark as Resolved), and tabs persisted in Redux claimDetailsTab:

  • Details — Client Answers (editable via EditClaimQuestionsModal when approved) and PMT Questions.
  • Chat — real-time client↔advocate chat (GET /chat-services/get-all-chats/:uid, POST /chat-services/send-message/:uid, multipart).
  • Updates — advocate progress updates with optional "notify client" (POST/PATCH/DELETE /service-claims/*-update/:uid).
  • Notes — internal notes, TipTap (POST/PATCH/DELETE /service-claims/*-note/:uid).
  • Files — case documents; upload (POST /service-claims/upload-files/:uid) + File Translator (POST /tool/translate/file).
  • Documents (E-Sign) — see 8.19.
  • Referrals — cross-agency referral UI (received-consent review, sent history, sharing snapshot); shown when referrals exist.

Status transitions: Approve (PATCH /service-claims/approve/:uid), Reject (PATCH /service-claims/reject/:uid), Resolve (SurveyModalForm to complete PMT/questionnaire → PATCH /service-claims/resolve/:uid). Edit intake answers (PATCH /service-claims/claims/:uid/questions). Update PMT/questionnaire answers (POST /reports/pmt-questionnaires/claim/:claimId/answers or legacy PATCH /reports/pmt-report/:uid). Link client contact (PATCH /service-claims/link-client/:claimUid, may return a login code). Refer to a partner via ReferAgencyModal (search accepted partners → target service → choose data to share → message → create referral with sharingPreferences). Each service request is its own case; consolidation happens by linking the client; cross-agency handoff happens by referral.

8.7 Inquiries Management

Inquiries are anonymous victim↔agency threads that precede a formal case (victims open them from the mobile app; they arrive as pending). The screen ("My Inquiries") is a two-pane master/detail (single pane on mobile) driven by GetInquiryThreads into Redux threads.inquiryThreads, filterable by status (All/Pending/Approved/Resolved/Rejected). 9. Detailed User & System Flows

9.1 Login → Landing (two-step OTP)

  1. On /, the user submits email + password; VS360 calls SignInApi (POST /auth/sign-in).
  2. On success the app stores a temp blob (identifier, privateKey, type, validTill ≈ now+179s, passwordResetRequired) via authActions.setTempData and navigates to /verify-user — or to /forgot-password if a password reset is required (including the 403 isPasswordResetRequired branch).
  3. The user enters the 6-digit OTP (5-minute countdown; 30s resend throttle via POST /auth/otp-request) and VS360 calls verifyOtpApi (POST /auth/verify-profile).
  4. On success it dispatches authActions.signin({ user, accessToken, refreshToken, modules }), clears temp data, and the persisted store now holds tokens and isAuth: true.
  5. getHomeRoute(navItems, user) computes the first nav item the user may see (fallback /dashboard) and navigates there.
  6. The auth change triggers SocketProvider to connect the socket (JWT in the handshake); PrivateLayout mounts, sets the document title, and pre-fetches chat threads and joined communities into Redux.
  7. Edge cases: wrong credentials → error toast, stay on /; expired OTP → prompt resend; expired temp window → return to login; any later HTTP 401 → clearAllUserState forces re-login.

9.2 Work a Case End-to-End

  1. From Dashboard or the Cases list, the advocate opens or creates a case. Create runs the 3-step stepper: pick a service (limited to assigned categories), enter first/last name (anonymous by default), optionally add contact + notification channel, fill optional demographics, and answer dynamic service questions (bypassing required ones if needed) → POST /service-claims.
  2. If contact matches an existing account, the case links to it ("Case Added to Existing Account"); if a new coded user is created, a CodeDisplayModal shows the one-time mobile login code to hand to the client. Advocate-created cases auto-approve on the backend.
  3. Opening the case loads GET /service-claims/:uid ({claim, claimChatThread}) plus the linked PMT questionnaire. The right rail shows client/case info and the Login Code (Used/Unused).
  4. If the case is pending (victim-initiated), the advocate Approves (PATCH /service-claims/approve/:uid) — which opens the SERVICE chat thread and notifies the client — or Rejects (PATCH .../reject/:uid). A received referral instead shows Accept/Decline referral.
  5. The advocate works the case across tabs: Chat with the client (attachments; real-time via message-service), record internal Notes (typed, TipTap), post client-facing Updates (optional notify), upload Files (with optional translation), and prepare Documents for e-signature.
  6. As needed, the advocate links client contact to an anonymous case (PATCH .../link-client/:claimUid, may issue a login code) or refers the case to an accepted partner (choose target service and field-level data to share → create referral; the client must consent on mobile before data is cloned to the partner).
  7. When work is complete, the advocate Resolves the case: the SurveyModalForm captures PMT/questionnaire values → PATCH /service-claims/resolve/:uid, which posts a "Resolved" update, archives the chat thread, and records resolution attribution. The case moves to the resolved bucket in the list.
  8. Branches: rejecting deletes the PMT snapshot; a resolved-then-reopened need is handled as a new case; referral acceptance/decline by the partner flips the referral's terminal state, reflected in the Referrals tab.

9.3 Build a Grant/Custom Report

  1. The admin opens Reports (gated by reports.pmt_reports) and reviews the explainer: set up grants → collect data via linked questionnaires → generate.
  2. Grant setup: create a grant with name/description, link questionnaires, and upload the funder's Excel template (POST /reports/grants). The backend can auto-generate the required questionnaire questions from the grant; linked questionnaires then collect data as staff resolve cases (answers land in each case's PMT tab).
  3. Generated (Excel) report path: in Report Management, select the grant, click New Report to clone the template (POST /reports/generated-reports), then edit in the Luckysheet editor. The AI ChatInterface (POST /reports/ai/chat) reads current cells and applies surgical edits/formulas under a strict confidence-and-approval policy; the report saves back as .xlsx (PUT .../file) with autosave and reset-to-template.
  4. NECX AI Report Builder path: open Custom Reports → setup form (type, title, org, date range, audience, sections, notes) → launch into the builder. The builder converts the form to a prompt and calls POST /reports/custom-reports/generate-chat; the backend fetches live agency data, assembles the prompt, calls the AI provider, and returns the report JSON, changed sections (highlighted/scrolled to), detected date range, and a validation result.
  5. Grounding with files: the user attaches Excel/CSV/PDF/DOCX/TXT in chat; non-txt files are parsed server-side (POST /reports/custom-reports/parse-file). File values override DB values; after generation the numeric validator flags conflicts (mismatches) and warnings (values not found in files = possible hallucination).
  6. Refine & finalize: the user manually edits sections (SectionEditModal, add/move/delete, undo/redo up to 20), the draft autosaves to localStorage every 30s and to the backend, then the user sets status to final (which locks the AI chat) and exports to PDF (client-side).
  7. Branches: sections ≥ 40 triggers backend skeletonization to fit token limits (user-added sections preserved verbatim); some live data sources are placeholders/zero and are flagged as not-yet-wired.

9.4 Manage a Shelter Bed

  1. The coordinator opens Shelter Locations, reviews KPI cards, and enters Bed Management for a location (/shelter/:locationId), which loads beds, stats, waitlist, history, incidents, available beds, and active assignments.
  2. To assign a walk-in: on an Available bed click Assign → choose New Client (or Existing / From Waitlist), enter details, set Expected Length of Stay (capped at the location's max_stay_nights), and for family rooms add family members up to capacity → POST /shelters/assignments/check-in.
  3. The backend, for a new unlinked client, finds-or-creates an app_user, creates an approved shelter service claim, and — if the user is unverified — mints a one-time login code. The modal shows the code (copyable) with delivery/instructions so the coordinator can hand it to the client. The bed flips to Occupied and history records a check-in.
  4. Ongoing management: the Occupied card shows a day counter vs expected stay (OVERDUE badge if exceeded). The coordinator can Check Out (reason → PATCH .../check-out, bed → Available), Transfer to another bed across their locations (PATCH .../transfer, client notified), or Log an Incident (type/severity/action → POST /shelters/history/log).
  5. Mobile request triage: a victim's mobile bed request arrives via shelter-request (socket to service-center-<id>), badging the Mobile Requests tab. The coordinator Accepts & Assigns (specific requested beds or auto), sends to Waitlist, or Cancels/Rejects; a 409 (no bed) on a pending request offers waitlisting instead. The app user is notified (assigned/rejected/waitlisted).
  6. Inter-agency transfer: from Partner Shelters the coordinator requests a bed at a partner (POST /shelters/transfers/request, message ≥20 chars); the partner accepts (assigns a bed via check-in) or declines.   7. Branches: confidential locations hide coordinates/address; occupied beds cannot be deleted; rooms/floors cannot be deleted until emptied.

9.5 Handle a Hotline Call

  1. The operator opens the Command Center and sets availability to Available (PATCH /hotlines/staff/my-status); StaffBoard, CallQueue, and RecentCalls load in parallel. Hotline socket events are registered on entry.
  2. An inbound call fires hotline:incoming to the service-center room; every Available operator sees a floating IncomingCallDialog (caller name/phone/line, auto-reject after 30s; OS notification if the tab is hidden). The first to Answer accepts the Twilio WebRTC leg and opens the LiveCallScreen; hotline:call-taken dismisses everyone else's dialog.
  3. During the call the operator sees an elapsed timer, mute/hold, and audio device selection. The live transcript streams in (hotline:transcript) and AI insights populate the target-labeled panel (hotline:insights, finalized by hotline:insights-resolved). The operator jots live notes and quick tags, and can add participants or transfer primary control (conference-backed).
  4. To end, the operator hangs up (PATCH /hotlines/calls/:id/end), moving the call to wrap-up. The operator completes the wrap-up form (category, mood, summary, services discussed, safety-plan and immediate-action flags, disposition), optionally pre-filled from AI insights (GET .../wrapup-prefill) → PATCH .../complete. Calls answered on a personal phone are auto-logged with no wrap-up.
  5. Disposition branches: created_case opens Create Case (seeded via prefill) and links the new case to the call; callback_scheduled (date+time) auto-creates a callback and emits hotline:callback-updated; linked_to_existing_case attaches to a searched case; referred_to_partner points to My Network.
  6. Outbound/callback: from CallQueue or Callbacks, Call opens a ringing screen immediately, then connects outbound; the real DB call id arrives via hotline:outbound; victim answering flips ringing→active (hotline:call-answered); no-answer resolves to a "no answer" state.
  7. Review: the Full Call Log offers filtered, exportable history with a detail drawer (assessment, outcome, linked case with "View Case"). Settings manage lines, staff assignments, insight targets, and AI transcript/insight toggles.

9.6 Real-Time Notification → UI Refresh

  1. A domain event (new claim, new message, shelter request, partnership action) fires on the backend, which writes a notification row and emits a Socket.IO event to the target room (vs360-user-<uid> or service-center-<id>).
  2. VS360's registered handler (socketEventCallbacks) shows a toast (NotificationUtil) and dispatches the matching refetch.* flag (e.g. setClaimsRefetch, setShelterRequestRefetch, setSpecificChatRefetch) plus a notification refetch.
  3. Pages observing that flag re-run their React Query queries; sidebar/header badges update from the Redux notification slice.
  4. Resilience: on disconnect/connect_error the app attempts reconnect (debounced), a periodic interval checks connectivity, socket.io's own reconnection runs, and on reconnect user/thread rooms are rejoined.

13. Non-Functional Requirements

13.1 Security & Privacy (Victim Safety)

  • Cases are anonymous by default; contact linking is a deliberate, consented action that may issue a login code. Confidential shelter locations hide coordinates/address.
  • Cross-agency data sharing (referrals) is opt-in per field (canShare* all default false) and requires client consent on the mobile app before any case data is cloned to the partner.
  • Hotline hotline:* socket payloads are suppressed from debug logging to avoid PII leakage; live transcripts carry PII and are handled accordingly.
  • API responses are AES-encrypted in non-dev environments and decrypted client-side; requests pass a Netlify edge-proxy shared secret. Tokens use sliding refresh; 401 forces full logout/state wipe.
  • Strict multi-tenant isolation: backend /web/* routes scope by issuer.serviceCenterInfo.id (CJIS cross-agency isolation for notes and cases).

13.2 Auditability

Nearly all create/update/delete operations write an ActionLogs row (action, referenceType, source/ip/url/context) for a full compliance audit trail.

13.3 Performance & Availability

  • React Query caching (5-min stale / 10-min cache), pagination on large lists, infinite scroll for chat and the full call log, and skeleton/fade-in loading states.
  • Socket resilience: reconnection (8 attempts, 2–10s backoff) plus app-level reconnect and periodic connectivity checks; room rejoin on reconnect.
  • Large-report token management (skeletonization at ≥40 sections) to keep AI generation within limits.

13.4 Compliance & Reporting

Reporting is built for federal/grant PMT (VOCA/VAWA-aligned): grants carry funder Excel templates, questionnaires collect required data through casework, and the AI builder enforces accuracy (prompt-level "never fabricate" rules plus a post-generation numeric validator with conflict/hallucination flags; file values override DB values).

13.5 Localization & Accessibility

No global i18n framework; UI strings are English. An on-demand content-translation service handles chat/file translation to a recipient's preferred language. Mantine components provide baseline accessibility; keyboard shortcuts exist for dashboard quick actions and bed management. Light/dark themes are supported (necx-color-scheme).
