2. Executive Summary

NECX Inc operates a coordinated platform that serves victims and survivors of crime and the agencies, advocates, and caseworkers who help them. The platform spans four clients over a single unified backend: the Victim mobile app (for survivors), the VS360 web workspace (for agency staff), the NECX Admin console (for platform operators), and the product described here — the NECX Workspace staff mobile app.

The Workspace app is the on-the-go tool for agency staff who normally work in the VS360 web dashboard. It puts the caseworker's daily work in their pocket: monitoring agency performance on a dashboard, working the queue of assigned service claims (which the app surfaces to users as "Cases"), communicating in real time over internal, agency-to-agency, and inquiry chat, handling public inquiries, managing PMT/grant reporting, and generating publication-quality AI custom reports with on-device PDF export.

The app is built with Flutter using a feature-first clean architecture, Riverpod state management, and GoRouter navigation. It authenticates staff with a two-factor email/SMS one-time-passcode (OTP) flow against the shared NECX backend at the /v2/web API surface, and it uses Socket.IO for real-time chat. It is a companion, not a replacement: the full breadth of agency administration remains in VS360 web, while the mobile app concentrates on the tasks staff most need to do away from their desks.

This document specifies the product as it exists in the documented develop build, and it is explicit about the boundary between what is implemented and what is planned. Several capabilities named in prior product discussions — a case-management AI agent, shelter management, a staff time clock, push notifications (FCM), and biometric/PIN unlock — are not implemented in any inspected branch and are called out as out of scope with clear risk flags. Two security caveats surfaced during discovery (hardcoded third-party API keys and on-device large-language-model calls) are documented as high-priority risks with remediation owners.

3. Product Overview & Vision

3.1 Problem Statement

Agency caseworkers and advocates are frequently away from their desks — in the field, in court, in shelters, or between appointments — yet the events that drive victim outcomes (a new case, a published update a survivor is waiting on, an urgent chat message, a referral needing action) do not wait for them to return to a browser. Today the full VS360 web workspace is the only way to act on this work, which forces staff to defer time-sensitive actions or work from personal notes. The Workspace app closes that gap by bringing the highest-frequency, highest-urgency slice of VS360 to a phone.

3.2 Product Vision

NECX Workspace aims to be the trusted mobile extension of the caseworker's desk: fast to open, safe by default, and focused on the handful of workflows that matter most in the moment — reviewing and advancing assigned cases, staying in the conversation with clients and partner agencies, keeping an eye on agency health, and producing the reports that keep grants funded. It should feel like VS360, respect the same permissions and multi-tenant boundaries, and never compromise the safety, privacy, and dignity of the survivors whose data it touches.

3.3 Position in the NECX Ecosystem

The NECX platform is a single unified backend (Node.js/Express, PostgreSQL via Objection/Knex, Socket.IO, Redis, BullMQ, Azure Blob Storage, Twilio, and OpenAI/Anthropic) serving four clients through a tri-router pattern (/admin, /web, /mobile):

  • Victim App — the survivor-facing mobile app (issuer type APP); requests services, tracks case status, receives published updates.
  • Workspace App (this product) — the agency-staff mobile app (issuer type VSMS, role context VS360); the mobile companion to VS360, consuming the /v2/web surface (with one /v2/mobile call for the richer user profile).
  • VS360 Web — the full agency workspace; the authoritative surface for agency administration.
  • NECX Admin — platform operators (issuer type NECX, context ADMIN).

The Workspace app is deliberately scoped to a subset of VS360. It shares VS360's identity, RBAC, multi-tenancy, and data model, but it implements the mobile-appropriate subset of workflows and defers breadth (e.g., hotline command center, shelter management, notes-center analytics, deep admin) to the web.

4. Goals, Objectives & Success Metrics

4.1 Goals & Objectives

  • Give agency staff secure, authenticated mobile access to their assigned cases and the ability to advance them (approve, reject, resolve, note, update, upload, refer, chat) from a phone.
  • Provide an at-a-glance agency dashboard (KPIs, demographics, victim needs, service performance, reminders) that mirrors the VS360 dashboard's key signals.
  • Keep staff in real-time conversation across internal (staff-to-staff), agency-to-agency, and inquiry chat threads.
  • Enable staff to produce grant/PMT and AI custom reports on the go, with PDF export for sharing.
  • Enforce the same identity, RBAC, and per-agency (tenant) isolation as VS360, including OTP two-factor authentication.
  • Preserve survivor safety and privacy in every mobile surface (never expose internal notes to victims, respect published-update gating, honor referral consent).

5. Scope

5.1 In Scope

The following are implemented in the documented develop build and are in scope for this PRD:

  • Authentication & session: email/password sign-in followed by 6-digit OTP two-factor (2FA); OTP resend with countdown; multi-step password reset (channel check → OTP → new password); token storage in secure storage; sliding token refresh; forced sign-out on 401.
  • Navigation shell: a 5-tab bottom-navigation shell (Dashboard, Cases, Chat, Reports, More) plus a Quick Access grid on the "More" screen launching all secondary features.
  • Dashboard: KPI cards, demographics and claim-trend charts, victim needs and trends, service performance and provider insights, reminders preview, and a notification bell with unread badge.
  • Case (claim) management: "My Cases" list with search, status filters, and stats; case detail with six tabs (Overview, Chat, Notes, Updates, Documents/Files, Referrals); approve/reject/resolve; link client; file upload; claim-question answers; per-case referrals (create/accept/decline).
  • Chat (real-time, Socket.IO): internal (staff-to-staff), agency-to-agency, and inquiry threads; thread lists with unread badges; message send with attachments; read tracking; inquiry moderation (approve/reject/resolve).
  • Inquiries: inquiry threads grouped by status with approve/reject/resolve.
  • Reports: PMT reports hub, grants (list/detail/edit and generated reports), PMT/VOCA questionnaires (list/detail/matrix).
  • AI Custom Report Builder: saved custom reports; a 4-step wizard (Type → Details → Content → Generate); a section-based editor with an AI chat panel and per-section improve/edit; save/finalize/draft status; PDF export.
  • Users, Roles, Services, My Network, Communities: staff user CRUD, roles view; victim-services CRUD and activate/deactivate; partnerships and referrals ("My Network"); communities (posts, comments, members, group chat, moderation).
  • Tools: calendar + reminders (month/list views, filters, create/edit/complete/delete).
  • Settings/"More": profile and agency-profile view/edit; email & SMS notification toggles; support links; feedback submission; sign out.
  • In-app notifications: REST-polled notification list with mark-read / mark-all-read; unread badges derived from the list.

5.2 Out of Scope

The following are named in prior product discussions or implied by dependencies but are not implemented in any inspected branch (develop, necx-571, necx-572, staging, main). They are out of scope for this baseline and tracked as risks/open questions:

  • Case Management AI Agent — planned/not implemented. Branch necx-572-implement-case-management-ai-agent contains no agent code (it is an empty merge of the navigation refactor). Treat as a future capability.
  • Shelter management — no shelter locations/floors/rooms/beds/assignments/waitlist/transfers UI or flows exist in the app, though the backend has a full shelter domain and a shelter_overview dashboard permission.
  • Staff timer / time clock — no time-entry UI, despite a backend time module (staff_time_entries) and a time.view permission.
  • Push notifications (FCM) — no Firebase/firebase_messaging dependency or code; flutter_local_notifications is declared but unused. Notifications are REST-polled only. Device-token registration (account/device-token) is not called.
  • Biometric / PIN unlock — local_auth and pin_code_fields are dependencies and a pin_setup storage flag exists, but there is no PIN or biometric screen/flow. (pin_code_fields is used only to render OTP boxes.)
  • Offline data cache / connectivity awareness — hive, hive_flutter, and connectivity_plus are declared but unused; all data is fetched live per screen with pull-to-refresh; connectivity loss surfaces as request errors.
  • Real localization — the language picker (English/Spanish/French) is a no-op dialog; all strings are hardcoded English; text scaling is forced to 1.0.
  • Dark theme — only a single light theme ships.
  • Navigation Drawer — the drawer-based navigation refactor (necx-571) is not on develop.
  • In-app subscriptions — the app-subscription work lives on a separate branch and is out of scope here.
  • Hotline / command center, notes-center analytics surface, data-migration, translation-tool UI — backend endpoints exist (/tool/translate, /tool/translate/file) but no dedicated mobile UI ships.
⚠ Planned-but-absent
Any reference elsewhere in the platform to a mobile "Case Management AI Agent," shelter management, or a staff timer must be read as a roadmap item, not a shipped feature. The Workspace app does not implement them today.

Priya — Advocate handling referrals. Priya works cross-agency cases. When a partner agency refers a client to her agency, the cloned case appears in her Cases list; she reviews the shared snapshot on the Referrals tab and approves the case, which flips the referral to accepted and opens a service chat thread with the client.

7.3 Networking, Interceptors & Response Encryption

api_client.dart builds a Dio client with three interceptors:

  1. ApiLoggingInterceptor — request/response logging.
  2. AuthInterceptor — on request, adds Authorization: Bearer <accessToken>, x-refresh-token, and X-Netlify-Secret. On response, captures a rotated x-refresh-token header and stores it. On 401 (non-/auth/ endpoints) it wipes storage and forces unauthenticated (GoRouter then redirects to sign-in); /auth/ endpoints pass through to avoid a sign-out→401 loop. On 403 it attempts a token refresh (POST /auth/refresh with x-refresh-token) and retries the original request once.
  3. Decryption interceptor — the backend may return AES-encrypted bodies (platform encryption-middleware); _decryptAndParse transparently decrypts and JSON-parses both success and error bodies.

Endpoint paths are centralized in api_endpoints.dart, except the custom-report paths, which are hardcoded in custom_reports_api.dart.

7.4 Real-time & Notifications

  • Sockets: socket_service.dart connects to socketUrl over websocket with auth {token: <accessToken>} and auto-reconnect (5 attempts, 3–5s). It joins/leaves thread-<uid> rooms on open/close and listens for message-internal, message-inquiry, message-agency-chat, and message-service. On an incoming message for the active thread it appends the message and reloads thread lists to refresh unread counts.
  • Push (FCM): not present. In-app notifications are fetched by REST polling (GET /users/notifications); unread badges derive from that list.

7.5 Tenancy & Identity

Every request is authenticated to a VSMS issuer with role context VS360 and an associated serviceCenterInfo (agency). The backend enforces multi-tenancy: all agency-scoped reads/writes are filtered to the issuer's serviceCenterInfo.id, and the allowOnly('VS360') context gate plus fine-grained checkPermission(module, action) gates protect each endpoint. The app must therefore assume its data is already agency-scoped and must render UI according to the user's permission JSON.

7.6 Security & AI-Call Caveats

⚠ Hardcoded secrets
Anthropic and OpenAI API keys and the X-Netlify-Secret header value are hardcoded in source (env_config.dart, api_interceptor.dart). Secrets embedded in a shipped mobile binary are extractable and must be treated as compromised; they require rotation and a server-mediated design (see Risks FR/NFR).
⚠ On-device LLM calls
AI custom-report generation calls Anthropic (claude-haiku-4-5-20251001) — with an OpenAI gpt-4o fallback — directly from the device using the embedded key. This exposes the key, bypasses server-side accuracy validation and rate limiting, and can send agency/case-derived data to a third party from the client. The platform already exposes a server-mediated equivalent (/reports/ai/chat, /reports/custom-reports/generate-chat) that should be adopted.

9. Detailed User & System Flows

9.1 Sign In (two-factor via OTP)

  1. The user launches the app; main.dart runs checkAuth(). If access_token + user_data exist, state is authenticated (splash → Dashboard); otherwise state is unauthenticated and the router shows the Sign-In screen.
  2. On Sign-In, the user enters email and password and taps Sign In; AuthController.signIn posts {identifier, password} to POST /auth/sign-in.
  3. The backend validates credentials against user_credentials (bcrypt), generates an OTP, and delivers it via the user's channel (email or SMS/Twilio). The response returns a privateKey; state becomes tempAuthenticated and the router redirects to the OTP screen (/verify-user). A 179-second validity timer starts.
  4. On the OTP screen, the user enters the 6-digit code; verifyOtp posts {privateKey, otp} to POST /auth/verify-profile.
  5. On success, the backend issues an access token and refresh token and returns the profile (with roleInfo/serviceCenterInfo); the app stores accessToken, refreshToken, and user_data; state becomes authenticated; the router redirects to /dashboard.
  6. Branch — resend: if the timer expires or the code is not received, the user taps Resend; resendOtp posts POST /auth/otp-request, returns a new privateKey, and the timer resets.
  7. Branch — wrong OTP: verify-profile returns an error; the app shows an inline error and the user retries (subject to the auth rate limiter).
  8. Branch — profile enrichment: after authentication, refreshUserProfile calls GET /mobile/users/{uid} (via the /v2/mobile rewrite) to load the richer staff profile.

9.2 Password Reset

  1. From Sign-In, the user taps Forgot Password (/forgot-password) and enters their email; the app calls POST /auth/check-reset-channels.
  2. The backend returns the channels available for the account (email and/or SMS); the user chooses a channel.
  3. The app calls POST /auth/reset-password-trigger; the backend sends an OTP and returns a privateKey.
  4. The user enters the OTP; the app calls POST /auth/verify-reset-password.
  5. On success, the user enters and confirms a new password; the app calls PATCH /auth/reset-password.
  6. On success, the user is returned to Sign-In to authenticate with the new password (and OTP). Branch: an invalid/expired OTP returns an error and the user may resend/retry.

9.3 Open & Update a Case

  1. The user taps the Cases tab; ClaimsController.loadClaims calls GET /service-claims. The list renders with search, status filter chips, stat chips, and per-case unread badges. (Visibility respects claims.view_all; without it, only pending/self-actioned cases appear, always within the user's agency.)
  2. The user taps a case; the app calls GET /service-claims/{uid} and renders the detail with six tabs (Overview, Chat, Notes, Updates, Documents/Files, Referrals).
  3. Approve (Overview): for a pending case, the user taps Approve; the app calls the approve endpoint (/service-claims/approve/{uid}). The backend transitions pending→approved, creates a published "Approved" update, opens a SERVICE chat thread (victim↔caseworker), records approvedBy/At in stats, flips any referral to accepted, and notifies the victim (notification row + FCM + socket). The Chat tab now has an active thread.
  4. Add an internal note (Notes): the user opens the Notes tab (GET /service-claims/get-notes/{claimUid}), taps add, enters a title/description and note type, and saves (POST /service-claims/new-note/{claimUid}). Notes are internal only and never shown to the victim.
  5. Publish an update (Updates): the user opens the Updates tab, creates an update (POST /service-claims/new-update/{claimUid}), and marks it published. Published updates become visible to the victim (mobile filters isPublished=true) and trigger a victim notification.
  6. Upload a document (Documents): the user taps upload, picks up to 5 files (≤50 MB; jpg/png/pdf/xls/doc/csv); the app posts multipart to POST /service-claims/upload-files/{uid}; the backend stores each file (context SERVICE_CLAIM) and returns the updated list.
  7. Resolve (Overview): when work is complete, the user taps Resolve; the app calls /service-claims/resolve/{uid} (optionally with pmtValues, threadUid). The backend transitions approved→resolved, posts a published "Resolved" update, records resolvedBy/At, archives the chat thread, and notifies the victim.
  8. Branch — reject: for a pending case, Reject (/service-claims/reject/{uid}) transitions pending→rejected, records rejectedBy/At, deletes the PMT report, flips any referral to declined, and notifies the victim.
  9. Branch — link client: for an anonymous/advocate-created case, the user links a client (PATCH /service-claims/link-client/{claimUid}), providing a contact and channel; the backend attaches the contact and issues (or reuses) a login code delivered to the survivor.
  10. Branch — referral: on the Referrals tab, the user creates a referral to a partner agency (POST /partnerships/referrals); the backend enforces the single-active-referral rule (409 if one exists), and the referral proceeds through client consent (in the Victim app) before the case is cloned into the partner agency.
  11. Edge — permission denied / stale data: actions the user's role does not permit return 403 (surfaced as an error); a 401 clears the session and forces sign-in; connectivity loss surfaces as a snackbar (no offline queueing).

9.4 Generate an AI Custom Report

  1. The user opens Reports → AI Report Builder (custom_report_builder_screen); the app calls GET /reports/custom-reports and lists saved reports plus a how-it-works panel.
  2. The user taps New; the wizard (report_wizard_screen) opens. Step 1 — Type: the user picks a report type. Step 2 — Details: the user enters organization name, date range, and audience. Step 3 — Content: the user selects the sections to include. Step 4 — Generate: the user taps Generate.
  3. Generation runs (today) client-side in report_ai_service.dart: the default provider is Anthropic claude-haiku-4-5-20251001 via https://api.anthropic.com/v1/messages (fallback OpenAI gpt-4o), using the embedded API key. The model returns JSON {message, reportUpdate, changedSections, detectedDateRange}, which becomes the structured report (typed sections: kpi, charts, tables, narrative, etc.).
  4. The app opens the Editor (report_editor_screen) rendering the sections (section_renderer), with an AI chat panel (ai_chat_panel). The user iterates: chatting to adjust the report (sendChatMessage) or improving a single section (improveSection).
  5. The user saves the report: create (POST /reports/custom-reports) or update (PUT /reports/custom-reports/{id}). Finalize sets status final; mark-draft sets draft. (A final report locks non-AI edits server-side.)
  6. Export: the user taps Export; pdf_export_service.dart builds a PDF (pdf/printing) for share/print.
  7. Branch — duplicate: the user duplicates a saved report (POST /reports/custom-reports/{id}/duplicate), which resets it to draft with a "(Copy)" title.
  8. Edge — generation error / key failure: an AI provider error (rate limit, network, invalid key) surfaces as an error; the user may retry.
  9. Target-state (must-fix) variant: generation is routed through the server (/reports/custom-reports/generate-chat), so keys stay server-side, live agency data and uploaded-file metrics feed the prompt, and the post-generation accuracy validator returns PASS/FAIL and conflict/hallucination warnings alongside the report.

9.5 Chat (Internal / Agency / Inquiry)

  1. The user taps the Chat tab; ChatController.loadAllThreads fetches, in parallel, GET /chat-services/get-inquiry-threads, /get-internal-threads, and /get-agency-threads. Thread lists render with unread badges; the tab shows an aggregate unread badge.
  2. Start a chat: the user taps New chat, picks a colleague (internal) or partner agency; the app calls POST /chat-services/start-internal-chat (or /start-agency-chat). If a thread already exists, the backend returns 400 and the app resolves the existing thread uid and opens it.
  3. Open a thread: the app calls GET /chat-services/get-all-chats/{threadUid}, marks it read (/chat-services/read/{threadUid}), and joins the socket room thread-<uid>.
  4. Send a message: the user types (and optionally attaches files) and sends; the app posts to POST /chat-services/send-message/{threadUid} and reloads the message list; files are listed via /chat-services/get-all-files/{threadUid}.
  5. Receive live: the socket service listens for message-internal/message-agency-chat/message-inquiry/message-service; for the active thread the message is appended immediately; thread lists are reloaded to refresh unread counts.
  6. Leave: on closing the thread, the app leaves the socket room.
  7. Branch — inquiry moderation: in an inquiry thread the user may approve/reject/resolve the inquiry (/chat-services/inquiry/{approve|reject|resolve}/{id}).
  8. Edge — reconnect: on socket disconnect, auto-reconnect retries up to 5 times (3–5s); missed messages are recovered on the next thread/list reload (REST is the source of truth).

9.6 Dashboard Load & Refresh

  1. On opening the Dashboard tab, DashboardController.loadDashboardData issues GET /dashboard/kpi-data, /demographics-and-claim-trends, /victim-needs-and-trends, /service-performance-and-provider-insights, and GET /reminders.
  2. KPI cards, charts, victim-needs, service-performance, and a reminders preview render; the notification bell shows an unread badge.
  3. The user pulls to refresh; all five calls re-fetch and the UI updates.
  4. Edge — partial failure: a failed call surfaces an error for that section without blocking the others; a 401 forces sign-in.

9.7 Session Expiry & Forced Sign-out

  1. A background or foreground request returns 401 on a non-auth endpoint.
  2. The AuthInterceptor wipes secure storage and calls forceUnauthenticated().
  3. The GoRouter guard redirects to Sign-In; the user must re-authenticate (email/password + OTP).
  4. Branch — 403: a 403 first triggers POST /auth/refresh (with x-refresh-token) and one retry of the original request before any user-visible failure.

13. Non-Functional Requirements

13.1 Security

  • Transport: all API traffic over HTTPS to the /v2/web surface; tokens sent as Authorization: Bearer + x-refresh-token; rotated refresh tokens captured from response headers.
  • Secrets (must-fix): Anthropic/OpenAI keys and X-Netlify-Secret are hardcoded in the shipped binary and are therefore extractable — treat as compromised, rotate, and remove from the client. Route AI calls through server endpoints; deliver proxy secrets via secure server mediation, not the client.
  • Session: access + refresh tokens and user JSON in flutter_secure_storage; 401 → full session wipe; 403 → single refresh-and-retry.
  • Two-factor: OTP 2FA is mandatory on every sign-in; there is no bypass path.
  • Response encryption: the app transparently decrypts AES-encrypted response bodies (matching the backend encryption-middleware).

13.2 Privacy & Victim Safety

  • Internal notes (claim_notes) are staff-only and must never be surfaced to victims; the app must respect this on the case Notes tab.
  • Updates are victim-visible only when isPublished=true; the app must make the published/unpublished state explicit when authoring.
  • Referral consent: case data is shared cross-agency only per the survivor's consent and the canShare* flags; the app must not present a referral as sharing more than granted.
  • On-device AI (must-fix): the current on-device generation path can send case/agency-derived content to a third party from the client; moving to server mediation restores central control, redaction, and audit.
  • Login codes: linking a client issues a login code the app must handle as a sensitive credential.

13.3 Performance

  • Dashboard load issues five parallel calls; the app should render progressively and support pull-to-refresh.
  • Chat should render incoming socket messages within perceptible real-time (sub-second on a healthy connection); REST is the source of truth on reconnect.
  • Case detail is data-heavy (six tabs); tabs should load lazily where possible.
  • No offline cache: every screen fetches live; the app must handle latency and errors gracefully with loaders and snackbars.

13.4 Availability & Resilience

  • Socket auto-reconnect: 5 attempts at 3–5s; on reconnect, thread/list reloads recover missed messages.
  • Connectivity loss surfaces as request errors (no queueing); the app must present clear retry affordances. (Offline support is a future enhancement — see Risks.)

13.5 Auditability

  • The backend writes action_logs for nearly all create/update/delete operations (with source/ip/url/context). Mobile actions inherit this audit trail; no additional client-side audit is required.

13.6 Localization & Accessibility

  • Localization: not implemented — all strings hardcoded English; the language picker is a no-op; this is a known gap for a survivor-serving platform and a future requirement.
  • Text scaling: currently forced to 1.0 in main.dart, which limits dynamic-type accessibility; this should be revisited to honor system font scaling.
  • Theming: single light theme only; no dark mode.

13.7 Compliance

  • The app operates within a platform that supports grant/PMT/VOCA reporting; report generation and PMT data capture must preserve the fidelity funders require. The server-side AI accuracy validator (verified-metrics cross-check) should be the source of truth for report numbers — a reason to retire the on-device generation path.
  • Cross-agency data isolation (CJIS-style tenant separation) is enforced server-side; the app must never assume access beyond the authenticated agency.

14. Analytics, Reporting & Notifications

14.1 Dashboards & KPIs

The in-app Dashboard mirrors VS360's key signals: claim KPIs (new/completed/total), inquiry counts, average response time, service-provider rating, repeat-victim rate, demographic and claim trends, victim needs, and service performance. Data comes from the /dashboard/* endpoints and renders via fl_chart. Location-based and predictive-insight endpoints exist and may be surfaced later.

14.2 Reporting

Two report surfaces: (a) PMT/grants — grants, generated Excel reports, and PMT/VOCA questionnaires with an answers matrix; and (b) the AI Custom Report Builder — structured JSON reports with an AI chat editor and PDF export. The server side additionally offers a stricter PMT Excel AI editor and a post-generation accuracy validator (verified-metrics table, conflict/hallucination flags) that the mobile app should adopt via the server-mediated endpoints.

14.3 Notifications

  • In-app (implemented): REST-polled notification list (GET /users/notifications) with mark-read / mark-all-read; unread badges on the Chat tab and dashboard bell derive from fetched lists and thread reloads. Per-claim and per-thread mark-read endpoints keep counts accurate.
  • Socket (implemented): live chat message events update the active thread and refresh unread counts.
  • Preferences (implemented): email & SMS notification toggles (PATCH /notifications/{email,sms}-notifications).
  • Push / FCM (not implemented): the platform sends FCM push for staff notifications, but this app registers no device token and receives no push; background/closed-app delivery is therefore absent. This is a priority gap (see Risks).
