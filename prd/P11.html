2. Executive Summary

NECX serves victims and survivors of crime and the agencies, advocates, and caseworkers who help them. Coordinated victim services depend on fast, private coordination between the staff who deliver those services — a caseworker needs to consult a supervisor about a safety plan, a hotline advocate needs to hand a call context to a colleague, and a shelter coordinator needs a quick answer about bed availability. Internal Chat is the staff-to-staff messaging channel that carries these conversations inside a single agency (service center).

Internal Chat is deliberately distinct from the other NECX chat surfaces. It is not the agency↔victim conversation (that is the INQUIRY and SERVICE thread types) and it is not the agency↔partner-agency conversation (that is AGENCY_CHAT / the My Network chat). Internal Chat is confined to the staff of one agency talking to one another — one-to-one direct messages today, with a planned group-chat extension for team conversations. It reuses the platform's shared chat backbone (chat_threads / chat_messages), so it inherits file attachments, read receipts, real-time Socket.IO delivery, and cross-channel notifications, while remaining scoped and gated to internal staff.

Because these conversations happen in the context of victim services, Internal Chat is a privacy-sensitive surface: staff discuss case details, safety concerns, and client information. The feature therefore inherits NECX's tenancy isolation (an agency's staff can only message within their own agency), permission gating (internal_chat.view), auditability, and the platform's security posture (encrypted transport, authenticated sockets). This PRD specifies the capability end to end: contacts/user list, direct messages, the planned group chats, attachments, presence, unread counts, real-time delivery and read state, the data model, endpoints, socket events, RBAC, non-functional requirements, notifications, and risks.

3. Product Overview & Vision

3.1 Problem statement

Agency staff coordinating victim services need a lightweight, private, real-time way to talk to each other without leaving the NECX workspace. Before Internal Chat, staff coordination fell back on external tools (personal SMS, email, third-party messengers) that sit outside NECX's tenancy, audit, and privacy controls — a poor fit when the subject matter is victim safety and confidential case detail. Coordination context (who is handling a case, what a colleague advised) is then lost from the platform entirely.

3.2 Product vision

Internal Chat gives every agency a secure, in-platform staff messenger. A staff member opens the Chat page, sees the roster of their agency's users with presence and unread badges, clicks a colleague, and is in a live conversation — with file sharing, read receipts, and instant delivery. Conversations stay inside the agency's tenant boundary, are gated by permission, and are carried on the same reliable messaging backbone as the rest of NECX. The vision extends to group chats so whole teams (e.g. the hotline shift, the shelter team) can coordinate in one place.

3.3 Where this sits in the NECX ecosystem

Internal Chat is a module of the VS360 web app (the agency-staff dashboard) at route /internal-chat, served by the unified NECX backend chat module (/web/chat-services/*). It sits alongside the other NECX surfaces:

  • Victim App (mobile) — victims open INQUIRY/SERVICE chats with agencies; victims never participate in Internal Chat.
  • Workspace App (staff mobile) — staff clients of the same backend; Internal Chat routes are web-oriented today (see Scope).
  • VS360 web — hosts Internal Chat (InternalChat.jsx), Agency Chat (My Network), and Inquiries (victim chat) as three separate surfaces sharing one message pane.
  • NECX Admin — platform super-admin console; not a participant in agency Internal Chat.
  • Unified backend — one chat module serves all thread types (INQUIRY, SERVICE, INTERNAL, AGENCY_CHAT), with Internal Chat being the INTERNAL type.

4. Goals, Objectives & Success Metrics

4.1 Goals & objectives

  • Provide secure, private, in-platform staff-to-staff direct messaging scoped to a single agency.
  • Deliver messages in real time over Socket.IO, with FCM/in-app notification fallback when the recipient is not actively viewing the thread.
  • Surface presence and unread counts so staff know who is reachable and where messages are waiting.
  • Support file attachments (documents, images) on internal messages using the shared upload pipeline.
  • Provide read receipts so senders know when a colleague has seen a message.
  • Keep all conversation within the agency's tenant boundary and behind the internal_chat.view permission.
  • Establish the foundation for group chats (team conversations) as a fast-follow extension.

5. Scope

5.1 In Scope

  • User list / contacts — the roster of the current user's agency staff, from which a staff member picks a colleague to message.
  • Direct messages (1:1) — start (with de-duplication of existing threads), send, and read staff-to-staff messages within the agency (INTERNAL thread type).
  • Attachments — up to 5 files per message via the shared file_uploads pipeline; per-thread file listing.
  • Presence — online/offline indication for staff, derived from active socket connections (see Risks for current-state caveat).
  • Unread counts — per-thread and aggregate internal-chat unread badges driven by the notification list / INTERNAL_NEW_MESSAGE type.
  • Read receipts / read state — mark a thread read; per-user unread computation on chat_messages.
  • Real-time delivery — Socket.IO message-internal and thread-creation-internal events, per-thread rooms.
  • Notifications — in-app (service_center_notifications), socket, and FCM push for internal messages when appropriate.
  • RBAC — gating on the internal_chat module view permission and agency tenancy.
  • Group chats (planned extension) — multi-party staff conversations. Documented here as the target design; see 5.2 for current-state limitation.

5.2 Out of Scope

  • Agency↔victim chat (INQUIRY / SERVICE thread types) — victim intake and case conversations are covered by the Chat Platform PRD, not here.
  • Agency↔agency chat (AGENCY_CHAT / My Network / pre-partnership chat) — cross-agency staff messaging is a separate surface.
  • Cross-agency internal messaging — Internal Chat is intra-agency only; a staff member cannot DM staff of a different agency via this module (that is Agency Chat).
  • Victim-facing participation — victims (app_users) are never participants in Internal Chat.
  • Group chats in the current backend build — the INTERNAL thread type is 1:1 only today. The chat_threads model carries exactly two staff participants (vsmsId, vsmsId2), and start-internal-chat takes a single receiverId. Multi-party staff group chat is planned but not yet implemented in the Internal Chat module; the platform's existing GroupChatMessages model powers Communities group chats (victim-facing), not staff Internal Chat. This is tracked as an Open Question / Risk.
  • Message editing / deletion / reactions / typing indicators — not present in the current chat backbone.
  • Voice / video calling between staff — out of scope (hotline module handles telephony).
⚠ Group chat current state
Staff-to-staff group chat is specified in this PRD as the target design, but the shipped Internal Chat backend supports 1:1 direct messages only. Sections that describe group behaviour are marked as planned. Implementation would extend the thread model to support N participants (or reuse the GroupChatMessages pattern from Communities). Do not represent group chat as available until built.

7. System Context & Architecture

7.1 Tech stack

  • Backend: Express (ESM) + Objection/Knex over PostgreSQL, Socket.IO, Redis, FCM (notificationServices), Azure Blob (attachments via fileService/file_uploads). Chat module files: src/apps/chat/chat-{routes,controllers,services}.js. Models: ChatThreads, ChatMessages, FileUploads (and GroupChatMessages for the group precedent).
  • Frontend (VS360 web): React 18 + Vite + Mantine + TanStack Query + Redux. Internal Chat pages: src/pages/InternalChat/InternalChat.jsx, ActiveChat.jsx, components/pages/InternalChat/UserList.jsx. Shared message pane reused from ActiveInquiry.jsx. Services: src/services/chat.js, threads.js, users.js.

7.2 How it talks to the backend

Internal Chat routes are mounted under the global /v2 prefix on the chat module base /web/chat-services (web) and guarded by allowOnly('VS360'), with rateLimiters.chatApi. The VS360 axios client injects Authorization: Bearer <accessToken>, x-refresh-token, X-Netlify-Secret, and X-Environment on every call; non-development responses are AES-decrypted client-side. Responses use the platform { status, msg, data } shape.

Key endpoints used by Internal Chat: POST /chat-services/start-internal-chat, GET /chat-services/get-internal-threads, GET /chat-services/get-thread/:threadUid, POST /chat-services/send-message/:threadUid, GET /chat-services/get-all-chats/:threadUid, GET /chat-services/get-all-files/:threadUid, PATCH /chat-services/read/:threadUid, and the agency user roster GET /users (pageless).

7.3 Real-time & notifications

A single Socket.IO connection is created per session (SocketContext.jsx), authenticated by JWT in the socket auth handshake (socket-middelware.js on the backend). The client joins vs360-user-<uid>, service-center-<id>, global-notifications, and per-thread thread-<threadUid>-<userUid> rooms (the last when a chat opens). Internal messages emit semantic events (message-internal, thread-creation-internal) to the relevant rooms; the frontend's socket callbacks show a toast and set refetch.internalThreadsRefetch / specific-chat refetch flags that trigger TanStack Query refetches. Offline recipients also receive a DB notification row (service_center_notifications, type INTERNAL_NEW_MESSAGE) and an FCM push.

7.4 Tenancy

Multi-tenancy is enforced by issuer.serviceCenterInfo.id: a VS360 user only ever sees and messages staff within their own agency. Internal thread queries (getInternalThreadsForVSMSUser) and the user roster are scoped to the agency. internal_chat.view further gates access. NECX platform super-admins are not participants; agency super-admins bypass fine-grained checks within their own agency.

8. Functional Requirements

8.1 User list / contacts

The Internal Chat left pane renders the roster of the current user's agency staff (UserList.jsx), fetched via GET /users (pageless, agency-scoped). Each entry shows the staff member's name/role, presence indicator, and unread badge. Selecting a user resolves an existing INTERNAL thread between the two users (matching vsmsId/vsmsId2) from the loaded internal threads; if none exists the right pane shows a "Start Chat" prompt. Deep-linking is supported via router state.senderId (e.g. arriving from a notification).

8.2 Direct messages (1:1)

Starting a DM calls POST /chat-services/start-internal-chat with receiverId. The backend (startInternalChat) de-duplicates an existing INTERNAL thread between the two users (findExistingInternalThread); if none exists it creates a new chat_threads row (type='INTERNAL', vsmsId=initiator, vsmsId2=receiver, status='ACTIVE'). Both participants must belong to the same agency. Sending a message uses POST /chat-services/send-message/:threadUid (multer files, up to 5). Internal messages create a service_center_notifications row (type INTERNAL_NEW_MESSAGE, additionalData carrying threadUid), emit via Socket.IO to the recipient, and send an FCM push to the recipient's device token(s) for background delivery. 9. Detailed User & System Flows

9.1 Start a direct message (DM) with a colleague

  1. A staff member opens the Chat page (/internal-chat); ProtectedPage verifies auth and the internal_chat.view permission.
  2. The left pane loads the agency roster via GET /users (pageless, scoped to the user's service center) and the user's existing internal threads via GET /chat-services/get-internal-threads.
  3. The staff member selects a colleague from UserList.
  4. The client checks the loaded internal threads for an existing INTERNAL thread matching the two users (vsmsId/vsmsId2).
  5. If a thread exists → open it directly (proceed to 9.4 to read/send).
  6. If none exists → the right pane shows a "Start Chat" prompt. On confirm, the client calls POST /chat-services/start-internal-chat with { receiverId }.
  7. The backend startInternalChat validates both users are in the same agency, prevents self-chat, and runs findExistingInternalThread to de-duplicate.
  8. If a thread already existed server-side, it is returned; otherwise a new chat_threads row is inserted (type='INTERNAL', vsmsId, vsmsId2, status='ACTIVE').
  9. The backend emits thread-creation-internal to the counterpart user's room; the recipient's client toasts and sets internalThreadsRefetch.
  10. The initiator's client opens the new thread; the staff member can now compose the first message (9.3).

9.2 Create and participate in a group chat (planned)

  1. A staff member clicks "New Group" and selects 2..N agency colleagues and a group name (planned UI).
  2. The client calls the group-create endpoint (to be defined) with participants + name; the backend creates a multi-participant internal group (extended chat_threads or GroupChatMessages-backed group), scoped to the agency.
  3. All selected participants receive a thread-creation-internal (group variant) event and see the new group in their list.
  4. Any participant opens the group and joins the per-group thread room for live delivery.
  5. A participant sends a message (with optional attachments); the backend persists it and fans out real-time delivery + per-recipient unread/notifications to all other participants.
  6. Read state is tracked per participant; the group shows who has read the latest message.
  7. The creator/admin may add or remove participants, rename, or archive the group.
⚠ Not yet built
This flow is the planned target. The current backend supports 1:1 internal threads only; group creation endpoints and multi-participant storage do not exist yet.

9.3 Send a message with an attachment

  1. In an open internal thread, the staff member types content and attaches up to 5 files.
  2. The client calls POST /chat-services/send-message/:threadUid as multipart (files[] + content).
  3. The backend authorizes the sender via isAuthorizedForThread (must be a thread participant); rejects otherwise.
  4. In a single transaction: insert a chat_messages row (threadId, vsmsSenderId, content), run processMessageFiles to persist each file to file_uploads (linked to message + thread) via the Azure Blob file pipeline, touch the thread updated_at, and create the recipient notification(s).
  5. On commit, the backend emits message-internal to the recipient's thread-<threadUid>-<userUid> room, inserts the INTERNAL_NEW_MESSAGE notification, and sends an FCM push to the recipient.
  6. The backend returns the message graph-fetched with its files; the sender's UI appends it optimistically/authoritatively.
  7. The recipient (if the thread is open) receives message-internal, appends the message, and can retrieve attachments via GET /chat-services/get-all-files/:threadUid.
  8. Edge case — recipient offline from the thread: no socket append, but the in-app notification + FCM push land, and the unread badge increments; the message appears on next thread load.
  9. Edge case — file rejected (size/type): the send fails with a field error and no partial chat_messages row is committed (transactional).

9.4 Real-time delivery & read state

  1. A recipient with the Chat page open has joined the thread room (joinThreadRoom(threadUid, userUid)) when the thread was opened.
  2. When the sender's message commits, message-internal is emitted to that room; the recipient's socket callback shows a toast and sets the specific-chat refetch flag, and the message is appended live (< 1 s target).
  3. If the recipient is not viewing the thread, the aggregate internal-chat unread count increments (Redux notification slice) and a contact/sidebar badge appears.
  4. When the recipient opens/reads the thread, the client calls PATCH /chat-services/read/:threadUid.
  5. The backend readAllMessagesInThread sets isRead=true on all the thread's messages for that user and calls markThreadNotificationsAsRead.
  6. The unread badge clears and the aggregate count decrements; the sender can see the messages are now read (read receipt).
  7. Resilience — missed socket event: because the client reconciles by refetching on socket callbacks (and React Query refetch-on-reconnect), a briefly-dropped socket does not lose messages; the next refetch restores correct state. On reconnect, the client rejoins user + thread rooms.

9.5 Presence indication

  1. On login, the VS360 client opens an authenticated socket and joins vs360-user-<uid>.
  2. The contact list reflects a colleague as online when they have an active connection and offline otherwise.
  3. On disconnect/reconnect, presence updates in near real time.
  4. Edge case — stale presence: presence fidelity depends on connection tracking; see Risks for the current-state caveat (presence may be approximate).

13. Non-Functional Requirements

  • Security & transport: All API traffic is authenticated (Bearer + refresh) and passes the Netlify edge secret; non-development responses are AES-encrypted end to end. Sockets require a JWT handshake. Attachments are stored in Azure Blob and served only to authorized participants.
  • Privacy (victim safety): Internal Chat frequently references case detail and safety concerns. Conversations are confined to the agency tenant; no victim or other-agency staff can read them. Access is permission-gated. Content should never be exposed in logs (the socket debug logger already suppresses PII for sensitive channels; the same discipline applies here).
  • Tenancy isolation: Every query is scoped by issuer.serviceCenterInfo.id; participant checks (isAuthorizedForThread) prevent cross-thread access. A staff member can only ever message within their own agency.
  • Performance: Real-time delivery target < 1 s p95 to online recipients; message history paginated; attachment listing separate from message history to keep payloads small.
  • Availability & resilience: Socket auto-reconnect (8 attempts, 2–10 s backoff) with room rejoin; client reconciles by refetch so a dropped socket never loses messages. Real-time emits fire only post-commit.
  • Auditability: Create/update operations across the platform write ActionLogs rows (action, referenceType, source/ip/url/context). Internal Chat message/thread operations should participate in this audit trail for compliance.
  • Rate limiting: Chat endpoints use rateLimiters.chatApi to protect against abuse/flooding.
  • Localization: UI strings are English (no app-wide i18n on VS360); the shared translate pipeline can localize message content on demand though staff-to-staff typically shares a language.
  • Accessibility: The chat UI (Mantine) should meet standard keyboard-navigation and contrast expectations; unread badges and presence must have non-color-only affordances.
  • Compliance: Because content may inform grant/PMT reporting context indirectly (coordination), retention and audit align with platform record-keeping policy; no separate PMT reporting derives from Internal Chat directly.
