2. Executive Summary

NECX serves victims and survivors of crime and the agencies, advocates, and caseworkers who help them. Victims rarely need a single service from a single agency; a survivor may need shelter from one provider, legal advocacy from a second, and financial compensation assistance from a third. The Referrals & Partnerships module — surfaced to staff as the My Network area of the VS360 web app — is how agencies build a trusted inter-agency network and hand a case off to a partner without the victim having to re-tell their story or re-submit intake.

This module delivers three tightly linked capabilities. First, an agency directory that lets staff discover other verified NECX agencies and invite non-member agencies by email. Second, partnerships: a bilateral, request/accept relationship between two agencies that, once accepted, unlocks a shared agency-to-agency chat thread and makes each agency eligible as a referral destination. Third, referrals: a consent-first workflow in which an agency owning a case refers the client to a partner agency for a specific service — but no case data crosses the agency boundary until the victim explicitly consents from their mobile app, at which point a sanitized snapshot of only the fields the sending agency chose to share is used to clone a new case into the partner agency.

The design centers on victim safety, consent, and data minimization. Sharing preferences default to sharing nothing; consent is time-boxed (two days) and revocable in effect through decline/resend history; only one active referral may exist per case at a time; and cross-agency isolation (CJIS-aligned) is enforced everywhere so an agency can never read another agency's case data except through the explicit, consented referral snapshot. Real-time socket notifications, email, and SMS keep both agencies informed at every state transition.

This PRD specifies the functional behavior, data model (AgencyPartnerships, AgencyReferrals, AgencyInvitations), API surface, RBAC, notifications, non-functional requirements, and end-to-end flows for the module, grounded in the current NECX backend and VS360 web implementation.

3. Product Overview & Vision

3.1 Problem Statement

Victim services are fragmented across many independent agencies. When one agency cannot fully serve a client, the handoff today is manual, insecure, and lossy: a phone call or email, re-collection of intake data at the receiving agency, and no shared record of who is helping the client with what. That fragmentation delays help, forces survivors to repeat trauma narratives, and creates uncontrolled copies of sensitive case data outside any consent framework.

3.2 Product Vision

My Network makes coordinated, consented, inter-agency care a first-class workflow. An advocate can find the right partner agency, establish a trusted connection, and refer a client for a specific service in minutes — with the victim in control of exactly what is shared and the receiving agency getting a ready-to-work case rather than a blank intake form. The network becomes the connective tissue of the NECX ecosystem: partnerships enable chat and referrals; referrals link cases across agency boundaries; and the resulting acceptance metrics feed agency dashboards and AI reporting.

3.3 Position in the NECX Ecosystem

  • VS360 web app — the primary surface. My Network (agency directory, partnerships, invitations, referral creation and management) and Agency Chat live here; all partnership and referral write operations are VS360-only.
  • Victim mobile app (Flutter) — the consent surface. The victim is the only actor who can approve or reject a referral, and they do so from the mobile app.
  • NECX Admin — owns agency lifecycle (create/verify/bulk-import agencies) that populates the directory; not a party to partnerships or referrals.
  • Unified backend (necx-backend_v2) — the single API hosting the referrals, partnerships, service-center, agency-services, and victim-services modules, Socket.IO real-time, BullMQ email, and the audit trail.
  • Reports / Dashboard — consume referral acceptance statistics (AgencyReferrals.getAgencyReferralStats) and accepted-partnership counts as inputs to agency KPIs and the AI report builder.

4. Goals, Objectives & Success Metrics

4.1 Goals & Objectives

  • Enable agencies to discover and connect with trusted partner agencies with minimal friction.
  • Make inter-agency case handoff consent-first, so no case data is shared without explicit victim approval.
  • Enforce strict data minimization: share only the specific case fields the sending agency selects, and nothing by default.
  • Guarantee a single, unambiguous active referral per case to prevent conflicting or duplicate handoffs.
  • Give the receiving agency a ready-to-work cloned case that preserves the client relationship without re-intake.
  • Keep both agencies informed in real time (socket) and via email/SMS at every partnership and referral transition.
  • Preserve a complete, compliance-grade audit trail of every partnership and referral action.

5. Scope

5.1 In Scope

  • Agency directory search and discovery (VS360 /web/service-centers), including the directory visibility rules.
  • Agency-to-agency email invitations for non-member agencies (agency-services), and the "Invitations Sent" list.
  • Partnership lifecycle: create/reactivate request, list, get one, accept, decline — with auto agency-chat creation on accept and socket/email/SMS notifications.
  • Pre-partnership and connected agency chat entry points (as they gate on partnership state).
  • Referral lifecycle: create referral against an accepted partnership, client consent decision (approve/reject) from mobile, consent-driven case clone + sanitized snapshot, update/resend, delete, toggle acceptingReferrals, referral options listing.
  • The single-active-referral-per-case rule and the two independent status axes (consent vs referral).
  • Referred-case acceptance on the partner (Agency B) side as it flips referralStatus (integration point with victim-services).
  • Partner assignment / eligibility (which agencies and services are selectable as referral targets).
  • Data model for AgencyPartnerships, AgencyReferrals, AgencyInvitations; endpoints; RBAC; notifications; NFRs; risks.

5.2 Out of Scope

  • Case/claim lifecycle mechanics inside a single agency (create, approve, reject, resolve, notes, updates) — owned by the Victim Services & Cases PRD; referenced here only at integration points.
  • Full chat/messaging feature specification (message pane, read receipts, attachments) — owned by the Chat & Inquiries PRD; only the auto-creation of the agency chat thread on partnership accept is specified here.
  • Shelter bed-transfer requests between partner agencies — a separate partner workflow owned by the Shelter Management PRD.
  • Converting an accepted email invitee into a provisioned agency (agency onboarding) and converting an invitee into VSMS staff — owned by NECX Admin / User Management.
  • PMT, grants, and AI report generation, except where they consume referral statistics.
  • A mobile router for partnerships (declared but has no registered routes) — not a delivered capability.

6.1 Persona Narratives

Dana, an advocate at Agency A (shelter provider). Dana is helping a survivor who now needs legal advocacy her agency doesn't offer. In My Network she searches the directory, finds a verified legal-services agency, and — if not already connected — sends a partnership request with a short note, or chats with them first. Once connected, she creates a referral for the client's case, choosing exactly which case details to share, and sends it. She watches for the consent and acceptance notifications.

Marcus, intake coordinator at Agency B (legal services). Marcus receives a partnership request notification and accepts it, which immediately opens a shared agency chat with Agency A. Later, once the client has consented, a new case appears in his queue pre-populated from the shared snapshot. He reviews and approves it as a normal service claim; that approval marks the referral accepted on Agency A's side.

The survivor (victim/client). Nothing about their case leaves Agency A until they open the referral on their mobile app and tap Approve. They can see the message from Agency A and reject if they prefer. If they do nothing, the request quietly expires after two days.

7. System Context & Architecture

7.1 Tech Stack

  • Backend: Node.js + Express (ESM), Objection.js ORM over Knex/PostgreSQL. knexSnakeCaseMappers converts snake_case columns to camelCase in service code. Modules in scope: src/apps/referrals, src/apps/partnerships, src/apps/service-center, src/apps/agency-services, with integration into src/apps/victim-services/service-claim.
  • Frontend (VS360 web): React + Vite + Mantine UI + TanStack Query + Redux. My Network lives at src/pages/MyNetwork/MyNetwork.jsx with agency-chat components under src/components/pages/AgencyChat/ and hooks in src/hooks/usePartnerships.js; services in src/services/partnerships.js, serviceCenters.js, agencyServices.js.
  • Mobile (consent surface): Flutter victim app calls the /mobile/partnerships/referrals/:uid/decision endpoint.

7.2 Backend Communication

  • Tri-router pattern: web routes are mounted under /web/... and guarded by allowOnly('VS360'); the single mobile referral route is under /mobile/... (auth-only, no context guard). API version prefix v2.
  • Mounts: partnerships /web/partnerships; referrals /web/partnerships/referrals and /mobile/partnerships/referrals; agency directory /web/service-centers; invitations /web/agency-services.
  • Response envelope: { status, msg|message, data }; the VS360 client reads payloads via response.data.data.
  • Rate limiting: rateLimiters.standard (~10/5s) on write routes.
  • Auth principal: req.issuer carries id, uid, type (APP/VSMS/NECX), roleInfo (with context + permission), and — for staff — serviceCenterInfo.{id,uid,name} identifying the agency.
  • Transactions: referral create and partnership accept/decline run inside withTransaction so audit logs and side effects commit atomically.

7.3 Real-Time & Notifications

  • Socket.IO via VS360NotificationService.emitPartnershipNotification. Delivery is to the recipient agency's users, filtered by service categories, in per-user rooms vs360-user-<uid>. The VS360 client listens for partnership-notification, shows a toast, and sets a Redux refetch flag.
  • Email + SMS via emailNotificationHelper.sendEmails / smsNotificationHelper.sendSMS to the service users of the recipient agency; agency email invitations use a BullMQ queue (SEND_EMAIL, type AGENCY_INVITATION, 3 attempts, exponential backoff).
  • No direct socket is emitted by the referrals module itself; app-facing notifications for the cloned/approved case are handled downstream by the service-claim / app notification services.

7.4 Tenancy & Isolation

Agency-scoped data is filtered by issuer.serviceCenterInfo.id; a VS360 user only ever sees or edits their own agency. Cross-agency case data is never readable directly — the only path across the boundary is the consented, sanitized referral snapshot. This CJIS-aligned isolation is enforced in service logic (e.g., resolveAgencyScopedClaim) and by ownership checks on every partnership/referral mutation.

8. Functional Requirements

8.1 Agency Directory & Discovery

Staff discover other agencies through the VS360 directory (GET /web/service-centers), rendered in My Network as a searchable, filterable grid excluding the caller's own agency. Search supports category, country, state, and city filters (draft vs applied). A single agency's detail is fetched via GET /web/service-centers/:uid for the profile modal used by partnership and chat actions.

Directory visibility rule: searchAgencies returns only agencies with status='ACTIVE' AND serviceType='VICTIM_SERVICE_PROVIDER' AND planId NOT NULL. Police stations, inactive agencies, and plan-less agencies never appear in search results, though a specific agency remains retrievable by direct :uid lookup.

FR-DIR-04The system shall return a single agency's full profile by uid for use in partnership and chat modals.Must

8.2 Agency Invitations (Email)

For agencies not yet on NECX, staff can send email invitations from My Network. The agency-services module (VS360-only) exposes exactly two endpoints. Sending validates the email list and message, resolves the sending agency from issuer.serviceCenterInfo, enqueues a BullMQ SEND_EMAIL job per address (type AGENCY_INVITATION), and persists an AgencyInvitations row per invitee with status='sent'. The "Invitations Sent" view lists invitations for the caller's agency.

FR-INV-02The system shall persist an AgencyInvitations row per invitee (status sent) attributed to the sending agency.Must
FR-INV-03Invitation emails shall be delivered asynchronously via the BullMQ email queue (3 attempts, exponential backoff).Must

ℹ Invitation ≠ onboarding
The agency-services module only sends and tracks invitations. Converting an invitee into a provisioned NECX agency, and converting an invitee contact into VSMS staff, are handled by NECX Admin / User Management and are out of scope here.

8.3 Partnerships — Create & Reactivate

A partnership is a bilateral relationship between two agencies. The requesting agency (the caller's agency) sends a request to a target agency identified by targetAgencyUid, with an optional message (≤1000 chars). The service rejects self-partnership, verifies both agencies exist, and checks for an existing mutual partnership in either direction. If a non-declined partnership already exists, it returns 202 (pending) or 200 (already accepted/other) rather than creating a duplicate. If a prior partnership was declined, it is reactivated in place — reusing the row, swapping requester/target roles, and resetting status to pending.

IDRequirementPriority
FR-PART-01Staff shall create a partnership request to another agency with an optional message (≤1000 chars).Must
FR-PART-02The system shall reject self-partnership (requesting = target) with 403.Must
FR-PART-03The system shall return 202 (existing pending) or 200 (already accepted) instead of creating a duplicate partnership.Must
FR-PART-04The system shall reactivate a previously declined partnership by reusing the row, swapping requester/target, and resetting to pending.Must
FR-PART-05On create/reactivate, the system shall notify the target agency via socket, email, and SMS.Must
FR-PART-06Create/reactivate shall be audited (PARTNERSHIP_REQUEST / PARTNERSHIP_REQUEST_REACTIVATED).Must

Endpoint: POST /web/partnerships → 201 {partnership} (or 202/200). Socket: emitPartnershipNotification(io, partnership, 'created') to the target agency. Side effects: for every service in the target agency, send EMAIL_TYPES.PARTNERSHIP email + SMS to that service's users.

8.4 Partnerships — List & Get

Staff list their agency's partnerships in both directions with optional status filter (pending|accepted|declined|inactive) and pagination (pageSize ≤ 50). A single partnership is retrievable by uid, returning 403 if the caller's agency is not one of the two parties. Both requestingAgency and targetAgency are eagerly loaded on every query.

IDRequirementPriority
FR-PART-07Staff shall list their agency's partnerships (both directions) with status filter and pagination (≤50/page).Must
FR-PART-08Staff shall retrieve a single partnership by uid; non-parties receive 403.Must
FR-PART-09Partnership responses shall include both agencies' profiles (auto-loaded).Must

Endpoints: GET /web/partnerships → {partnerships, pagination}; GET /web/partnerships/:uid → {partnership}.

8.5 Partnerships — Accept

Only the target agency of a pending request may accept it. On accept, the system sets status accepted, records lastActionByUser, and — critically — auto-creates a shared agency-to-agency chat thread if both agencies have users and no existing AGENCY_CHAT thread between them already exists (ChatThreads.findExistingAgencyThreadByAgencies). The new ChatThreads row is type='AGENCY_CHAT', status='ACTIVE', with vsmsId/vsmsId2 set to the first user of each agency. The requesting agency is then notified.

IDRequirementPriority
FR-PART-10Only the target agency shall accept a pending partnership (403 otherwise); status must be pending (400 otherwise).Must
FR-PART-11On accept, the system shall auto-create a shared AGENCY_CHAT thread between the two agencies if none exists.Must
FR-PART-12On accept, the system shall notify the requesting agency via socket, email, and SMS.Must
FR-PART-13Accept shall be audited (PARTNERSHIP_ACCEPTED) within a transaction.Must

Endpoint: POST /web/partnerships/:uid/accept (empty body) → 200 {partnership}. Socket: emitPartnershipNotification(...,'accepted') to the requesting agency.

8.6 Partnerships — Decline

Same guards as accept: only the target agency may decline a pending request. Status is set to declined, audited (PARTNERSHIP_DECLINED), and the requesting agency is notified (socket + email/SMS). A declined partnership is not permanent — either side may later reactivate it via create (with role swap, §8.3).

IDRequirementPriority

FR-PART-15On decline, the system shall notify the requesting agency and audit PARTNERSHIP_DECLINED.Must
FR-PART-16A declined partnership shall remain reactivatable by either agency.Must

Endpoint: POST /web/partnerships/:uid/decline (empty body) → 200 {partnership}.

8.7 Agency Chat Gating (Partnership-Dependent)

Agency-to-agency chat is a partnership-gated capability. The AgencyChatTab (in the agency profile modal) is enabled only once Connected; starting it calls POST /chat-services/start-agency-chat and handles: 200 = thread ready; 201 with needsPartnership = no partnership; 201/202 = partnership request pending. A separate ReferralChatModal allows pre-partnership messaging via POST /chat-services/start-pre-partnership-chat, so staff can message an agency before sending a request; those messages persist after connecting.

8.8 Referrals — Create

An agency owning an existing case refers the client to a partner agency for a specific service. Create requires requestingAgencyUid, partnershipUid, originalCaseUid, targetServiceId, an optional message, and the six canShare* booleans. The service validates the service center and issuer agency, loads the partnership (issuer must be one of the two agencies; partnership status must be accepted), validates the original case, and enforces the single-active-referral guard: if any non-terminal referral already exists for the case, it returns 409 Conflict with the active referral summary. On success it inserts an agency_referrals row with consentStatus='pending', referralStatus='pending', referredCaseId=null, consentExpiresAt=now+2 days, and metadata carrying the sharing flags + referral message.

IDRequirementPriority
FR-REF-01Staff shall create a referral only against an accepted partnership their agency is party to (403/400 otherwise).Must
FR-REF-02The system shall enforce a single active (non-terminal) referral per case, returning 409 with the active referral if violated.Must
FR-REF-03New referrals shall start consentStatus=pending, referralStatus=pending, referredCaseId=null, with a 2-day consent expiry.Must
FR-REF-04Sharing preferences shall default to all-false; staff explicitly opt into each of the six canShare* fields.Must
FR-REF-05Referral creation shall be audited (AGENCY_REFERRAL_CREATED).Must

Endpoint: POST /web/partnerships/referrals → 201 { referral, referredCase: null }. The six flags: canShareCaseNotes, canShareCaseFiles, canShareCaseChat, canShareCaseUpdates, canShareVictimAnswers, canSharePmtDemographics.

8.9 Referrals — Client Consent Decision (Mobile)

The victim/client is the sole approver. The mobile endpoint requires issuer.type='APP' (403 otherwise) and loads the referral with its partnership context. Reject sets consentStatus='denied' (appending denialHistory), audits REFERRAL_CONSENT_DENIED, and makes the referral terminal. Approve sets consentStatus='granted', builds a sanitized case-data snapshot from only the enabled canShare* fields, resolves the destination agency by validating the target Service's serviceCenterId belongs to one of the partnership's two agencies, clones the case into the target agency, sets referredCaseId to the new case, and leaves referralStatus='pending' (the receiving agency has not accepted yet). Audit REFERRAL_CONSENT_GRANTED.

FR-REF-07Reject shall set consentStatus=denied, record denial history, and terminate the referral.Must
FR-REF-08Approve shall set consentStatus=granted and build a snapshot containing only fields whose canShare* flag is true.Must

Snapshot (buildCaseDataSnapshot) and clone (§9.3 detail): the snapshot loads the claim with victimInfo, notes, files, updates, claimQuestions.questionInfo, pmtReportInfo, claimThreads and copies sanitized subsets per flag — notes, files, published updates only, chat threads, victim questionnaire answers, and PMT demographics. The clone inserts a new service_claims row (uid=CL<yy><month><uuid6>, status='pending', same victimId, serviceId=targetService.id, copied statementFromVictim), a mirrored pmt_report row (age/gender/race/classification from victimInfo), and a baseline service_claim_stats row.

8.10 Referrals — Partner Acceptance (Integration Point)

The receiving agency (Agency B) does not act in the referrals module. It approves or rejects the cloned service claim in the normal case workflow (victim-services). On approve, the service-claim service patches referralStatus='accepted' where referredCaseId matches the claim; on reject, referralStatus='declined'. This is what flips a consent-granted referral to its terminal accepted/declined state.

8.11 Referrals — Update / Resend

If consentStatus='denied' and resend:true, the referral resets to pending with a new consentExpiresAt, appends to denialHistory, and audits AGENCY_REFERRAL_RESENT. Otherwise only pending referrals may be edited (409 if granted/denied); an edit requires at least one sharing flag and audits AGENCY_REFERRAL_UPDATED.

9. Detailed User & System Flows

9.1 Flow — Find an agency and send a partnership request

  1. Staff at Agency A open My Network in VS360; the directory grid loads via GET /web/service-centers, excluding Agency A.
  2. Staff apply filters (category, country, state, city) and/or a search term to narrow results (draft filters, then Apply).
  3. Staff open a candidate agency's profile modal (GET /web/service-centers/:uid); the header shows partnership status (Your Agency / Connected Partner / Request Pending / Not connected).
  4. If desired, staff click Chat to start a pre-partnership conversation (POST /chat-services/start-pre-partnership-chat) before committing.
  5. Staff click Request to Connect, enter an optional message (≤1000 chars), and submit POST /web/partnerships with targetAgencyUid and message.
  6. Backend guards: reject self-partnership (403); verify both agencies exist (404); check getMutualPartnership.
  7. Branch — existing non-declined partnership: return 202 (pending exists) or 200 (already accepted); UI shows the current status, no duplicate created.
  8. Branch — previously declined: reactivateDeclinedPartnership reuses the row, swaps requester/target roles, resets to pending; audit PARTNERSHIP_REQUEST_REACTIVATED.
  9. Branch — none exists: createPartnership with status pending; audit PARTNERSHIP_REQUEST.
  10. Backend emits emitPartnershipNotification(io, partnership, 'created') to Agency B's users (title "New Partnership Request") and sends email + SMS to Agency B's service users.
  11. Agency A's UI flips the agency status to Request Pending (optimistic cache update; ["partnerships"] invalidated). Response 201 {partnership}.

9.2 Flow — Accept a partnership (and auto-create agency chat)

  1. Agency B receives a partnership-notification socket toast and a notification refetch; the request also appears in email/SMS.
  2. A staff member at Agency B opens the requesting agency's profile modal and reviews the request and any message.
  3. Staff click Accept (confirm modal) → POST /web/partnerships/:uid/accept (empty body).
  4. Backend verifies the caller's agency is the targetAgencyUid (403 otherwise) and status is pending (400 otherwise).
  5. updatePartnershipStatus(uid,'accepted',{userUid}); audit PARTNERSHIP_ACCEPTED — all transactional.
  6. Auto-chat: if both agencies have users and no existing AGENCY_CHAT thread between them, insert a ChatThreads row (type='AGENCY_CHAT', status='ACTIVE', vsmsId/vsmsId2 = first user of each agency).
  7. Backend emits emitPartnershipNotification(...,'accepted') to Agency A and sends email + SMS to Agency A's service users.
  8. Both agencies now see status Connected Partner; the Chat tab becomes enabled; each agency now appears as a potential referral target for the other (subject to acceptingReferrals). Response 200 {partnership}.

9.3 Flow — Refer a case/client to a partner agency, end-to-end (with consent)

  1. At Agency A, staff open a case (service claim) they own, or start from the referral network in My Network.
  2. Staff fetch referral options via GET /web/partnerships/referrals/:serviceCenterUid/referral-options (optionally filtered by categoryIds/search); only accepted-partnership agencies where both agencies have acceptingReferrals=true and the target has ≥1 service appear.
  3. Staff select the target partner agency and a specific target service (targetServiceId) — this is the partner assignment.
  4. Staff choose exactly which case data to share by enabling any of the six canShare* flags (all default off) and write an optional message to the client.
  5. Staff submit POST /web/partnerships/referrals with requestingAgencyUid, partnershipUid, originalCaseUid, targetServiceId, message?, and the flags.
  6. Backend validates the agency/issuer, loads the partnership (issuer must be a party; status must be accepted), and validates the original case.
  7. Single-active-referral guard: checkActiveReferrals(originalCaseUid) — if any non-terminal referral exists, return 409 Conflict with the active referral summary; staff must resolve/delete it first.
  8. Backend inserts the agency_referrals row (consentStatus=pending, referralStatus=pending, referredCaseId=null, consentExpiresAt=now+2 days, metadata with flags + message); audit AGENCY_REFERRAL_CREATED; return 201 { referral, referredCase: null }. No case data has crossed the boundary yet.
  9. The client sees a pending referral needing consent in their mobile app (surfaced via getReferralsForSingleClaim for APP issuers).
  10. Consent decision — Approve (POST /mobile/partnerships/referrals/:uid/decision, action='approve', issuer.type='APP'):

a. updateConsentStatus('granted'). b. buildCaseDataSnapshot runs only if ≥1 canShare* flag is true, copying sanitized subsets (notes, files, published updates only, chat threads, victim answers, PMT demographics) into metadata.caseSnapshot. c. Resolve destination: load target Service by targetServiceId; verify its serviceCenterId belongs to one of the partnership's two agencies (else throw). d. Clone: insert a new service_claims row into Agency B (uid=CL..., status='pending', same victimId, serviceId=targetService.id, copied statementFromVictim); insert a mirrored pmt_report row and a baseline service_claim_stats row. e. Patch referral referredCaseId=newClaim.uid; referralStatus stays pending. f. Audit REFERRAL_CONSENT_GRANTED; return 200 { referralUid, newClaim: { ...isReferred:true } }.

  1. Consent decision — Reject (action='reject'): updateConsentStatus('denied') (appends denialHistory); audit REFERRAL_CONSENT_DENIED; referral is now terminal; no clone occurs.
  2. Consent lapse: if the client takes no action before consentExpiresAt (now + 2 days), the referral is effectively stale/terminal pending; Agency A may resend (§9.5).

9.4 Flow — Partner accepts and a linked case is created

  1. At Agency B, the newly cloned case appears in the case queue as a pending service claim (flagged isReferred).
  2. A caseworker reviews the case; the shared snapshot (per Agency A's canShare* selections) provides context without re-intake.
  3. Caseworker approves the claim in the normal victim-services workflow (PATCH .../service-claims/approve/:uid).
  4. Approve side effects: create a published "Approved" update; open a SERVICE chat thread (victim ↔ Agency B caseworker); set stats approved_by/at; notify the victim (victim_notifications + FCM + socket).
  5. Referral linkage: the service-claim service patches referralStatus='accepted' where referredCaseId = this claim's uid — the referral is now terminal accepted.
  6. Alternative — Agency B rejects the claim (PATCH .../reject/:uid): stats rejected_by/at; the mirrored PMT row is deleted; the linked referral is patched to referralStatus='declined' (terminal); the victim is notified.
  7. Agency A sees the outcome reflected in its case list / referral segment and in referral statistics.

9.5 Flow — Update / resend a denied referral

  1. Agency A staff open a referral whose consentStatus='denied'.
  2. Staff optionally revise the message and click Resend → PATCH /web/partnerships/referrals/:uid with resend:true and metadata.resendMessage?.
  3. Backend confirms the caller is the requesting agency, resets consentStatus='pending', sets a new consentExpiresAt, appends to denialHistory; audit AGENCY_REFERRAL_RESENT.
  4. The client again sees a pending referral needing consent; flow rejoins §9.3 step 10.
  5. Edit-only branch (no resend): if the referral is still pending, staff may change sharing flags (≥1 required); audit AGENCY_REFERRAL_UPDATED. Granted/denied referrals cannot be edited (409).

9.6 Flow — Decline a partnership

  1. Agency B receives the partnership request (socket + email/SMS).
  2. Staff open the requesting agency's profile and click Decline (confirm modal) → POST /web/partnerships/:uid/decline.
  3. Backend verifies the caller's agency is the targetAgencyUid (403) and status is pending (400); sets declined; audit PARTNERSHIP_DECLINED.
  4. Backend emits emitPartnershipNotification(...,'declined') to Agency A plus email/SMS.
  5. Agency A sees status revert to "Not connected." Either agency may later re-request, which reactivates the declined row with swapped roles (§9.1 step 8).

9.7 Flow — Invite a non-member agency by email

  1. Agency A staff click Send Invitation in My Network.
  2. Staff enter one or more email addresses (comma/newline separated, validated) and a message.
  3. Submit POST /web/agency-services/send-invitations (emails[], message).
  4. Backend validates, resolves the sending agency (requires serviceCenterInfo), and per email enqueues a BullMQ SEND_EMAIL job (type AGENCY_INVITATION, 3 attempts, exponential backoff) and inserts an AgencyInvitations row (status='sent').
  5. Staff open Invitations Sent (GET /web/agency-services/invitations, paginated/searchable) to track status.
  6. (Out of scope) When an invited agency later joins NECX and is provisioned by Admin, it becomes discoverable in the directory and available for partnership.

9.8 Flow — Toggle inbound referral availability

  1. An Agency B admin opens their agency's referral-status setting.
  2. They flip acceptingReferrals → PATCH /web/partnerships/referrals/:serviceCenterUid/toggle-referrals.
  3. Backend verifies the caller belongs to and matches serviceCenterUid, flips service_centers.acceptingReferrals; audit SERVICE_CENTER_REFERRALS_TOGGLED.
  4. Effect: when off, Agency B no longer appears in any partner's getReferralOptions (both agencies must be accepting), so no new referrals can target it.

10.1 Status Semantics

Two independent axes on AgencyReferrals: consentStatus (the client's decision) and referralStatus (the receiving agency's decision on the cloned case). A referral is terminal when consentStatus='denied', OR (consentStatus='granted' AND referralStatus ∈ {accepted, declined}). It is active otherwise (consent pending; or consent granted + referral pending). The single-active-referral rule keys off this active/terminal distinction.

Default sharing preferences (DEFAULT_SHARING_PREFERENCES) are all false: canShareCaseNotes, canShareCaseFiles, canShareCaseChat, canShareCaseUpdates, canShareVictimAnswers, canSharePmtDemographics.

13. Non-Functional Requirements

13.1 Consent & Victim Safety

  • Consent-first: No case data crosses the agency boundary until the victim explicitly approves the referral from their own mobile app; only an APP issuer can decide.
  • Time-boxed consent: consentExpiresAt defaults to now + 2 days; unactioned referrals lapse rather than remaining indefinitely open.
  • Denial history: Every denial and resend is recorded in metadata.denialHistory, giving an auditable record of the client's decisions.
  • Client control of message: The referral message the client sees is stored and re-shown on resend, so the client always has context.

13.2 Data Sharing Minimization

  • Opt-in per field: All six canShare* flags default to false; the sending agency must explicitly enable each category.
  • Snapshot sanitization: buildCaseDataSnapshot copies only the enabled categories and only sanitized subsets — notably published updates only (never unpublished caseworker-only updates). If no flag is enabled, no snapshot is built at all.
  • Point-in-time snapshot: The shared data is a snapshot captured at consent, not a live feed; the receiving agency does not gain ongoing read access to the source case.
  • No direct cross-agency reads: CJIS-aligned isolation ensures an agency can never query another agency's cases except via the consented clone.

13.3 Security & Access Control

  • All partnership/referral write routes are VS360-context-gated and further constrained by ownership (party/requesting/case-owning agency).
  • The mobile consent route is restricted to issuer.type='APP' and the client's own referral.
  • Response encryption (AES) is applied by the platform's encryption middleware; clients decrypt.
  • Destination validation: the target service's serviceCenterId must belong to the partnership's two agencies before any clone, preventing misdirected shares.

13.4 Integrity & Consistency

  • Single active referral per case prevents conflicting or duplicate handoffs (409 on violation).
  • Referral create and partnership accept/decline run inside withTransaction, so the row change, audit log, and clone/chat side effects are atomic.
  • Soft-delete for referrals preserves history and auditability.

13.5 Performance & Availability

  • Write routes are protected by rateLimiters.standard (~10/5s).
  • Partnership listing is paginated (pageSize ≤ 50); directory search is paginated.
  • Notification calls (socket/email/SMS) are wrapped defensively — a delivery failure is logged and never breaks the main transaction.
  • Email invitations are queued (BullMQ, 3 attempts, exponential backoff) to decouple delivery from the request.

13.6 Auditability & Compliance

  • Every partnership and referral action writes an ActionLogs row: PARTNERSHIP_REQUEST, PARTNERSHIP_REQUEST_REACTIVATED, PARTNERSHIP_ACCEPTED, PARTNERSHIP_DECLINED, AGENCY_REFERRAL_CREATED, REFERRAL_CONSENT_GRANTED, REFERRAL_CONSENT_DENIED, AGENCY_REFERRAL_RESENT, AGENCY_REFERRAL_UPDATED, AGENCY_REFERRAL_DELETED, SERVICE_CENTER_REFERRALS_TOGGLED.
  • Audit descriptions capture source, ip, url, and context for compliance.

13.7 Localization & Accessibility

  • The VS360 UI follows the platform's Mantine-based accessibility conventions; client-facing referral messages are free-text authored by staff.
  • Platform static translations cover mobile-app UI strings shown around the consent decision.
