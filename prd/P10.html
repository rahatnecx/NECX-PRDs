2. Executive Summary

Agency Chat is NECX's secure, real-time messaging channel between agency staff (advocates / caseworkers, referred to internally as VSMS users) and the victims/survivors they serve. It is the human, one-to-one conversational surface that wraps a victim's journey through a service — from the first inquiry about a published service, through an approved case (service claim), to case resolution. Where NECX's AI assistant provides automated triage, Agency Chat is the channel where a real advocate and a real victim exchange words, documents, and reassurance in real time.

The feature serves two audiences from a single backend. On the victim side, the NECX mobile app exposes an inbox of conversations tied to the services and cases a survivor is engaged with. On the agency side, the VS360 web dashboard exposes the same conversations to the assigned advocate and, where permitted, the broader agency team, embedded directly in the case and inquiry workspaces. Messages, attachments, and read state are shared through a unified Express backend, delivered instantly over Socket.IO, and backed by durable notifications (in-app, push/FCM, and — for high-signal events — email and SMS).

This matters because timeliness and continuity are safety-critical for victim services. A survivor reaching out about a shelter bed, a protective order, or a crisis cannot be left waiting, and an advocate must be able to pick up the thread of a case without losing history. Agency Chat binds each conversation to a specific case/claim so that context, attachments, and accountability travel together, and so that a conversation opens automatically when a case is approved and is archived automatically when the case is resolved.

Above all, Agency Chat is designed under NECX's mission constraints: the safety, privacy, and dignity of victims. Conversations are access-scoped to the assigned advocate and their agency, content can be translated into the recipient's preferred language, and the victim app layers on additional protections (PIN/biometric lock, an emergency-exit control, encrypted transport) so a sensitive conversation stays private on a shared or monitored device.

3. Product Overview & Vision

3.1 Problem Statement

Victim services are relationship-driven and time-sensitive, yet the conversation between a survivor and their advocate is easily fragmented across phone calls, personal email, SMS, and memory. Without a purpose-built channel, context is lost between the intake question and the approved case, attachments live in inboxes outside any case record, and no one can prove a message was seen. Generic consumer messaging tools also fail the privacy bar for victim work: they are not scoped to a case, not access-controlled to the assigned advocate, and not safe on a device that an abuser may share or monitor.

3.2 Product Vision

Agency Chat is the single, case-anchored conversation channel between a victim and the agency helping them. Every message belongs to a thread, every thread belongs to a service or case, and the thread's lifecycle mirrors the case's lifecycle — it opens when help is approved and archives when help is complete. Advocates get continuity and accountability; victims get a private, translated, always-current line to the person helping them.

3.3 Where This Sits in the NECX Ecosystem

  • Victim App (Flutter mobile) — the survivor's inbox: starts inquiries, reads and sends case messages, receives push notifications, and enforces device-level safety (PIN, emergency exit).
  • VS360 Web (React SPA) — the advocate's workspace: the shared message pane inside Inquiries Management and Cases (Claims) Management where staff triage inquiries and correspond with victims on active cases.
  • Unified Backend (Express + Objection/Knex over PostgreSQL, Socket.IO, Redis) — the chat module (chat_threads, chat_messages), thread creation on case approval (victim-services module), notification fan-out, translation, and file storage.
  • NECX Admin — platform administration; not a direct participant in Agency Chat but governs plans, roles, and the permission templates that gate it.
  • Adjacent modules — Agency Chat is one of four thread types in the shared chat backbone (SERVICE, INQUIRY, INTERNAL staff↔staff, AGENCY_CHAT agency↔agency). This PRD covers the two victim-facing types (SERVICE, INQUIRY); the staff-only types are out of scope (see §5.2).

4. Goals, Objectives & Success Metrics

4.1 Goals

  • Provide a real-time, one-to-one messaging channel between a victim and their assigned advocate that is anchored to a specific service/case.
  • Automatically open a durable SERVICE conversation the moment a case (service claim) is approved, and automatically archive it when the case is resolved — no manual thread management.
  • Deliver messages, attachments, and read state instantly to whichever clients are open (web + mobile) via Socket.IO, with reliable fallback notifications (in-app, push, email/SMS) when they are not.
  • Preserve full, auditable conversation history and attachments as part of the case record.
  • Remove language as a barrier by translating message content into the recipient's preferred language.
  • Enforce victim safety and privacy at the message, thread, transport, and device layers.

5. Scope

5.1 In Scope

  • Victim-facing chat thread types: SERVICE (advocate ↔ victim on an approved case/claim) and INQUIRY (victim ↔ agency intake conversation about a published service).
  • Automatic SERVICE thread creation on case approval and thread archival on case resolution.
  • Message send/receive with text content and up to 5 file attachments per message.
  • Attachment/media upload, storage, and per-thread file listing.
  • Read receipts (mark-all-read) and per-user, per-type unread counts / badges.
  • Real-time delivery via Socket.IO thread rooms, plus durable in-app, push (FCM), email, and SMS notifications.
  • Message translation into the recipient's preferred language.
  • RBAC scoping of chat access to the assigned advocate and their agency.
  • Inquiry lifecycle triage (approve / reject / resolve) as it relates to opening and closing conversations.

5.2 Out of Scope

  • INTERNAL chat (staff ↔ staff 1:1 within an agency) and AGENCY_CHAT / pre-partnership chat (staff ↔ staff across agencies). These share the same backend and message pane but are advocate-only channels; note the naming collision described in §16 and §15.
  • The NECX AI assistant chat (a separate REST-based guidance chatbot, not human messaging).
  • Community group chats and post/comment feeds (covered by the Communities PRD).
  • Voice/video calling and the Twilio hotline (covered by the Hotline PRD).
  • Typing indicators and rich online/offline presence (not implemented today; see §15 Open Questions).
  • Message editing/deletion/recall and end-to-end (client-side) encryption (transport encryption only today).
  • Case notes and victim-facing case updates (separate case-record surfaces, not chat messages).

7. System Context & Architecture

7.1 Tech Stack

  • Backend: Node.js + Express (ESM), Objection/Knex over PostgreSQL, Socket.IO for real-time, Redis for scaling/pub-sub and caching, BullMQ for background jobs, Azure Blob Storage for attachments, FCM for push, Twilio for SMS, and an AI/translation provider layer. Chat lives in src/apps/chat/chat-{routes,controllers,services}.js; thread creation on approval lives in the victim-services module.
  • VS360 Web: React 18 + Vite SPA. A single Socket.IO connection in SocketContext.jsx; thread rooms joined via socketRoomManager; event names in SocketEvents.js; chat calls in services/chat.js and services/threads.js; the shared message pane is ActiveInquiry.jsx.
  • Victim App: Flutter. SocketService singleton (socket_service.dart); chat via InquiryChatController / InboxController over VictimService chat-services endpoints.

7.2 How Clients Talk to the Backend

All endpoints are mounted under the global /v2 API prefix. Web chat routes live under /web/chat-services/* (guarded by allowOnly('VS360')); victim routes under /mobile/chat-services/*. Requests carry a JWT access token; responses use the standard { status, msg, data } envelope and may be AES-encrypted (clients decrypt). Chat routes run under the rateLimiters.chatApi bucket.

7.3 Real-Time & Notifications

Socket.IO is initialized in src/utils/socket.js and authenticated by socket-middelware.js using the JWT passed in the connection auth handshake ({ token, refreshToken }). The mobile client additionally sends the app-secret header (x-mobile-app-secret / X-Netlify-Secret). Clients join rooms via join-room / leave-room emits. Per-thread delivery uses room thread-<threadUid>-<userUid>; staff also join vs360-user-<uid> and service-center-<id>; victims join app-notification-room. Every notable event follows the same fan-out: a DB notification row (service_center_notifications for staff, victim_notifications for victims, with additionalData carrying threadUid) + a Socket.IO emit to the target room + an FCM push + (for select events) email/SMS. Notification calls are wrapped defensively — failures are logged, never breaking the message transaction.

7.4 Tenancy

NECX is multi-tenant by agency (service center). Staff visibility is scoped by issuer.serviceCenterInfo.id and by the staff member's assigned service categories; victim visibility is scoped by issuer.id. SERVICE and INQUIRY threads carry both a victimId and the agency staff participants (vsmsId/vsmsId2) plus the originating serviceId, so tenant scope is derivable from the thread itself.

8. Functional Requirements

8.1 Chat Threads

A chat thread is the container for a conversation. The chat_threads table supports four type values; Agency Chat uses SERVICE (advocate ↔ victim on an approved case) and INQUIRY (victim ↔ agency intake). A thread carries victimId (→ app_users), vsmsId and vsmsId2 (→ users, the staff participant(s)), serviceId (→ services), a status (ACTIVE / ARCHIVED), and timestamps. INQUIRY threads additionally have an inquiry_lifecycle row (pending / approved / rejected / resolved).

Threads are surfaced through type-specific query helpers: getInquiryThreadsForVSMSUser (filtered by the staff member's service categories + service center), getThreadsForVSMSUser, and existence checks used for dedup. Victims retrieve all their threads via get-all-threads and open a specific one by uid.

8.2 Messages

Messages are rows in chat_messages: uid, threadId, exactly one sender key (victimSenderId → app_users or vsmsSenderId → users), content, translatedContent, isRead, and timestamps. Sending is authorized against thread participation (isAuthorizedForThread), the sender/receiver are resolved by thread type, content is translated to the receiver's preferred language, and — within a single transaction — the message is inserted, attachments are persisted, the thread is touched (updated last message), and notification rows are created. Socket + FCM (and email/SMS for select types) fire after commit; the created message is returned graph-fetched with its files.

Staff pre-join their user and service-center rooms on connect; victims join app-notification-room. Clients auto-reconnect and re-join rooms on reconnect.

9. Detailed User & System Flows

9.1 A SERVICE thread opens when a case is approved

  1. A victim's service claim exists in pending (victim-initiated) — or is created and auto-approved on the advocate path (shouldCreateUser).
  2. An advocate opens the case in VS360 and approves it: PATCH /web/service-claims/approve/:uid.
  3. The backend transitions the claim pending → approved and writes service_claim_stats.approved_by/at.
  4. The backend inserts a new chat_threads row with type = SERVICE, victimId = the claim's victim, vsmsId = the approving caseworker, serviceId = the claim's service, status = ACTIVE.
  5. The backend publishes a case update titled "Approved".
  6. The backend notifies the victim: inserts a victim_notifications row (SERVICE_CLAIM_APPROVED), sends FCM, and emits socket service-claim-action-for-<victimUid> to app-notification-room.
  7. If the claim originated from a partner referral, the referral is marked accepted.
  8. The victim app receives the notification; the new case conversation appears in the inbox. The advocate sees the conversation attached to the case workspace (case detail returns the latest thread).
  9. Both parties may now exchange messages (see §9.2). No manual "start chat" step is required.

9.2 Advocate and victim exchange messages in real time

  1. The advocate opens the case conversation in VS360; the client joins socket room thread-<threadUid>-<staffUid>.
  2. The victim opens the same conversation in the app; the client joins thread-<threadUid>-<victimUid> and listens on the new-message-* stream.
  3. The advocate types a message and calls POST /web/chat-services/send-message/:threadUid with content.
  4. The backend authorizes the sender via isAuthorizedForThread, resolves receiver = victim (SERVICE thread), and translates content into the victim's preferredLanguage → translatedContent.
  5. In one transaction the backend inserts the chat_messages row (vsmsSenderId = advocate), touches the thread's last-message pointer, and creates a victim_notifications (SERVICE_NEW_MESSAGE, additionalData.threadUid) row.
  6. After commit the backend emits message-service to the victim's thread room and pushes FCM.
  7. The victim app receives the socket event, appends the message live (showing translatedContent), and — if the app was backgrounded — also shows the push.
  8. The victim replies via POST /mobile/chat-services/send-message/:threadUid; the mirror path resolves receiver = advocate, translates into the advocate's language, creates a service_center_notifications row, emits message-service to the advocate's thread room, and pushes to staff.
  9. Each client optimistically renders its own outgoing bubble and reconciles it with the returned graph-fetched server message.

9.3 Sending an attachment / media

  1. The sender attaches up to 5 files and submits send-message/:threadUid as multipart/form-data (content + files[]).
  2. Multer (upload.array('files', 5)) parses the parts; the file service writes each buffer to blob storage and returns URLs.
  3. Within the send transaction, the message is inserted and each file is recorded in file_uploads linked to the message and thread (processMessageFiles).
  4. The message is returned graph-fetched with its files; the socket message-service / message-inquiry payload carries them so the peer renders attachments inline without a refetch.
  5. Either party can later browse all thread attachments via GET /get-all-files/:threadUid.
  6. Edge case: if an upload fails, the transaction does not commit a partial message; the client surfaces a retryable error.

9.4 Marking a thread read and unseen counts

  1. When a participant opens a thread, the client calls PATCH /{web,mobile}/chat-services/read/:threadUid.
  2. The backend sets isRead = true on all messages in that thread and clears the participant's associated thread notifications (markThreadNotificationsAsRead).
  3. Unread badges (sidebar "Chat" indicator on web; inbox counts on mobile) recompute from per-type unread counts on chat_messages and from the notification list.
  4. Edge case: messages that arrive while the thread is open are delivered by socket and are effectively read; the client may re-issue read to keep counts precise.

9.5 Archiving on case resolution

  1. When the case work is complete, the advocate resolves the case: PATCH /web/service-claims/resolve/:uid (approved→resolved), passing pmtValues and the threadUid.
  2. The backend transitions the claim approved → resolved, writes service_claim_stats.resolved_by/at, and publishes a "Resolved" case update.
  3. The backend sets the SERVICE thread status = ARCHIVED.
  4. The backend notifies the victim of resolution.
  5. The archived conversation and its attachments remain readable as part of the case record (history is preserved, not deleted); it no longer appears as an active conversation.

9.6 Inquiry intake → approval (branch that leads into a case)

  1. From the app, the victim starts an inquiry about a service: POST /mobile/chat-services/start-inquiry { inquiryData: { serviceCenterId, serviceId } }.
  2. The backend deduplicates on an existing active INQUIRY for (victimId, serviceId); otherwise it creates the thread (type = INQUIRY) and an inquiry_lifecycle row (pending).
  3. The backend notifies the agency's staff whose service categories match the service: socket thread-creation-inquiry, plus email and SMS to the agency.
  4. Victim and staff exchange messages on the inquiry thread (message-inquiry / INQUIRY_NEW_MESSAGE), same mechanics as §9.2.
  5. Staff approve the inquiry (PATCH /inquiry/approve/:id): lifecycle → approved, vsmsId assigned to the actor, messages marked read, victim notified (FCM INQUIRY_APPROVED, socket inquiry-approve-for-<victimUid>), action logged.
  6. The approved inquiry moves the victim toward a service claim; when that claim is approved, the case-bound SERVICE thread opens (§9.1).
  7. Branch — reject: PATCH /inquiry/reject/:id sets lifecycle rejected and archives the thread.
  8. Branch — resolve: PATCH /inquiry/resolve/:id closes the inquiry.

9.7 Message translation

  1. On send, the backend resolves the receiver and their preferredLanguage.
  2. translateMessageContent produces translatedContent; both original content and translatedContent are stored on the message.
  3. The receiving client renders translatedContent when the receiver's language differs from the sender's; the on-demand /mobile/tool/translate endpoint (and the web translate service) support additional translation as needed.

9.8 Reconnection, offline, and safety edge cases

  1. Dropped socket → the client auto-reconnects (websocket-first, polling fallback) and re-joins all thread/user rooms; any messages missed while disconnected are retrieved via get-all-chats and covered by the durable notification rows.
  2. Recipient offline → no socket delivery, but the DB notification + FCM push ensure the message is seen on next open; tapping the push deep-links to the thread.
  3. Non-participant access → get-thread, get-all-chats, and send-message reject unauthorized principals (participant check).
  4. Victim safety on device → the app enforces PIN/biometric lock and offers an emergency-exit control; sensitive conversations require re-unlock after inactivity (session lock 423 / lockout 403).

13. Non-Functional Requirements

13.1 Security

  • Transport is TLS; Socket.IO connections are authenticated by JWT in the handshake auth payload (not query strings); the mobile client additionally presents the app-secret header (x-mobile-app-secret / X-Netlify-Secret).
  • API responses may be AES-encrypted end-to-server with client-side decryption.
  • Every thread access and message send is authorized against thread participation and agency tenancy; non-participants are rejected.

13.2 Privacy & Victim Safety

  • Conversations are strictly scoped: a victim sees only their own threads; staff see only their agency's threads within their permission scope.
  • The victim app enforces a second-factor PIN and optional biometric lock, an always-available emergency-exit control to leave the app quickly, and session lock/lockout after inactivity — protecting a conversation on a shared or monitored device.
  • Attachments (which may contain sensitive documents/images) inherit the same participant-scoped access and are stored in private blob storage.
  • Confidential-location and referral-consent norms from adjacent modules apply; referral chats are gated on explicit consent.
  • Notification payloads keep sensitive content minimal; PII is suppressed from client-side debug logging.

13.3 Performance

  • Real-time message delivery to an open peer within 2 s (p95) after commit.
  • Message history is paginated (default page size 20 on mobile) to bound payloads.
  • The send path performs message insert, file attach, thread touch, and notification creation in a single transaction to guarantee consistency without extra round-trips.

13.4 Availability & Reliability

  • Socket.IO auto-reconnects (websocket-first with polling fallback) and re-joins rooms; durable DB notifications + FCM guarantee eventual delivery when a client is offline.
  • Notification side effects are wrapped defensively so a notification failure never rolls back a committed message.
  • Redis backs horizontal scaling of the socket layer.

13.5 Auditability

  • Inquiry approvals write an action log; case lifecycle transitions record caseworker attribution (approved_by/at, resolved_by/at).
  • Full message history and attachments are retained as part of the case record (archival does not delete content).

13.6 Localization & Accessibility

  • Message content is translated into the recipient's preferred language (translatedContent); the victim app supports dynamic UI translations and a language switcher.
  • Clients should meet standard mobile/web accessibility guidance (contrast, screen-reader labels on message and attachment controls).

13.7 Compliance

  • Conversation records tied to cases support grant/PMT reporting continuity by keeping the service-delivery interaction attached to the claim; resolution captures pmtValues.

14. Analytics, Reporting & Notifications

14.1 Dashboards & KPIs

  • Advocate response time and message read rate (from §4.2), computed over chat_messages timestamps and isRead.
  • Unread/attention indicators: the VS360 left nav shows a "Chat" unread badge; the notification slice tracks per-type unread counts (inquiries, and message counts) rendered as sidebar indicators.
  • Case throughput context (new/approved/resolved claims) ties conversation volume to casework via the claims dashboards.
