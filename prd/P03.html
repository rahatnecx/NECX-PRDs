2. Executive Summary

The NECX Victim App is the survivor-facing mobile client of the NECX Coordinated Victim Services Platform. It is used directly by victims and survivors of crime to discover and request victim services from partner agencies, track the progress of their cases, communicate in real time with agency caseworkers and with a guidance-oriented NECX AI assistant, find emergency shelter and request a bed, complete agency-assigned intake assessments, electronically sign case documents, and manage a private, secure account — all from a phone.

The app serves people who are often in crisis and at elevated personal risk. Its design therefore treats safety, privacy, and dignity as first-class product requirements rather than afterthoughts. It ships with a second-factor NECX PIN lock, biometric unlock, an always-available emergency exit button, encrypted API traffic, secure on-device storage of tokens and keys, and data-minimization guardrails that verify a case belongs to the current user before any detail is rendered.

The app is a client of a single unified NECX backend (Node.js/Express, PostgreSQL, Socket.IO, Redis, Twilio, FCM, and AI providers) shared by four clients: the Victim App, the VS360 web/Workspace app for agency staff, and the NECX Admin console. All survivor-facing endpoints are served under the /mobile/* router prefix and authenticated as an APP (victim) principal. A distinctive workflow lets an advocate or caseworker create a case on a victim's behalf (including at shelter check-in) and hand the victim a one-time login code; the victim then activates that account and claims the case from the app.

This document specifies the app's architecture, its authentication and multi-step onboarding in detail, the full screen inventory, and every feature — service catalog and pre-assessment/service requests, my cases, AI and agency chat, shelter search and bed requests, intake assessments, e-sign, communities, resources, notifications, and profile/settings — with functional requirements, step-by-step flows, and safety/privacy non-functional requirements.

3. Product Overview & Vision

3.1 Problem Statement

Victims and survivors of crime frequently struggle to find and access the services they are eligible for. Help is fragmented across many agencies; intake is repetitive; there is no single, private place to track what has been requested, what has been approved, and who to talk to. People in crisis need to find shelter quickly, communicate safely, and complete paperwork without exposing themselves to further risk. Existing tools rarely account for the safety realities of a survivor holding the device.

3.2 Product Vision

The NECX Victim App gives every survivor a single, private, safety-first mobile front door to coordinated victim services: discover the right service, request it with a guided pre-assessment, track the resulting case, chat with the agency and an AI assistant, find shelter and request a bed in real time, sign documents in-app, and do all of it behind a PIN and biometric lock with an instant escape hatch.

3.3 Position in the NECX Ecosystem

The NECX platform is a single unified backend serving four coordinated clients:

  • NECX Victim App (this product) — survivor-facing mobile client (Flutter + GetX), issuer type APP.
  • VS360 Web / Workspace App — agency staff/caseworkers (issuer type VSMS, context VS360) who publish services, triage inquiries, manage cases, run shelters and hotlines, and moderate communities.
  • NECX Admin console — NECX Inc platform super-admins/staff (issuer type NECX, context ADMIN).
  • Unified backend — Node.js/Express (ESM), Objection/Knex over PostgreSQL, Socket.IO, Redis, BullMQ, Azure Blob Storage, Twilio, FCM, and OpenAI/Anthropic providers.

Actions taken in the Victim App are mirrored to agency staff on VS360 and vice versa: a service claim created in the app appears in the caseworker's queue; a caseworker approving a claim, publishing an update, assigning a shelter bed, or requesting a signature triggers real-time and push notifications back to the survivor's phone.

4. Goals, Objectives & Success Metrics

4.1 Goals & Objectives

  • Give survivors a private, safety-first mobile experience with second-factor PIN + biometric lock and an emergency exit.
  • Make discovering and requesting victim services fast, guided (pre-assessment claim questions), and geographically relevant (state/city filtering).
  • Provide a single "My connections" view of every case the survivor has with any agency, with live status and updates.
  • Enable low-friction, real-time communication: AI assistant for guidance/triage plus human agency chat for inquiries and cases.
  • Deliver emergency shelter discovery, live bed availability, and in-app bed requests with real-time status.
  • Let survivors complete agency-assigned intake forms and electronically sign case documents from the phone.
  • Support advocate-created and shelter-check-in cases via a one-time login-code activation flow.
  • Preserve privacy and dignity through data minimization, encrypted transport, and ownership checks on all case data.

5. Scope

5.1 In Scope

  • Onboarding: intro slides, connectivity check, Terms & Conditions and Privacy Policy screens.
  • Authentication: multi-step registration (personal, location, demographics, password), OTP verification (email/SMS), login, auto-login on relaunch, password reset, login-with-code activation and first-time password set, logout, delete account.
  • App security: NECX PIN (set/change/delete/toggle/verify), biometric unlock (per-type), session lock (423) and lockout (403) handling, emergency exit button.
  • Service catalog: browse/search/filter services by category/state/city; service detail with pre-assessment claim questions; submit service claim; request-to-connect; rate; mark visit duration; referral consent.
  • My cases: "All my connections" list with status filters/sort; claimed-case detail with appointments, published updates, documents, chat availability, and rating.
  • Chat: NECX AI assistant (REST session, recommendations) and real-time agency chat (service inquiry threads + case/inbox threads over Socket.IO) with attachments, translation, read receipts, and media browsing.
  • Shelter: search/list/map with filters and distance, shelter detail with live layout, directions, bed request (with room/bed preference and max-stay), my shelters/requests, cancel, real-time status.
  • Intake assessments: view and submit agency-assigned intake forms; pending-count surfacing.
  • E-sign / documents: view case documents, sign (drawn/typed/saved), manage saved signature.
  • Notifications: in-app list + pull-down overlay, FCM push (foreground/background/cold-start), type-based deep-link routing.
  • Profile & settings: view/edit profile, change email/phone (OTP), language switcher, self-help videos, feedback, security section.
  • Localization (en, es, bn, fr, ar) with dynamically fetched translations.

5.2 Out of Scope

  • All agency staff/caseworker functionality (service publishing, inquiry/case triage, notes, shelter administration, hotline operation, community moderation, PMT/grant reporting) — delivered by the VS360 Workspace/Web app.
  • NECX platform administration (agency/user/role/plan/category management) — delivered by the NECX Admin console.
  • NECX Drive (agency cloud file storage) — VS360 web only; no victim surface.
  • Hotline softphone / call handling — staff-side only (the survivor is the external caller).
  • Communities are implemented in the app but currently hidden (tab and routes commented out); treated as present-but-disabled (see Risks).
  • The legacy simulated e-sign agreement flow (e_sign_controller.dart, dummy data) is superseded by the live CaseDocumentsController path and is not a shipped feature.
  • Tutorial video route is present but currently disabled.

7. System Context & Architecture

7.1 Tech Stack

  • Framework: Flutter (Dart SDK >=2.17.6 <4.0.0).
  • State / DI / routing: GetX (get: ^4.6.5) — GetMaterialApp, GetxController, Bindings, named routes, GetMiddleware.
  • HTTP: dio: ^5.7.0 (single DioModule client with interceptors).
  • Realtime: socket_io_client: ^3.0.1.
  • Push / notifications: firebase_core, firebase_messaging, flutter_local_notifications.
  • Crash / analytics: firebase_crashlytics, posthog_flutter.
  • Storage: get_storage (non-sensitive) + flutter_secure_storage (tokens, user JSON, private key, PIN/biometric flags).
  • Auth extras: local_auth (biometric), pin_code_fields, sms_autofill (OTP autofill).
  • Maps / location: google_maps_flutter, geolocator, flutter_polyline_points (shelter directions).
  • Media / docs: syncfusion_flutter_pdfviewer, flutter_html, image_picker, file_picker, photo_manager, video_player, youtube_player_flutter, cached_network_image.
  • Responsive / UI: flutter_screenutil (design size 390×850), flutter_zoom_drawer, shimmer, lottie, carousel_slider, flutter_svg.
  • Encryption: encrypt (decrypts encrypted API responses).
  • Deep links: app_links (necxapp://config?api=...&socket=...).

7.2 Application Structure & Initialization

The app uses a flat layout: lib/screens, lib/controller, lib/widgets, lib/models, lib/services, lib/bindings, lib/utils, lib/config, lib/middleware.

  • lib/main.dart binds Flutter, eagerly Get.puts AuthController, NECXPinController, and NECXAuthService (permanent), installs FlutterError.onError, runs AppInitializer.initialize(...), starts DeepLinkService, then runApp(NECX()). A root Listener unfocuses inputs on tap.
  • lib/app_initializer.dart performs ordered startup: environment + logger + response decrypter → Firebase.initializeApp → Crashlytics → NECXStorage.init() (critical for auth) → deep-link API override → system UI (edge-to-edge, portrait only) → non-blocking PostHog / version info / FCM setup → deferred (microtask) notification init, socket init, cold-start FCM handling → GetX DI → translations (TranslationService.init() via isolate JSON parsing).
  • lib/screens/root_screen.dart wraps ScreenUtilInit → GetMaterialApp with translations: AppTranslations(), getPages: NECXRoutes.pages, initialRoute: '/', theme: AppTheme.lightTheme, initialBinding: RootBindings(). The global builder overlays the EmergencyExitButton (when enabled and not on splash/onboarding/no-internet) and an optional dev NetworkInspector FAB.

7.3 GetX Architecture Pattern

  • Controllers (lib/controller/*) hold reactive .obs state and orchestrate service calls. Singletons include AuthController, NECXPinController, HomeController, ShelterController, InboxController, InquiryChatController, AiController, IntakeController, CaseDocumentsController, CommunityController, ProfileController, SettingsController.
  • Services (lib/services/*): NECXAuthService (GetxService holding session state), per-domain API service classes (lib/services/api_services/victim/*), SocketService, ShelterSocketService, NotificationService, TranslationService, BiometricService, DeepLinkService, and upload/download/maps helpers.
  • Bindings (lib/bindings/*): RootBindings (global) plus per-route bindings attached to GetPages.
  • Endpoints are centralized in lib/services/api_services/endpoints/*.

7.4 Backend Communication (Dio client)

  • Base URL from Environment.baseUrl; default header X-Netlify-Secret: <mobileAppSecretKey> gates all API access.
  • Request interceptor injects Authorization: Bearer <accessToken> and x-refresh-token from secure storage.
  • Response interceptor decrypts encrypted payloads via responseDecrypter.decryptAsync (isolate for large payloads); skips HTML.
  • 401 handling: reads a rotated token from response headers and silently retries with the new access + refresh token.
  • 423 (session locked) and 403 (PIN/LOCKOUT) are surfaced to callers; PIN screens handle them.
  • Options(extra: {'suppressErrors': true}) lets a caller opt out of the auto error toast (used by PIN/device-token/sign-in flows).

7.5 Environments

local / development / staging / production (lib/config/env.dart), each with base + socket URLs (e.g. prod https://necx-prod-proxy.eastus2.cloudapp.azure.com/v2, dev https://necx-backend-v4.onrender.com/v2). Values are overridable at build via --dart-define and at runtime via the necxapp://config?api=...&socket=... deep link, which DeepLinkService persists.

7.6 Real-Time & Notifications

  • SocketService (singleton) connects to Environment.socketUrl with an x-mobile-app-secret header and auth: {token, refreshToken}; websocket-first with polling fallback, auto-reconnect, and room re-join on reconnect. It exposes an onNewMessage stream dispatched from a catch-all onAny handler for new-message-* events. Inbox/inquiry chat join per-thread rooms thread-{threadUid}-{userUid} (matching the web client).
  • ShelterSocketService plus a dedicated /me namespace carry shelter events (request.status_changed, bed.status_changed, availability.changed, layout.changed, shelter.directory_changed).
  • FCM push via NotificationService (high-importance Android channel; iOS permission request). The device token is obtained by AuthController.getDeviceFcmToken(), sent on login (deviceToken in the verify payload), and refreshed via PATCH /mobile/account/device-token. NotificationNavigator routes taps by type to the correct screen.

7.7 Tenancy & Identity

The app is single-tenant from the survivor's perspective: one authenticated victim (issuer.type=APP, scoped to issuer.id) who may hold cases across many agencies. There is no agency/context gate on mobile routes; isolation is enforced by scoping every query to the authenticated victim and by client-side ownership checks on case-detail screens.

9. Detailed User & System Flows

9.1 Register a New Account (Self-Registration)

  1. From /login, the survivor taps "Register" → /registration.
  2. AuthController.statesList() preloads states/cities (GET /mobile/states?pageLess=true, isolate-parsed, 3× retry) for the location step.
  3. Step 1 — Personal Information: enter first/last name, email, phone (with country code), and choose OTP channel (EMAIL/SMS); validate via the step's GlobalKey before advancing.
  4. Step 2 — Location Details: enter address; select state, then city from the cascading dropdown; enter zip; validate.
  5. Step 3 — Additional Information: select race/ethnicity, gender (+ optional "other"), age, and special classification (+ optional "other"); validate.
  6. Step 4 — Set Password: enter password + confirm (regex-validated) and check the Terms & Conditions box; Register enables only when a channel is chosen, T&C checked, and passwords match.
  7. Submit → AuthController.registeruser(...) → POST /mobile/users.
  8. On success, reset registration data and auto-log-in (victimLogin → POST /mobile/auth/sign-in), then navigate to the OTP screen (actionType:'loginVerify').
  9. The survivor enters the OTP (SMS autofill supported) → POST /mobile/auth/verify-profile {otp, privateKey, type:'APP', deviceToken}.
  10. On success, persist tokens/user/privateKey, identify analytics, reset+init the socket, preload critical home data behind NECXLoaderScreen, then route through PIN setup/verification (navigateAfterAuth).
  11. Edge cases: duplicate email/phone → inline error on Step 1; network failure during states preload → retry (3×) then a soft error; invalid/expired OTP → inline error with resend; back navigation preserves entered data.

9.2 Log In and Auto-Login on Relaunch

  1. On /login, enter identifier (email/phone) + password + OTP channel → victimLogin() → POST /mobile/auth/sign-in (errors suppressed, handled inline).
  2. On 200, navigate to the OTP screen with {privateKey, identifier, password, otp_channel, actionType:'loginVerify'}.
  3. Enter OTP → victimProfileVerify() → POST /mobile/auth/verify-profile; establish the session as in 9.1 (tokens, socket, analytics, home preload).
  4. navigateAfterAuth(user, allowSkipPinValidation:false) decides home vs PIN-set vs PIN-verify.
  5. Auto-login (relaunch, splash_screen.dart): connectivity check → authService.ensureInitialized() → if onboarding incomplete /onboarding; else if isLoggedIn with user data, preload home + init ShelterController → navigateAfterAuth(userData, allowSkipPinValidation:false) (PIN enforced on relaunch); else /login. Any error → /login.
  6. Edge cases: expired access token → silent refresh via rotated header token; session locked (423) → PIN verify; lockout (403 PIN/LOCKOUT) → notify + logout.

9.3 Request a Service (Pre-Assessment → Service Claim)

  1. Home preloads categories and the user's claimed services; the survivor opens the Services tab.
  2. The list loads with filters (category, state, city) and debounced search: GET /mobile/victim-services?page&pageSize=15&categoryId&searchKey&stateId&cityId.
  3. Tap a service → getServiceDetails GET /mobile/victim-services/{uid} (with language when non-English); the detail returns claimQuestions (the pre-assessment) and the current claim state.
  4. Tap "Request / Claim" → ServiceClaimScreen renders the claim questions (fill-in-the-blank / multiple-choice / true-false), enforcing isRequired.
  5. Submit → claimService() POST /mobile/service-claims {victimId, serviceId, questionAnswers}.
  6. Backend creates the claim (uid=CL<yy><month><uuid6>, status pending for a self-service victim), stores answers, creates a stats row and a PMT report snapshot, and emits claim-request to category-assigned caseworkers.
  7. On success, the app refreshes the service detail and the claimed list; the new case appears under "My connections" as pending.
  8. Alternative — request-to-connect: PUT /mobile/victim-services/{uid}/request-to-connect (dedupes; emails the center; 409 if already requested).
  9. Edge cases: an existing ongoing (pending/approved) claim for the same service is blocked (duplicate guard) and surfaced gracefully; referred cases prompt for referral consent (POST /mobile/partnerships/referrals/{uid}/decision).
  10. When the caseworker approves the claim, the app receives service-claim-action-for-<victimUid> + FCM; the case flips to approved, a published "Approved" update appears, and a SERVICE chat thread opens.

9.4 Claim an Advocate-Created Case via Login Code

  1. An advocate creates a case in VS360 (or staff checks the victim into a shelter bed); the backend mints a one-time login code and delivers it via SMS/email.
  2. The survivor opens the app → /codeLogin and enters the code → loginWithCode(code) POST /mobile/auth/login-with-code.
  3. The app navigates to the OTP screen with actionType:'activateAccount'; if needed, sendOtpForCode() POST /mobile/auth/send-otp-for-code resends the OTP.
  4. Enter OTP → verifyOtpAndActivate() POST /mobile/auth/verify-otp-for-login-code.
  5. Branch A — verified but no password: navigate to first_time_password_set_screen → setPasswordFirstLogin() POST /mobile/auth/set-password-first-login (with preAuthToken) → log in and route through PIN.
  6. Branch B — already fully verified: log in directly (tokens established) and route through PIN.
  7. The previously anonymous/advocate case is now linked to the real victim account and appears in "My connections" (advocate-created cases are auto-approved), along with any pending intake forms or documents awaiting signature.
  8. Edge cases: invalid/expired code → inline error with retry; code already used → guidance to use normal login; shelter check-in case surfaces under "My Shelters" with the active bed assignment and check-in window.

9.5 Chat with a Caseworker (Real-Time Agency Chat)

  1. From a service detail or an approved case, start an inquiry: handleChat() POST /mobile/chat-services/start-inquiry {inquiryData:{serviceCenterId, serviceId}} returns a thread; open /serviceInquiryChat. (For approved cases, a SERVICE thread already exists.)
  2. The Inbox tab loads threads: getAllChatThreads(type?) GET /mobile/chat-services/get-all-threads; once the socket is connected, join room thread-{threadUid}-{userUid} per thread.
  3. Load history: GET /mobile/chat-services/get-all-chats/{threadUid}?page&pageSize=20 (paginated, reversed).
  4. Send a message: POST /mobile/chat-services/send-message/{threadUid} as multipart/form-data (content + up to 5 attachments); show an optimistic bubble, then replace it with the server message (carrying translatedContent).
  5. Real-time receipt: SocketService.onNewMessage (from new-message-*, payload additionalData.threadUid) appends incoming messages to the matching thread live; caseworker replies arrive without refresh.
  6. Mark read on open/scroll: PATCH /mobile/chat-services/read/{threadUid}; browse attachments via GET .../get-all-files/{threadUid} and preview/download media.
  7. Edge cases: socket disconnect → auto-reconnect and room re-join; chat availability rule enforced (referred cases need consent + agency acceptance; non-referred need an existing thread); an inquiry approved by staff emits inquiry-approve-for-<victimUid> and notifies the survivor.

9.6 Sign a Case Document

  1. A caseworker sends a document for signature in VS360; the backend creates a victim signature row and notifies the survivor (socket + in-app + FCM + email DOCUMENT_SIGNATURE_REQUEST).
  2. The survivor sees the e-sign pending banner on home (or a notification) and opens "Documents & Forms" (signed_documents_screen) → Documents tab, which lists docs via GET /mobile/case-documents (needsMySignature, claimUid).
  3. Tap a document → GET /mobile/case-documents/{uid} → open the PDF viewer (document_pdf_viewer_screen, syncfusion), streaming bytes from .../{uid}/file (signed copy preferred; the preview shows dashed placeholders where signatures go).
  4. Tap "Sign" → choose to draw a signature, type one (rendered in a cursive font style), or reuse the saved signature (useSaved:true).
  5. Submit → POST /mobile/case-documents/{uid}/sign with either the signature image (signatureType:'drawn'|'typed', signerName) or useSaved:true.
  6. Backend authorizes (signerAppUserId === issuer.id), stores the signature image, records the IP + timestamp, and re-stamps the signed PDF from the pristine original; document status advances (partially_signed → signed when all required signatures are collected).
  7. On completion the survivor receives DOCUMENT_SIGNED; a partially signed doc where staff signed first shows a DOCUMENT_SIGNATURE_REQUEST reminding the victim their signature is still needed.
  8. Edge cases: useSaved with no saved signature → 400 handled inline (prompt to draw/type); a withdrawn document emits DOCUMENT_REMOVED and drops from the list; stamping failures are non-fatal server-side and do not block recording the signature.

9.7 Find a Shelter and Request a Bed

  1. Open the Shelter tab → GET /mobile/shelters with filters and GPS (lat/lng/radius_km); results show distance and availability; recent searches load via GET /mobile/me/shelter-searches/recent.
  2. Optionally search by query/zip; record the term via POST /mobile/me/shelter-searches.
  3. Tap a shelter → GET /mobile/shelters/{uid} + layout GET /mobile/shelters/{uid}/layout; connect ShelterSocketService for live bed/availability updates; view directions GET /mobile/shelters/{uid}/directions (confidential shelters return 403 and hide address until intake).
  4. Tap "Request a bed" → optionally pick room/bed from the live layout (shelter_room_picker_screen), then shelter_request_bed_screen collects party_size, urgency, bed_type_preference, needs, contact, notes, and max_stay_nights.
  5. Submit → POST /mobile/shelter-requests; status is waiting if beds are free else waitlisted; the response includes waitlist position + check-in window; web staff are notified (SHELTER_REQUEST).
  6. Track via "My Shelters" (GET /mobile/me/shelters) and "My shelter requests" (GET /mobile/me/shelter-requests[/{uid}]), showing mapped status, assigned-bed labels, days remaining, and history.
  7. Real-time: /me request.status_changed updates status live and fires notifications when staff accept (SHELTER_BED_ASSIGNED/SHELTER_REQUEST_ACCEPTED), reject (SHELTER_REQUEST_REJECTED), waitlist (SHELTER_WAITLISTED), transfer (SHELTER_BED_TRANSFERRED), or check out (SHELTER_CHECKED_OUT).
  8. Cancel: POST /mobile/me/shelter-requests/{uid}/cancel (blocked once assigned).

9.8 Complete an Intake Assessment

  1. Home/drawer surface the pending intake count; open "Documents & Forms" → Forms tab or the intake screen.
  2. fetchSubmissions() GET /mobile/intake-submissions lists pending + submitted forms.
  3. Tap a form → GET /mobile/intake-submissions/{uid} (questions + service-center name).
  4. Answer and submit → POST /mobile/intake-submissions/{uid}/submit {responses}; local status flips to submitted with a success toast and a background refresh.
  5. Edge case: a 400 indicates the form was already completed.

9.9 Set / Unlock with NECX PIN and Biometrics

  1. First-time (post-auth): if PIN not set, navigateAfterAuth routes to NecxPinSetScreen → POST /mobile/account/pin/setup; after creation skipPinValidation prevents an immediate re-prompt and the survivor lands on home.
  2. Relaunch/lock: navigateAfterAuth(..., allowSkipPinValidation:false) routes to NecxPinVerificationScreen.
  3. Unlock via PIN: POST /mobile/auth/pin/unlock → on success store the fresh access/refresh tokens and enter home.
  4. Unlock via biometric: authenticateWithBiometric() prompts the OS; success unlocks equivalently.
  5. Manage in Settings → NECX Security → PIN tile (/necx-pin): change (PUT /mobile/account/pin/change, new ≠ current), delete (DELETE /mobile/account/pin), toggle active (POST /mobile/settings/pin/toggle), and per-type biometric toggles (only when a PIN is set and the device supports biometrics).
  6. Edge cases: too many wrong PIN attempts → 403 PIN/LOCKOUT → notify + force logout to reset; expired session → 401 → logout; wrong PIN → inline message (no double toast).

9.10 Chat with the NECX AI Assistant

  1. From home, open ask_ai_screen → ensure a session POST /mobile/tool/ai/create-session (session id cached); load history GET /mobile/tool/ai/get-messages/{sessionId}.
  2. Send a message → queued and processed serially → POST /mobile/tool/ai/send-message/{sessionId} {message} with backoff retries and optimistic sending/sent/failed bubbles.
  3. The assistant replies with guidance and may include recommended services and videos rendered as cards; recommended videos also feed the self-services grid.
  4. End the session → DELETE /mobile/tool/ai/end-session/{sessionId} → return to home.

9.11 Reset a Forgotten Password

  1. From /login → /forgotPassword: enter identifier → checkResetChannels() POST /mobile/auth/check-reset-channels returns masked available channels (EMAIL/SMS).
  2. Choose a channel → passwordResetTrigger() POST /mobile/auth/reset-password-trigger sends a code.
  3. Enter the code → verifyOtp() POST /mobile/auth/verify-reset-password → navigate to new_password_set_screen.
  4. Set the new password → setNewPassword() PATCH /mobile/auth/reset-password → return to /login.

12. RBAC & Permissions

The Victim App authenticates a single role: the survivor (issuer type APP). Mobile routes carry no allowOnly context gate; access control is auth-only plus per-victim scoping and client-side ownership checks. There are no in-app role/permission flags for the survivor; capability differences arise from account/case state rather than roles.

13. Non-Functional Requirements

13.1 Security

  • Transport & access: all API and socket access gated by the mobile app secret header; Bearer access token + refresh token with silent rotation on 401.
  • Encrypted payloads: encrypted API responses decrypted client-side via response_decrypter (AES key/IV in env; large payloads decrypted in an isolate).
  • Secure storage: all tokens, user JSON, private key, and PIN/biometric flags stored in flutter_secure_storage; only non-sensitive prefs in get_storage.
  • Second factor: NECX PIN (4–6 digit) verified via POST /mobile/auth/pin/unlock, which rotates tokens; biometric unlock via local_auth (per-type toggles).
  • Session hardening: 423 session-lock (inactivity) and 403 PIN/LOCKOUT (too many attempts) force PIN re-verification or a full re-auth/reset.

13.2 Privacy & Victim Safety

  • Emergency exit button: EmergencyExitButton (toggle in Settings) overlaid on all authenticated screens for instant escape.
  • Data minimization: the app requests and stores only what each flow needs; case-detail screens verify ownership before rendering to guard against data leakage; referral chats are gated on explicit consent.
  • Confidential shelters: address/coordinates hidden and directions denied (403) until after an intake interview.
  • Analytics discipline: PostHog and Crashlytics identify by user uid and are reset on logout; Crashlytics is disabled in debug.
  • No signing links in email: document-signature emails direct the survivor into the app; signing happens in-app only.

13.3 Performance

  • Critical home data preloaded behind NECXLoaderScreen; heavy JSON parsing (states, claimed services) offloaded to isolates.
  • Lists paginated and debounced (services pageSize=15, 500 ms search debounce; chat pageSize=20); shelter layout supports ETag/304.
  • AI send uses queuing + exponential backoff with jitter and 15→30 s timeouts; states preload retries 3×.
  • Responsive layout via flutter_screenutil (390×850 design size); images via cached_network_image; shimmer placeholders during load.

13.4 Availability & Offline

  • Splash connectivity check routes offline users to /noInternet; ConnectivityController (connectivity_plus) monitors the network continuously.
  • Socket auto-reconnects and re-joins rooms; network-sensitive calls (states, AI, shelter) retry/timeout to degrade gracefully.

13.5 Auditability

  • Backend writes ActionLogs for create/update/delete operations and records IP + timestamp per document signature.
  • The app surfaces case updates, shelter request history, and document status transitions so the survivor has a visible trail.

13.6 Localization & Accessibility

  • Locales: en, es, bn, fr, ar (Arabic in the supported list for RTL). Base en shipped; dynamic translations fetched from backend and merged at runtime; service details re-fetched with a language param when non-English; chat messages carry translatedContent.
  • Typed e-signature uses a dedicated cursive font family; portrait-only, edge-to-edge system UI.

13.7 Compliance

  • The pre-assessment/demographics captured at registration and claim time feed the backend PMT (Performance Measurement Tool) report snapshot used for federal/grant reporting; the app must collect these fields accurately and consistently.

14. Analytics, Reporting & Notifications

14.1 Analytics

  • PostHog captures product events and identifies the survivor by uid (reset on logout).
  • Crashlytics captures crashes/non-fatals (disabled in debug), keyed to the user uid; target crash-free sessions ≥ 99.5%.

14.2 Notifications

The app delivers notifications through three channels that mirror backend events:

  • In-app list + pull-down overlay: GET /mobile/users/notifications; mark read PATCH /mobile/users/notifications/read/{uid}.
  • Socket (real-time): chat new-message-* / message-service / message-inquiry; case service-claim-action-for-<victimUid>, update-<receiverUid>; inquiry inquiry-approve-for-<victimUid>; shelter request.status_changed, availability.changed, bed.status_changed, layout.changed, shelter.directory_changed; document case-document-signed-* and signature-request events.
  • FCM push: foreground → local notification; background/cold-start → NotificationNavigator.navigateFromFCMData(data).

Notification types routed by NotificationNavigator: service-claim / appointment / document → claimed-case detail; document-signature-request → document sign; inquiry / service new-message → inbox chat; community post/comment/group-chat → community screens. Shelter notification types include SHELTER_BED_ASSIGNED, SHELTER_BED_TRANSFERRED, SHELTER_REQUEST_REJECTED, SHELTER_WAITLISTED, SHELTER_CHECKED_OUT, SHELTER_REQUEST_ACCEPTED; document types include DOCUMENT_SIGNATURE_REQUEST, DOCUMENT_SIGNED, DOCUMENT_REMOVED.

14.3 Survivor-Facing Dashboards

The app has no reporting dashboards (reporting is staff/admin-side). The survivor's equivalent surfaces are: home (categories, cases, intake pending count, e-sign banner, recommended content), "All my connections" (case list with status filters/sort), "My Shelters"/"My shelter requests," and "Documents & Forms" (pending badge).
