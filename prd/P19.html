2. Executive Summary

NECX Communities are moderated, agency-run community spaces where victims and survivors of crime (app users) can connect with peers and with agency staff in a safe, supervised environment. An agency creates a community, sets its membership rules (open or private, with optional mandatory joining questions), and staff act as administrators and moderators. Members participate either through a real-time group chat (GROUP_CHAT) or through a feed of posts and comments (POST), depending on the community type.

The module serves two distinct audiences from a single unified backend. Agency staff use the VS360 web application to create and configure communities, review and approve join requests, manage membership and moderators, and moderate content through a report-and-warning workflow. Victims use the NECX mobile app to discover communities, request to join, and participate by posting, commenting, and messaging. Every message, post, and comment passes through automatic text moderation, and any participant can file a report against content or a member, which staff then triage.

Communities matters because peer support is a recognized element of trauma recovery, but unmoderated peer spaces can expose vulnerable people to re-victimization, harassment, and privacy loss. NECX Communities is built around safety first: mandatory content sanitization, staff-only moderation controls, banning and warning workflows, and cross-platform notifications that keep both staff and members informed of moderation actions.

This document specifies the module as implemented in the NECX backend (/web/communities* and /mobile/communities*), the VS360 CommunitiesManagement web surface, and the victim-app community screens. It is important to note that the victim-facing community experience is fully built on both backend and mobile but is currently hidden in production — the community tab and routes are commented out in the mobile app. This PRD documents the intended end-to-end behavior and explicitly flags the hidden status in Scope and Risks so that the module can be safely enabled.

3. Product Overview & Vision

3.1 Problem Statement

Victims and survivors are often isolated. Peer connection helps recovery, but generic social platforms are unsafe for this population: they lack trauma-informed moderation, expose identity and location, and offer no bridge to the agencies providing services. Agencies need a way to run supervised support spaces where staff retain control over who joins, what is said, and how harmful content is handled — without standing up separate tooling outside the victim-services platform.

3.2 Product Vision

NECX Communities gives every agency a turnkey, safety-first community capability inside the platform victims already use. Staff spin up a community in minutes, gate entry with screening questions, and moderate with one-click controls. Victims find a curated, agency-backed space to share and support one another, with automatic content protection working invisibly in the background. The result is peer support that is coordinated with — not disconnected from — the agency's broader service delivery.

3.3 Position in the NECX Ecosystem

Communities is one module of the unified NECX platform, which comprises the Victim App (mobile, for survivors), the Workspace App, the VS360 web app (agency staff / VSMS), NECX Admin (platform super-admin), and a single Express + Objection/Knex (PostgreSQL) backend with Socket.IO, Redis, and FCM. Communities reuses the platform's shared primitives: the app_users (victim) and vsms_users (staff) identity model, agency (service center) multi-tenancy, the text-moderation tool shared with Chat, the notification delivery pipeline (DB row + Socket.IO + FCM), and the RBAC permission model. It sits alongside Chat (1:1 and agency messaging), Shelter, and Hotline as an agency-operated, victim-facing service surface.

4. Goals, Objectives & Success Metrics

4.1 Goals & Objectives

  • Provide agencies a safe, moderated space to host peer-support communities for victims, with full control over membership and content.
  • Support two interaction models — real-time group chat and a posts-and-comments feed — under one community abstraction.
  • Enforce trauma-informed safety by default: automatic text sanitization on all user content, staff-only moderation, and clear ban/warning workflows.
  • Give victims a low-friction path to discover, request to join, and participate in communities from the mobile app.
  • Screen entrants where needed via private communities with mandatory joining questions and staff approval.
  • Keep both staff and members informed through cross-platform notifications for posts, comments, messages, approvals, bans, and content removals.
  • Preserve agency multi-tenancy: staff only see and manage communities within their own agency.

5. Scope

5.1 In Scope

  • Community lifecycle (VS360): create a community (type GROUP_CHAT or POST), configure open vs private, member limit, mandatory-questions flag, joining questions, and initial admin/moderator staff assignments; edit; toggle active/inactive status.
  • Membership: victim join (open = immediate, private = request + approval), joining questions and answers, join-request review, member listing, member banning, leaving a community.
  • Moderator management: staff join a community as moderator, and staff moderator removal.
  • Posts & comments (POST-type communities): create/list posts, create/list comments, edit posts/comments, toggle post/comment status, and moderation (deactivate, deactivate-and-ban) of posts and comments.
  • Group chat (GROUP_CHAT-type communities): paginated message listing with reported-content flagging, send message (with mobile file attachments), and moderation (deactivate message, deactivate-and-ban sender).
  • Content moderation: automatic text sanitization on member content via the shared text-moderator tool; a report workflow (file a report against a post/comment/message/member) and staff triage (deactivate content, deactivate-and-ban author, issue warning).
  • Notifications: app notifications and FCM to victims for posts, comments, group-chat messages, join approval, bans, and content removals; report-warning notifications.
  • Victim-app experience: discover/list communities, view a community, join (answer questions), view/create posts and comments, participate in group chat, file reports, and leave. Documented here even though currently hidden (see 5.2).

5.2 Out of Scope / Not Yet Enabled

⚠ Victim community experience is currently hidden
The mobile app's community tab and routes are commented out in production (community/community_page.dart is hidden; the Shelter tab is shown in its place). The victim-side screens (community detail, join, group chat, post create, post detail) and the /mobile/communities/* endpoints exist and are documented here, but the feature is not exposed to victims until the tab and routes are re-enabled. Enabling it is a product decision, not new development.
  • Direct 1:1 messaging between members (that is Chat, not Communities).
  • Media/file attachments on posts and comments (only mobile group-chat messages support files in the current implementation).
  • Community discovery ranking, recommendations, or search beyond the basic list/filter provided.
  • Rich reactions (likes, emoji), threaded/nested comment replies, or read receipts within communities.
  • Cross-agency shared communities (each community belongs to one agency / service center).
  • Voice/video within communities.
  • Member-initiated community creation (only staff create communities).

7. System Context & Architecture

7.1 Tech Stack

Communities is delivered by the unified NECX backend: Express (ESM) with Objection/Knex over PostgreSQL, Socket.IO for real-time, Redis for scaling/caching, and FCM for push. The VS360 web client is a React app (src/pages/CommunitiesManagement/) calling /web/communities*. The victim client is a Flutter (GetX) mobile app whose CommunityController and community screens call /mobile/communities* (currently hidden). All routes are mounted under the global API prefix /v2.

7.2 Client-to-Backend Contract

The backend follows a tri-router pattern: webRouter (/web/communities*, guarded by allowOnly('VS360')) for staff management/moderation, and mobileRouter (/mobile/communities*, auth-only) for victims. Services return { status, msg?, data? }; controllers relay via res.status(response.status).json(response). Business keys are uid strings; DB primary keys are integer id. Rate limiting uses the standard limiter for community/report routes and the chatApi limiter for group-chat and posts/comments routes.

7.3 Identity, Authorship & Tenancy

Two identity tables underpin authorship: app_users (victims, referenced by member_id) and vsms_users (agency staff, referenced by staff_id / created_by). Community membership rows (community_members) carry exactly one of member_id (victim) or staff_id (staff) plus a role of ADMIN, MODERATOR, or MEMBER. Multi-tenancy is enforced by scoping community data to the staff member's agency via issuer.serviceCenterInfo.id — a VS360 user only ever sees and manages their own agency's communities (relation serviceCenterInfo on communities).

7.4 Real-Time & Notifications

Content and moderation events are delivered to victims through the platform notification pipeline: a DB notification row plus a Socket.IO emit to the victim room (app-notification-room) plus an FCM push. The Socket.IO io instance is threaded through the send and moderation service calls so open clients update live. App notification types come from appNotificationEvents.js; report-warning delivery writes VictimNotifications and pushes via notificationServices. (Notifications are wrapped defensively — a delivery failure is logged and never breaks the main transaction.)

7.5 Content Safety Pipeline

All member-authored text (group-chat messages, and by extension posts/comments) is passed through the shared text-moderator tool (src/tools/text-moderator, also used by Chat). textModerator.moderateText detects unsafe content and returns a sanitized version, which is what gets persisted — sanitization happens before content is visible to other members.

8. Functional Requirements

8.1 Community Lifecycle & Configuration (VS360)

Staff create and configure communities from the VS360 CommunitiesManagement surface. A community has a title, a type (GROUP_CHAT for real-time group messaging or POST for a feed of posts and comments), an isPrivate flag (private communities require join approval), a memberLimit, an areQuestionsMandatory flag, an optional set of joining questions, and an optional set of moderators. On create, the backend (createCommunity) runs a transaction that inserts the community with created_by set to the staff vsms_users id, inserts any joining questions, inserts an ADMIN community_members row for the creator, and inserts MODERATOR rows for any assigned moderators; an ActionLog entry is written. Communities can be edited (update), and their active/inactive state toggled (change-status).

8.3 Membership & Join Flow

Membership behavior depends on whether the community is private. For an open community, a victim join (joinCommunity, POST /join/:communityId) inserts a community_members row (plus answers) immediately. For a private community, the same call inserts a community_joining_requests row (plus answers) and returns "submitted for approval"; a member is added only when staff approve. In both cases the backend dedupes existing membership and enforces memberLimit. Staff approve requests (approveJoiningRequest, PATCH /:communityId/:memberId/approve-joining), ban members (banMember, PATCH /:communityId/ban-member/:memberId), and either side can leave (DELETE /:communityUid/leave).

8.6 Group Chat (GROUP_CHAT-type communities)

GROUP_CHAT-type communities are real-time group messaging spaces. Members send messages (POST /communities/group-chats/:uid/send-message; mobile allows file attachments), and staff can send from the web. sendMessage validates that the community exists and is not POST-type, runs textModerator.moderateText (replacing unsafe content with a sanitized version), inserts a group_chat_messages row keyed by memberId (mobile) or staffId (web), writes an ActionLog, and fires sendGroupChatMessageNotification (GROUP_CHAT_MESSAGE). The message list (GET /communities/group-chats/:uid) is paginated and flags reported messages/senders via correlated subqueries.

8.7 Reports & Moderation Workflow

Any member or staff can file a report (POST /communities/reports/create/:uid) against a post, comment, message, or member; reports are deduped per reporter + target. 9. Detailed User & System Flows

9.1 Create a Community with Joining Questions (Staff, VS360)

  1. A staff member with communities.add opens CommunitiesManagement and clicks Create Community (CreateCommunity.jsx → CommunityForm.jsx).
  2. They enter a title, choose a type (Post feed or Group chat), and choose visibility (Public/open or Private).
  3. They set a memberLimit and decide whether joining questions are mandatory (areQuestionsMandatory).
  4. They add one or more joining questions (JoiningQuestions.jsx) — used to screen applicants.
  5. They optionally select staff (VSMS users) to assign as moderators, and confirm.
  6. The client calls POST /communities/create (newCommunityValidator).
  7. The backend createCommunity opens a transaction: inserts the communities row with created_by = the staff vsms_users id and serviceCenterId = the staff's agency; inserts each joining question; inserts an ADMIN community_members row for the creator; inserts MODERATOR rows for each assigned staff member.
  8. An ActionLog entry is written and the transaction commits.
  9. The new community appears in the agency's "My Communities" list; the staff member can open it, and (if not already a participant) may "Join as Moderator."
  10. Edge case — validation failure (missing title/type, or invalid questions): the service returns a non-2xx { status, msg } and no rows are inserted (transaction rolls back).

9.2 Victim Requests to Join a Private Community and Is Approved

  1. A victim opens the community list in the app (getCommunitiesForApp) and taps a private community, then "Join" (/communityJoin).
  2. The app shows the joining questions; because areQuestionsMandatory is true, all must be answered.
  3. The app calls POST /mobile/communities/join/:communityId with the answers[].
  4. The backend joinCommunity dedupes any existing membership/request and checks memberLimit.
  5. If mandatory, it validates that all questions are answered; incomplete submissions are rejected.
  6. Because the community is private, it inserts a community_joining_requests row plus community_joining_question_answers, and returns "submitted for approval." No membership is created yet. An ActionLog is written.
  7. On VS360, a staff member opens the community's join requests (GET /communities/:uid/joining-requests, PendingJoinRequests.jsx) and reviews the applicant's answers.
  8. Staff click Approve → PATCH /communities/:communityId/:memberId/approve-joining.
  9. The backend approveJoiningRequest adds the applicant as a MEMBER (community_members) and sends an APPROVE_COMMUNITY_JOINING app notification (DB row + socket to app-notification-room + FCM).
  10. The victim's app receives the notification; the community now appears in their joined list and they can participate.
  11. Branch — open community: at step 6 the backend instead inserts the community_members row immediately and the victim is a member without staff approval.
  12. Branch — decline / no action: if staff do not approve, the request remains pending; staff may effectively deny by not approving (or by banning if later added). Edge case — member limit reached before approval: approval is blocked by the memberLimit enforcement.

9.3 Post and Comment in a POST-type Community

  1. A member opens a POST-type community; the feed loads via GET /communities/posts/:uid (CommunityPostDisplay).
  2. The member creates a post (/postCreate) → POST /communities/posts/:uid/create (validated).
  3. The backend sanitizes the post text via the text-moderator, inserts a posts row keyed by the author (member_id for a victim, staff_id for staff) with isActive/status, and notifies members with COMMUNITY_POST.
  4. Members see the post; another member opens it (/postDetails) and adds a comment → POST /communities/posts/:postUid/create-comment.
  5. The backend sanitizes and inserts a post_comments row and notifies with COMMUNITY_POST_COMMENT.
  6. The author (or staff) may edit (.../update) or toggle status (.../change-status).
  7. Edge case — content flagged by the moderator: the persisted text is the sanitized version, so members only ever see sanitized content.
  8. Edge case — group-chat community: post endpoints do not apply; the community renders as a message thread instead (see 9.4).

9.4 Send a Message in a GROUP_CHAT Community

  1. A member opens a GROUP_CHAT community (/communityChat); the client loads messages via GET /communities/group-chats/:uid (paginated; reported messages/senders are flagged).
  2. The member sends a message → POST /communities/group-chats/:uid/send-message (mobile may attach files).
  3. The backend sendMessage validates the community exists and is not POST-type; runs textModerator.moderateText (unsafe content replaced with a sanitized version).
  4. It inserts a group_chat_messages row with memberId (mobile) or staffId (web) and isActive=true, writes an ActionLog.
  5. It fires sendGroupChatMessageNotification (GROUP_CHAT_MESSAGE) to members (socket + FCM).
  6. Open clients update live; recipients get a push that deep-links to the group chat.
  7. Edge case — send to a POST-type community: rejected with a non-2xx response.

9.5 Report and Moderate Content (Report → Deactivate / Ban / Warn)

  1. A member (or staff) sees objectionable content and files a report → POST /communities/reports/create/:uid, identifying the target (post, comment, message, or member) and context.
  2. The backend dedupes per (reporter, target) and inserts a community_reports row with status PENDING.
  3. On VS360, staff open the community's Reports panel (GET /communities/reports/:uid, ReportsData.jsx/ReportCard.jsx).
  4. Staff choose an action:
  5. Deactivate content — for a reported message PATCH /communities/group-chats/deactivate/:reportId/:messageId (post/comment equivalents exist): the content's isActive is set false, the report is marked REVIEWED, and the author is notified (DELETE_MESSAGE/DELETE_POST/DELETE_COMMENT).
  6. Deactivate and ban — deactivate-and-ban-user/...: as above plus the author's membership is removed/flagged; the author is notified (DELETE_*_AND_BAN_MEMBER / BAN_MEMBER).
  7. Issue a warning — PATCH /communities/reports/:reportUid/issue-warning: issueWarning writes a VictimNotifications row and pushes via FCM, warning the member without removing them.
  8. Dismiss / no violation — staff may deactivate content without a report (deactivate/:messageId) or leave the report as-is if no action is warranted.
  9. The reporter and other members see the content disappear from feeds/threads once deactivated.
  10. Edge case — content already deactivated: subsequent reports/actions on the same item are idempotent (the item stays inactive).

9.6 Ban a Member and Leave a Community

  1. Staff open a community's member list (GET /communities/:uid/members).
  2. Staff ban a member → PATCH /communities/:communityId/ban-member/:memberId (banMember): the membership is removed/flagged and a BAN_MEMBER notification is sent.
  3. The banned member's app reflects loss of access; they can no longer post/comment/message in that community.
  4. Voluntary leave: a member or moderator calls DELETE /communities/:communityUid/leave to remove their own membership.
  5. Moderator lifecycle: staff join as moderator (POST /:uid/join-as-moderator) and are removed by another staff member via PATCH /:communityId/:staffId/remove-moderator.

12. RBAC & Permissions

Communities is an agency (VS360) module with the fine-grained action map communities = {view, add, edit, delete}, defined in role-permissions.js (vs360Modules). The context gate allowOnly('VS360') restricts management/moderation routes to agency staff; the fine-grained checkPermission('communities', action) gate enforces the specific action. SUPER_ADMIN bypasses fine-grained checks. Victim (mobile) routes are auth-only (no RBAC flags) and are further constrained by community membership and privacy rules.

13. Non-Functional Requirements

13.1 Safety & Content Moderation

  • Automatic sanitization: 100% of member-authored text (group-chat messages, posts, comments) must pass through textModerator.moderateText before it is persisted or visible to other members. The sanitized version is what is stored.
  • Staff-only moderation controls: deactivation, banning, and warning are restricted to VS360 staff with communities.edit; victims can only report.
  • Report-driven review: reports create an auditable PENDING→REVIEWED trail; deactivation/ban notify the affected author.
  • Escalation: repeat or severe violations support a single deactivate-and-ban action to remove content and the offender together.

13.2 Privacy & Victim Safety

  • Communities are agency-scoped; staff cannot view or manage another agency's communities.
  • Private communities gate entry behind staff approval and screening questions, protecting members from unvetted entrants.
  • Member content and identity are exposed only within the community; there is no cross-community or cross-agency leakage.
  • The victim app's global safety features (NECX PIN app-lock, emergency-exit button, encrypted responses, secure storage) apply to community screens once enabled.

13.3 Performance

  • Message, post, and comment lists are paginated; group-chat listing uses correlated subqueries to flag reported items without additional round-trips.
  • Community/report routes use the standard rate limiter; group-chat and posts/comments routes use the chatApi limiter to protect high-volume messaging paths.

13.4 Availability & Reliability

  • Notification delivery is wrapped defensively: a failure to emit a socket event or send FCM is logged and never breaks the primary content/moderation transaction.
  • Create-community is transactional (community + questions + admin/moderator rows commit atomically or roll back).

13.5 Auditability

  • Community create, join, message, and moderation actions write ActionLog entries.
  • Reports persist reporter, target, context, and status, providing a moderation audit trail.

13.6 Localization & Accessibility

  • The victim app supports multiple languages platform-wide; community screens should respect the user's preferred language and translation utilities as with Chat.
  • Client UIs should meet the platform's accessibility standards (readable feeds, keyboard/screen-reader support on VS360).

13.7 Compliance

  • Content moderation and audit trails support the agency's duty of care and any grant/reporting obligations around victim safety.
