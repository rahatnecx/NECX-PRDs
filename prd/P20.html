2. Executive Summary

NECX is a coordinated victim-services platform that connects survivors of crime with the agencies and advocates who help them. Service Management is the module through which agencies (service centers) define and publish the concrete offerings — legal aid, counseling, shelter intake, financial assistance, safety planning, and more — that a victim can discover and request from the Victim mobile app. It is the supply side of the platform: everything a victim can ask for exists because an agency created and activated a service here.

A service belongs to a global service category (an admin-governed taxonomy), carries descriptive content (title, short and long descriptions, an optional explainer video), and — critically — owns a claim-questions questionnaire that the agency designs to capture exactly the intake information it needs. When a victim requests a service, those questions become the victim's answers and seed a service claim (a case) for the agency's caseworkers to triage. Service Management therefore sits directly upstream of Case Management: the quality, discoverability and intake design of services determines the quality of every case that follows.

The module serves two audiences. Agency staff (case workers and agency admins) use the VS360 web app to author, edit, activate/deactivate and search their own services and design their intake questionnaires. NECX platform administrators use the Admin console to govern the shared category taxonomy and to create or verify services on an agency's behalf. Victims never author anything here; they consume the output — a searchable catalog of active services, filterable by category and geography and resolvable by a human-readable service code.

Because this module shapes what victims see and what they are asked at their most vulnerable, it is governed by NECX's mission commitments: safety, privacy and dignity. Visibility rules (geographic filtering, national flags, active/verified flags) ensure victims are shown only relevant, vetted help; the questionnaire builder is constrained to keep intake proportionate; and every mutation is audited.

3. Product Overview & Vision

3.1 Problem Statement

Victim-services agencies each offer a distinct mix of programs with distinct eligibility and intake needs, and those offerings change over time. Without a structured catalog, victims cannot discover the right help, agencies cannot present their programs consistently, and intake is ad hoc — leading to incomplete cases, duplicated data entry and poor grant/PMT reporting. Agencies also operate in specific geographies; a survivor should not be shown a service they cannot access, nor be denied a nationally available program because of a location filter.

3.2 Product Vision

Service Management gives every agency a self-service, governed way to publish its programs as a rich, searchable catalog and to design the exact intake it needs per program — while NECX centrally governs a shared taxonomy so services are comparable and discoverable across the network. A victim opening the app sees a curated, geographically relevant set of active, verified services; requesting one is a single guided flow that captures the agency's questions and instantly creates a case.

3.3 Where This Sits in the NECX Ecosystem

  • Victim App (mobile, issuer.type = APP) — consumes the catalog: browse/search services, view detail (increments view count, records visit duration), request-to-connect, and claim a service (which creates a case).
  • VS360 Web (caseworker/agency, issuer.type = VSMS, context VS360) — the primary authoring surface: "My Services" list, create/update service, questionnaire builder, activate/deactivate, delete, service detail.
  • NECX Admin (issuer.type = NECX, context ADMIN) — governs the global service-category taxonomy and can create/verify services and bulk-import them for agencies.
  • Unified backend (necx-backend_v2) — the victim-services module (service/, service-categories/) exposes tri-router endpoints (/admin, /web, /mobile) over PostgreSQL (Objection/Knex), with the service-center module owning the agency↔services relationship.

Service Management is upstream of Case Management (a claim references a serviceId), draws its taxonomy from NECX Admin, is scoped by the agency identity from Service Center, and feeds intake answers that ultimately support Reports/PMT.

4. Goals, Objectives & Success Metrics

4.1 Goals & Objectives

  • Enable agencies to publish, edit and retire their service offerings self-service, without NECX intervention.
  • Maintain a single, admin-governed service-category taxonomy so offerings are consistent and comparable across the network.
  • Let each agency tailor intake per service via a no-code claim-questions builder (fill-in-the-blank, multiple-choice, true-false; required flags; options).
  • Ensure victims discover only relevant, active, verified services through category filtering, geographic filtering and national-visibility rules, plus service-code lookup.
  • Guarantee a clean bridge from discovery to intake: requesting a service reliably creates a well-formed case with the victim's answers attached.
  • Protect data integrity: prevent deletion of services that already have cases, prevent duplicate services, and audit every change.

5. Scope

5.1 In Scope

  • Service categories: admin-managed taxonomy CRUD (create with name-dedup, list/search with pagination and page-less mode, detail, update); routing of claim/thread notifications to caseworkers assigned to a category.
  • Services CRUD: create, read (list + detail), update, activate/deactivate (soft), delete (guarded). Fields: title, shortDescription, description, videoUrl, categoryId, serviceCenterId, generated service_code, isNational, isActive, isVerified, viewCount, visitDuration.
  • Claim-questions / questionnaire builder per service: question text, questionType (fill-in-the-blank | multiple-choice | true-false), isRequired, options (JSON, for multiple-choice); asked at claim time.
  • Visibility & discovery rules: geographic filtering (city/state), isNational bypass, active/verified gating, category filter, free-text search, service-code lookup, view-count increment and visit-duration capture.
  • Victim-facing surfacing: catalog list/detail on mobile, request-to-connect, and claim creation (case intake) driven by a service and its questions.
  • Data model for Services, ServiceCategories, ClaimQuestions (and their relationship to ServiceClaims / ClaimQuestionAnswers).
  • RBAC for the services module (view, add, edit, delete) and admin category permissions; endpoint surface and NFRs.

5.2 Out of Scope

  • Case lifecycle (approve/reject/resolve, notes, updates, chat, files, ratings, referrals) — owned by the Case Management PRD; only the claim-creation bridge is described here.
  • PMT questionnaires / grants / report builder — a separate questionnaire system under reports/pmt-questionnaires; distinct from per-service claim questions and covered by the Reports PRD.
  • Agency / service-center CRUD, directory search, hotline, staff & seat management, partnerships/referrals — covered by the Service Center PRD. Only the agency↔services ownership relationship is referenced.
  • Category-to-caseworker assignment UI (vsms_user_service_categories) — managed in staff/user management; referenced only for notification routing.
  • Pricing/monetization of services, external directory syndication, and versioned change-history of a service's questionnaire (see Risks/Open Questions).

7. System Context & Architecture

7.1 Tech Stack

  • Backend (necx-backend_v2, branch develop): Node.js + Express (ESM), Objection/Knex over PostgreSQL, Redis, BullMQ (email/jobs), Socket.IO, Azure Blob Storage (media/files), Twilio (email/SMS for request-to-connect and login codes). Module root src/apps/victim-services/ with sub-modules service/ and service-categories/.
  • VS360 Web (vs360-webapp): React 18 + Vite, Mantine v7, Redux Toolkit + redux-persist, TanStack React Query v4, react-router-dom v6, TipTap (rich-text descriptions & question editing), @dnd-kit. Service layer: src/services/services.js. Pages under src/pages/ServiceManagement/ (ServiceManagement.jsx, CreateService.jsx, UpdateService.jsx, ServiceDetails.jsx) with ServiceForm / QuestionnaireForm / QuestionBuilder.
  • Victim App: mobile client (issuer.type = APP) consuming the /mobile/victim-services routes.

7.2 Backend Request Model

Every route file in victim-services builds three sub-routers by URL prefix + RBAC context, each wrapped with rateLimiter(rateLimiters.standard):

Sub-routerPrefixGuardConsumer
adminRouter/admin/*allowOnly('ADMIN')NECX Admin console
webRouter/web/*allowOnly('VS360')VS360 web / caseworker (VSMS staff)
mobileRouter/mobile/*none (auth only)Victim mobile app (issuer.type = APP)

The typical chain is rate-limiter → auth (populates req.issuer) → context-middleware → allowOnly(context) → permission-middleware (module.action) → Joi validator → controller → service → model. Service behavior branches heavily on issuer.type (APP vs VSMS vs ADMIN). API version prefix is v2 (e.g. /v2/web/victim-services).

7.3 Talking to the Backend (Web)

VS360 uses an Axios factory (src/services/axios.js) adding Authorization: Bearer <accessToken>, x-refresh-token, X-Netlify-Secret, X-Environment. Responses are AES-encrypted and decrypted client-side; refresh tokens rotate via response header; 401 logs out, 403 renders UnauthorizedAccess. Server state is cached with React Query; the victim-services and services/categories query keys drive the Service Management screens.

7.4 Real-time & Notifications

Service Management itself emits no Socket.IO events for CRUD. Its downstream, victim-initiated claim creation triggers a claim-request socket event and service_center_notifications to caseworkers assigned to the service's category (via vsms_user_service_categories); request-to-connect sends an email to the service center. FCM/socket delivery for the case lifecycle is described in Case Management.

7.5 Tenancy

Services are agency-scoped: a service belongs to a service_centers row via serviceCenterId. VSMS callers are constrained to their own issuer.serviceCenterInfo.id for create/update/delete; the victim (APP) catalog spans all agencies subject to visibility rules; NECX Admin spans all agencies. Categories are a single global taxonomy shared across all agencies.

8. Functional Requirements

8.1 Service Categories (Taxonomy)

Service categories are a global, NECX-admin-governed taxonomy (service_categories: name, description). A category groups comparable services across all agencies and also drives notification routing: caseworkers are linked to categories via vsms_user_service_categories, so a new claim on a service notifies the caseworkers who handle that service's category. Categories are read by every client (to filter/label services) but written only by NECX Admin.

Endpoints (/{admin,web,mobile}/services/categories): GET list (searchKey, page, pageSize, pageLess), GET /:uid (admin/web), POST create (admin only; dedupe by name), PATCH update/:uid (admin only).

8.2 Services — Creation & Publishing

A service (services, Services.js) is a concrete offering published by a service center under one category. On create the system generates a human-readable service_code, sets isVerified = true when the creator is VSMS (agency self-publishing is trusted), and defaults isActive so a newly created active service is immediately "published" to victims. Creation is available to VS360 (self, for own agency) and NECX Admin (on an agency's behalf). Duplicate services — same (title, categoryId, serviceCenterId) — are blocked.

Create endpoint: POST /{admin,web}/victim-services with body serviceCenterId, title, shortDescription, description, categoryId, videoUrl, claimQuestions[]. (No mobile create.)

8.3 Services — Editing, Activation & Deletion

Services are edited in place (PATCH), soft-toggled between active/inactive (a single activate/deactivate endpoint that flips isActive), and hard-deleted only when safe. Deactivating hides a service from victims without destroying it or its history — the preferred way to retire a seasonal or paused program. Deletion is permitted only when the service has no cases (totalCases === 0), preventing loss of case-linked data; VSMS deletes are scoped to the caller's own agency.

Endpoints: PATCH update/:uid; POST deactive-activate/:uid (toggle isActive); DELETE /:serviceUid (only if totalCases === 0; VSMS scoped to own center).

8.4 Claim-Questions / Questionnaire Builder

Each service owns an intake questionnaire — its claim questions (service_claim_questions) — designed by the agency and asked to the victim at claim time. A question has question text, a questionType of fill-in-the-blank, multiple-choice, or true-false, an isRequired flag, and an options JSON array (used by multiple-choice). These questions drive the "Client Answers" shown in the case and are distinct from PMT questionnaires (Reports module). On the web, the builder is QuestionnaireForm + QuestionBuilder embedded in ServiceForm.

8.5 Visibility, Geographic & National Rules

The victim catalog is filtered so survivors see only relevant, vetted help. Geographic filtering matches services against the victim's cityId/stateId; the isNational flag on a service bypasses geo filtering so nationwide programs always appear. Only isActive (and, for victims, verified) services surface. Categories, city and state act as additional filters. Agencies themselves are only discoverable when active and plan-enabled (Service Center rules), which further bounds which services appear.

8.6 Discovery, Search & Service-Code Lookup

Victims and staff find services by free-text search (searchKey), by category, and by geography; a service's human-readable service_code provides a precise, shareable lookup key (e.g. an advocate on a hotline can give a victim a code). Viewing a service detail increments viewCount, and the mobile client reports visitDuration (how long the victim spent), feeding engagement analytics.

Endpoints: GET /{admin,web,mobile}/victim-services (filters page, pageSize, searchKey, categoryId, cityId, stateId); GET /:uid (increments viewCount; for APP returns the victim's claim-state for that service); PATCH mark-visit-duration (mobile).

8.7 Victim Surfacing, Request-to-Connect & Case Intake

Active, visible services surface in the victim app as a browsable/searchable catalog with detail pages (title, descriptions, video, agency). From a detail, the victim can request to connect (a lightweight interest signal that emails the service center; deduped — a second request returns 409) or claim the service, which answers the service's claim questions and creates a case.

Claim creation (claimService, transactional) resolves the victim, enforces one ongoing claim per (service, victim), generates the case uid (CL<yy><month><uuid6>), inserts the question answers, and hands off to Case Management. Advocate-created claims (from VS360) auto-approve; victim-initiated claims start pending and notify category-assigned caseworkers.

Endpoints: PUT request-to-connect/:uid (mobile; emails center; 409 if already requested); POST /{web,mobile}/service-claims (claimService).

9. Detailed User & System Flows

9.1 NECX Admin Creates a Service Category

  1. Admin opens the category management screen in the NECX Admin console (context ADMIN, category.add permission).
  2. Admin enters a name and optional description and submits.
  3. Client sends POST /admin/services/categories.
  4. Backend runs allowOnly('ADMIN'), permission check, and Joi validation.
  5. Service dedupes by name; if a category with the same name exists, it returns a conflict error and no row is created.
  6. On success it inserts the service_categories row and writes an ActionLogs CREATE entry.
  7. The new category immediately appears in category selectors and catalog filters across web and mobile (via the pageLess list).
  8. Edge case: to rename, admin uses PATCH update/:uid; existing services keep their categoryId and now display the new name.

9.2 Agency Creates a Service with Claim Questions

  1. Agency admin/case worker opens VS360 → "My Services" → Create Service (services.add permission; context VS360).
  2. In ServiceForm they enter Title, optional YouTube Video URL, select a Category (ServiceCategorySelectField, from /services/categories), and write a Description (TipTap rich text); optionally a short description.
  3. In the Client Questionnaire builder (QuestionnaireForm + QuestionBuilder) they add questions, each with question text, a questionType (fill-in-the-blank / multiple-choice / true-false), an isRequired flag, and — for multiple-choice — an options[] list.
  4. They confirm via the confirmation modal; the client calls CreateService(payload) → POST /web/victim-services with serviceCenterId (the caller's agency), categoryId, title, shortDescription, description, videoUrl, and claimQuestions[].
  5. Backend runs allowOnly('VS360'), services.add permission, validation, and a duplicate guard on (title, categoryId, serviceCenterId).
  6. It generates a unique service_code, sets isVerified = true (VSMS-created), sets isActive, inserts the services row, and inserts each claim question into service_claim_questions.
  7. It writes an ActionLogs CREATE entry and returns the created service.
  8. The service now appears in "My Services" (Active) and, subject to visibility rules, in the victim catalog.
  9. Edge case: duplicate (title, categoryId, serviceCenterId) → rejected with a duplicate error; the author changes the title or category.

9.3 Agency Edits and Then Deactivates a Service

  1. From "My Services", staff choose Edit on a service row (services.edit); the update form loads current values including the questionnaire.
  2. They change wording, swap the category, add a new required question, or edit options, then confirm.
  3. Client calls UpdateService(uid, {data}) → PATCH /web/victim-services/update/:uid (scoped to own agency); backend validates, updates the services row and questionnaire, and audits the change.
  4. Existing claims retain the answers captured under the prior questionnaire; the read side merges the current question set with stored answers (unanswered → id: null).
  5. To pause the program, staff click Deactivate; client calls DeactivateService(uid) → POST /web/victim-services/deactive-activate/:uid, flipping isActive to false.
  6. The service disappears from the victim catalog but remains in "My Services" (Inactive) with all history intact; clicking again reactivates it.
  7. Edge case — Delete: staff choose Delete → DeleteService(uid) → DELETE /web/victim-services/:serviceUid. If the service has any cases (totalCases > 0) the backend rejects the delete; staff must deactivate instead. Only a zero-case service is hard-deleted.

9.4 Victim Discovers a Service (Search, Category, Code, Geo/National)

  1. In the victim app the survivor opens the services catalog; the client calls GET /mobile/victim-services with page/pageSize and any categoryId, cityId, stateId, searchKey.
  2. Backend returns only isActive services; geographic filtering matches the victim's cityId/stateId, while services flagged isNational = true are included regardless of location.
  3. The victim narrows by category or types a keyword; entering a service code in search resolves the specific service.
  4. The victim opens a service; the client calls GET /mobile/victim-services/:uid, which increments viewCount and (for APP) returns the victim's claim-state for that service (e.g. already requested).
  5. The detail page shows title, descriptions and the explainer videoUrl.
  6. As the victim reads, the app periodically calls PATCH /mobile/victim-services/mark-visit-duration to record visitDuration for engagement analytics.
  7. Edge case: a victim in a region with no matching local services still sees national services; if none match, an empty state prompts broadening the search.

9.5 Victim Requests a Service, Creating a Case

  1. From a service detail the victim taps Request to Connect or Claim/Request Service.
  2. Request-to-connect path: client sends PUT /mobile/victim-services/request-to-connect/:uid; the backend emails the service center and records the request. A repeat request returns 409 ("already requested"). This is a lightweight interest signal, not a case.
  3. Claim path: the app presents the service's claim questions; the victim answers, respecting isRequired. The client submits POST /mobile/service-claims (claimService) with serviceId, optional statementFromVictim, and questionAnswers[].
  4. Backend (transactional) resolves victimId from issuer.id, then runs the duplicate guard: if an ongoing (pending/approved) claim already exists for (service, victim), it rejects the request.
  5. It creates the claim with uid = CL<yy><month><uuid6> and status pending (victim-initiated), inserts the questionAnswers into claim_question_answers, creates the stats row, computes new-vs-returning client, and creates the PMT report snapshot.
  6. It emits claim-request to the caseworkers assigned to the service's category (via vsms_user_service_categories) and inserts service_center_notifications.
  7. Control passes to Case Management, where staff triage (approve/reject/resolve); the victim sees the new case and, on approval, gains a chat thread and published updates.
  8. Edge case — advocate-created (VS360): an advocate opens Create Case, selects the service (from their agency's categories), fills the intake (may bypass required questions), and submits POST /web/service-claims with shouldCreateUser/userData; the claim auto-approves, and if the victim is new a login code is generated for the victim to claim the case on mobile.

13. Non-Functional Requirements

  • Victim safety & privacy — the catalog must never leak victim identity; service content is public-facing to authenticated victims only. Claim questions must be designed proportionately; the platform should discourage collecting more sensitive data than needed at intake. Answers are stored per claim and access-controlled by Case Management RBAC.
  • Security — all mutations pass context + permission guards; VS360 tenancy isolation prevents cross-agency service access; responses are AES-encrypted; auth uses bearer + rotating refresh tokens; rate-limited via rateLimiters.standard.
  • Auditability — every service and category create/update/delete writes an ActionLogs row with source/IP/URL/context, supporting compliance and grant reviews.
  • Data integrity — duplicate services and categories are blocked; deletion is prohibited while cases exist; question-answer edits are full-replace and never orphan a claim; question changes must not corrupt historical answers.
  • Performance — list endpoints are paginated and filterable server-side; category lists offer a pageLess mode for selectors; detail fetches increment viewCount cheaply; search (incl. service-code) resolves interactively.
  • Availability & resilience — notification side effects (request-to-connect email, claim notifications) are wrapped defensively so a delivery failure never blocks a create/claim; bulk import runs per-agency transactions.
  • Localization & accessibility — victim-facing catalog strings support the platform's multi-language static translations; the web builder and app catalog must meet accessibility standards (keyboard, contrast, screen-reader labels on question controls).
  • Compliance / reporting — service-category assignment and claim-question design must support downstream PMT/grant reporting; category taxonomy stays centrally governed for cross-network comparability.

14. Analytics, Reporting & Notifications

14.1 Engagement Analytics

  • viewCount — incremented on each service detail fetch; measures interest per service.
  • visitDuration — victim reading time reported via mark-visit-duration; feeds the VS360 dashboard's time-on-service-page vs claim-submission-rate insight.
  • Top services / requests — service and category assignments feed dashboard analytics (Top 5 Services Requested, Claims by Service Category, service-provider rating over time) and grant/PMT reporting.
  • Discovery mix — share of detail views arriving via search vs category filter (product KPI).

14.2 Notifications

  • Request-to-connect → email to the service center (deduped; 409 on repeat).
  • New victim claim → claim-request socket event to vs360-user-<uid> for caseworkers assigned to the service's category, plus a service_center_notifications row (routing via vsms_user_service_categories).
  • Service/category CRUD themselves emit no real-time notifications; downstream case-lifecycle notifications (approve/reject/resolve, updates) are owned by Case Management (FCM + socket + victim_notifications).
