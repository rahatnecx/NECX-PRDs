2. Executive Summary

NECX is a coordinated victim-services platform that serves victims and survivors of crime and the agencies, advocates, and caseworkers who help them. Advocates carry high caseloads across shelter intake, hotline calls, court dates, service follow-ups, and inter-agency referrals. The Calendar & Appointments capability gives agency staff a single place to schedule and track their work: lightweight reminders and tasks that can be tied to a specific case (service claim) and therefore to a specific victim, presented in both a calendar and a list view, with an overlay of scheduled hotline callbacks so a caseworker sees everything on their plate in one screen.

This document specifies the Calendar & Appointments feature as it exists in the VS360 web application "Tools" area, backed by the backend Reminders module (/web/reminders). In the current platform, reminders are the scheduling primitive: a reminder carries a title, a message, a priority, a start/end date and time, an optional case link (claimId), and an optional agency-wide broadcast flag. Staff use them interchangeably as reminders, to-do tasks, and appointments. The calendar additionally surfaces hotline callbacks (owned by the Hotline module) as read-only events, so the calendar is a true unified day/week/month view of a caseworker's commitments.

This capability matters because coordination failures in victim services have real consequences: a missed court-support appointment, a dropped safety-planning follow-up, or an un-actioned callback can leave a survivor without help at a critical moment. A shared, case-linked, priority-aware calendar reduces those failures while keeping every scheduled item auditable and scoped to the correct agency.

This PRD is deliberately explicit about what is present versus planned. The backend Reminders module is a plain CRUD scheduling store with no dedicated appointments table, no recurrence engine, and no in-app/socket/FCM reminder notifications; "appointments" are modeled as reminders. Email/SMS notification toggles for tasks exist as user-level preferences surfaced on the Tools screen, but there is no scheduled reminder-delivery job in the Reminders module today. These gaps are recorded precisely in Section 5 (Scope) and Section 15 (Risks & Open Questions) so that stakeholders do not over-read current behavior.

3. Product Overview & Vision

3.1 Problem Statement

Advocates and caseworkers juggle time-sensitive commitments across multiple NECX modules — hotline callbacks, shelter follow-ups, court dates, service-claim milestones, and administrative tasks. Without a consolidated calendar, these commitments live in disparate places (or in the advocate's memory), which leads to missed follow-ups, double-booking, and loss of continuity when a case is handed between staff. Staff also need to attach a reminder to a specific case and victim so the reminder carries context ("Follow up with the victim on the protective-order filing"), and supervisors need to broadcast agency-wide reminders.

3.2 Product Vision

Provide agency staff with a unified, case-aware scheduling surface that consolidates reminders, tasks, and appointments alongside hotline callbacks in a single calendar/list experience. Every scheduled item is priority-ranked, optionally tied to a case (and therefore to a victim), scoped to the staff member's agency, and auditable. The long-term vision is dependable, notification-driven scheduling (timely alerts before an appointment, recurrence for standing commitments, and assignment to colleagues), building on today's reminders foundation.

3.3 Position in the NECX Ecosystem

NECX is a single unified backend serving four clients: the Victim mobile app, the Workspace mobile app, the VS360 web app (agency staff), and the NECX Admin console. Calendar & Appointments is a VS360 web, staff-facing capability. It is delivered under the VS360 "Tools" area and is gated by the tools.view permission. It reads and writes the backend Reminders module (/web/reminders, guarded by allowOnly('VS360')) and overlays read-only data from the Hotline module's callbacks (/hotlines/callbacks). Reminders link to the Cases/Claims domain through claimId (a service_claims row), which is how an appointment is associated with a case and its victim. It is a peer of the other Tools-area staff productivity modules (Staff Time, Notes Center).

4. Goals, Objectives & Success Metrics

4.1 Goals & Objectives

  • Give every agency staff member one calendar/list surface for their reminders, tasks, and appointments.
  • Allow any reminder/task/appointment to be tied to a case (claimId) so it carries victim and case context.
  • Support both a calendar view (month/week, colored by priority) and a list view of the same data.
  • Overlay scheduled hotline callbacks read-only so staff see all commitments together.
  • Let supervisors (super-admin) broadcast reminders to the entire agency.
  • Keep all scheduling data agency-scoped and gated behind the tools.view permission.
  • Provide a foundation for future notification-driven, recurring, and assignable appointments.

5. Scope

5.1 In Scope

  • Creating, editing, completing, and deleting staff reminders/tasks/appointments via the backend Reminders module (/web/reminders).
  • Combining a separate date field and time field into a single ISO datetime for start and end (via combineDateTime).
  • Associating a reminder/task/appointment with a case/claim (and therefore the victim) through claimId.
  • Priority ranking (LOW/MEDIUM/HIGH) and status lifecycle (PENDING → DONE).
  • Agency-wide broadcast reminders (isBroadcast), authored by super-admin.
  • The unified calendar view (FullCalendar, month/week) and the list view of reminders, with priority-based coloring.
  • Read-only overlay of scheduled hotline callbacks (overdue/today/upcoming) on the calendar, opening a read-only callback detail that can jump to the Hotline module.
  • Fetching the union of the staff member's own reminders and the agency's broadcast reminders, each graph-fetched with owner (userInfo) and case (claimInfo.victimInfo) context.
  • Access control via allowOnly('VS360') (platform gate) plus the tools.view permission.
  • Email/SMS notification preference toggles surfaced on the Tools screen (user-level notification preferences).

5.2 Out of Scope

  • A dedicated appointments/calendar table. There is no separate appointment or calendar entity; "appointments" are reminders. (Recorded as an enhancement in Risks.)
  • Recurrence / recurring appointments. The Reminders module has no recurrence fields or expansion engine. Each reminder is a single-instance event.
  • Assignment of a reminder/task to another staff member. Reminders are owned by their creating user (userId); the only cross-user visibility mechanism is isBroadcast (agency-wide). There is no per-assignee task delegation in the Reminders module. (Hotline callbacks do carry an assignedToUserId, but that assignment is owned by the Hotline module, not Reminders.)
  • Scheduled reminder notifications (in-app / Socket.IO / FCM / email delivery at reminder time). The Reminders module performs no Socket.IO, FCM, or email delivery — it is a plain CRUD store. The email/SMS toggles on the Tools screen set user notification preferences; they do not schedule per-reminder alerts.
  • Creating or editing hotline callbacks from the calendar. Callbacks are read-only on the calendar; their lifecycle lives in the Hotline module.
  • Victim-facing calendar. This is a VS360 staff surface only; victims do not see or receive these reminders.
  • Cross-agency scheduling. Reminders are strictly scoped to the staff member's own service center.
⚠ Partial feature
The "Appointments" concept, recurrence, staff assignment/delegation, and reminder-time notifications are only partially present or absent in the current platform. Appointments are implemented as case-linked reminders; recurrence, delegation, and reminder-time delivery are not implemented. See Section 15 for enhancement tracking.

7. System Context & Architecture

7.1 Tech Stack

  • Frontend (VS360 web): React + Vite + Mantine UI + TanStack Query + Redux. The calendar uses FullCalendar (RemindersCalendarView.jsx) with a parallel list view (RemindersListView.jsx). Task authoring uses CreateTask.jsx; callback overlay detail uses CallbackEventModal.jsx. The Tools page is src/pages/Tools/Tools.jsx; supporting components live under src/components/pages/Tools/.
  • API client: all calls go through axios() (src/services/axios.js); the reminders service is src/services/reminders.js (base /reminders); callbacks are read via src/services/hotlineService.js (/hotlines/callbacks). Responses follow the platform shape { status, msg, data } and are read via response.data.data.
  • Backend: Express (ESM) + Objection/Knex over PostgreSQL. The Reminders module is src/apps/reminders/reminders.{routes,controller,services}.js; model Reminders (table reminders, src/model/v2/). All routes are mounted under the global /v2 API prefix.

7.2 How It Talks to the Backend

The VS360 client calls GET /reminders on load and renders both calendar and list views from the same dataset. Create/update/complete/delete map to POST /reminders, PATCH /reminders/{uid}, PATCH /reminders/complete/{uid}, and DELETE /reminders/{uid}. Callbacks are fetched separately per tab (overdue/today/upcoming) via the Hotline service and merged into the calendar client-side; callback fetch failures are handled silently so the calendar still renders reminders.

7.3 Real-Time & Notifications

The Reminders module does not use Socket.IO, FCM, or email. It is a plain CRUD scheduling store. Real-time behavior in the calendar is limited to TanStack Query invalidation/refetch after a mutation. The only notification-related UI is the email/SMS notification preference toggles on the Tools screen, which write to /notifications/* (user preference endpoints), not to the Reminders module. (By contrast, the overlaid hotline callbacks are backed by the Hotline module, which does emit hotline:callback-updated over Socket.IO to the agency; that behavior is owned by the Hotline PRD.)

7.4 Tenancy

Every reminder carries userId (owner) and serviceCenterId (agency). The middleware chain is rate-limiter → auth (req.issuer) → allowOnly('VS360') → permission (tools.view) → controller → service → model. A VS360 user only ever sees reminders they own plus broadcast reminders for their own service center; agency scoping is enforced from issuer.serviceCenterInfo.id. SUPER_ADMIN bypasses fine-grained permission checks.

8. Functional Requirements

8.1 Reminder / Task / Appointment CRUD

Reminders are the single scheduling primitive: they act as reminders, to-do tasks, and appointments. A reminder carries a title, message, priority, start/end datetime, optional case link, and optional broadcast flag. On create, the client sends separate date and time fields; the service combines each date+time pair into a single ISO datetime using combineDateTime (which handles 12-hour and 24-hour input and avoids timezone shifting). Priority defaults to MEDIUM and is upper-cased; status defaults to PENDING.

9. Detailed User & System Flows

9.1 Create an appointment tied to a case

  1. An advocate opens the VS360 Tools screen; the client verifies allowOnly('VS360') + tools.view (route-gated) and loads GET /reminders.
  2. The advocate selects a day cell in the calendar (or clicks "+") to open CreateTask.jsx.
  3. The advocate enters a title (e.g., "Court-support appointment") and a message, and picks a priority (LOW/MEDIUM/HIGH).
  4. The advocate sets a start date and start time and an end date and end time (a time range for the appointment).
  5. In the Case field, the advocate associates the appointment with a non-resolved claim; the client sends the corresponding claimId. (Leaving Case empty creates a general reminder.)
  6. If the user is a super-admin and wants the item visible agency-wide, they enable the broadcast switch (isBroadcast=true).
  7. On submit, the client calls POST /reminders with title, message, startDate, endDate, startTime, endTime, priority, isBroadcast, claimId.
  8. The service combines each date+time pair into an ISO datetime via combineDateTime, upper-cases priority (default MEDIUM), sets status PENDING, stamps userId and serviceCenterId from issuer, and inserts the reminders row.
  9. The controller returns { status, data }; the client invalidates/refetches reminders. The new appointment appears on the calendar colored by priority; because claimId is set, the fetch join claimInfo.victimInfo shows the linked victim on the event.

9.2 View the calendar (unified with callbacks)

  1. The advocate opens Tools; the client fires GET /reminders and, in parallel, GetCallbacks({tab}) for overdue/today/upcoming.
  2. GET /reminders returns the union of the advocate's own reminders plus agency broadcast reminders, each graph-fetched with userInfo and claimInfo.victimInfo.
  3. The calendar (FullCalendar, month/week) renders reminders colored by priority and overlays callbacks as teal, phone-icon events.
  4. If the callback fetch fails, the failure is swallowed silently and the calendar still renders reminders.
  5. The advocate toggles between Calendar and List views of the same reminder data; optional priority/status filters narrow the list.
  6. Clicking a reminder opens CreateTask in edit mode (see 9.5); clicking a callback opens CallbackEventModal (read-only), which offers a jump to the Hotline callback.

9.3 Receive a reminder notification (current behavior and gap)

  1. Current baseline: The Reminders module performs no scheduled delivery — there is no in-app, Socket.IO, FCM, or email notification fired at a reminder's start time. Staff become aware of due items by opening Tools and reading the calendar/list (priority colors, overdue position).
  2. The only notification controls on the Tools screen are user-level email/SMS preference toggles, which write to /notifications/*; they set preferences and do not schedule per-reminder alerts.
  3. Related, but out of module: scheduled hotline callbacks overlaid on the calendar are backed by the Hotline module, which does emit hotline:callback-updated over Socket.IO to the agency and refreshes the callbacks view; that behavior is owned by the Hotline PRD, not Reminders.
  4. Intended future flow (deferred — see Risks): a scheduled job would, ahead of a reminder's startDate, insert a service_center_notifications row, emit to the owner's vs360-user-<uid> room (and broadcasts to service-center-<id>), push via FCM, and optionally send templated email via the BullMQ queue — mirroring the platform's standard notification delivery pattern.

9.4 Complete or cancel a task

  1. From the calendar or list, the advocate opens a reminder/task they own.
  2. Complete: the advocate marks it done; the client calls PATCH /reminders/complete/{uid}; the service sets status DONE. The item re-renders as completed after refetch.
  3. Cancel / remove: the advocate deletes the task; the client calls DELETE /reminders/{uid}; the service hard-deletes the row. The event disappears from the calendar/list after refetch.
  4. There is no separate "cancelled" status in the Reminders module — cancellation is a delete; completion is the DONE status. (Hotline callbacks, by contrast, have their own complete/cancel actions in the Hotline module and are read-only here.)
  5. All mutations invalidate the TanStack Query cache so calendar and list stay consistent.

9.5 Edit / reschedule a reminder

  1. The advocate clicks a reminder event on the calendar (or a row in the list); CreateTask opens pre-filled in edit mode.
  2. The advocate changes any of: title, message, priority, status (edit only), Case (claimId), start/end date, or time range.
  3. On submit, the client calls PATCH /reminders/{uid}; the service recombines start/end date+time, strips the non-column startTime/endTime helper fields, and updates the row.
  4. Rescheduling is simply changing the start/end datetimes on the same reminder; there is no recurrence expansion (each reminder is a single instance).

9.6 Broadcast an agency-wide reminder (supervisor)

  1. A super-admin opens CreateTask and enables the broadcast switch (visible only to super-admin).
  2. On submit (POST /reminders with isBroadcast=true), the reminder is stored against the supervisor's userId and serviceCenterId.
  3. Every user in that service center then receives the reminder in their GET /reminders union (own reminders + agency broadcasts), so it appears on all agency staff calendars.

11.3 External Integrations

The Reminders module itself has no external integrations — no Twilio, no FCM, no Azure Blob, no OpenAI/Anthropic, and no email. It is a plain PostgreSQL-backed CRUD store served over the unified /v2 API. The calendar's callback overlay depends indirectly on the Hotline module (Twilio-backed), but only reads already-persisted callback rows. Any future reminder-time delivery would use the platform's standard channels (Socket.IO, FCM, and the BullMQ templated-email queue) — see Risks.

12. RBAC & Permissions

The fine-grained permission tools.view (from the VS360 module map vs360Modules.tools = {view}) gates the entire Tools area, including the calendar and reminders. SUPER_ADMIN bypasses the fine-grained check.

13. Non-Functional Requirements

  • Security & access control. Every route is protected by allowOnly('VS360') + tools.view; ownership and serviceCenterId come from the authenticated issuer, never from client input, preventing cross-agency or cross-user reminder access. Access tokens are Bearer JWTs with sliding refresh.
  • Privacy & victim safety. Case-linked reminders expose victim context (claimInfo.victimInfo). This data must never leak outside the owning agency; broadcast reminders are agency-scoped and should avoid embedding sensitive victim identifiers in a title/message that will be seen agency-wide. Reminders are staff-only and are never surfaced to victims.
  • Data integrity. combineDateTime must produce timezone-stable ISO datetimes across 12h/24h input; on update, non-column helper fields (startTime/endTime) must be stripped before write.
  • Performance. GET /reminders returns a bounded per-user + per-agency-broadcast set; p95 calendar month render < 1.5 s after the response. Callback overlay fetches must not block reminder rendering (parallel + silent failure).
  • Availability. The module is stateless CRUD over PostgreSQL; availability tracks the unified backend. A callback-service outage degrades gracefully (calendar still shows reminders).
  • Auditability. Consistent with platform conventions, create/update/delete operations are expected to be captured in the platform audit trail (ActionLogs) for compliance; reminders tied to cases contribute to the case's activity picture. (See Open Question OQ-3 on confirming audit coverage for this module.)
  • Localization. UI strings follow the VS360 localization approach; reminder content is free text entered by staff.
  • Accessibility. The calendar (FullCalendar) and list views should meet the VS360 accessibility baseline (keyboard navigation, color-plus-text for priority so color is not the sole signal).
  • Compliance / reporting. Case-linked scheduling supports advocate accountability and continuity of care; while reminders are not a PMT/grant reporting source themselves, they reinforce timely follow-up that underpins grant-funded service delivery.

14. Analytics, Reporting & Notifications

14.1 Dashboards & KPIs

The Reminders module does not ship a dedicated analytics dashboard. Operationally meaningful signals available from the data include: count of PENDING vs DONE reminders per staff member, overdue reminders (past endDate, still PENDING), case-linkage rate (claimId present), and priority distribution. These map to the success metrics in Section 4 and can be derived from GET /reminders.
