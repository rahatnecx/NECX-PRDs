2. Executive Summary

NECX serves victims and survivors of crime and the agencies, advocates, and caseworkers who help them. Every funded victim-services agency must repeatedly prove its impact — to boards, to funders, and to state and federal programs such as VOCA and VAWA — through Performance Measurement Tool (PMT) submissions, grant-template spreadsheets, and narrative program reports. This reporting work is manual, error-prone, and pulls advocates away from client-facing work.

The NECX AI Report Builder is the platform's reporting subsystem and its two AI-assisted authoring surfaces. It spans (a) the OAG/PMT data pipeline — grants, data-collection questionnaires, per-case answers, and PMT demographic rows; (b) Excel-template-based generated grant reports edited in an in-browser Luckysheet spreadsheet with a conservative, human-approval AI cell editor; and (c) the flagship NECX AI Report Builder, a JSON-schema-driven, publication-quality custom-report generator with a natural-language chat, agency-brain and live-data context, file-upload metric extraction, and a post-generation accuracy validator that flags value conflicts and possible hallucinations.

Two deliberately different AI postures coexist. The PMT spreadsheet editor is conservative by design: it uses OpenAI gpt-4o, returns only surgical cell edits, enforces an 80–100% confidence-only fill policy, and requires human approval before edits are applied. The custom-report AI builder is generative: it produces an entire structured report from live agency data plus uploaded files, using OpenAI gpt-4o or Anthropic claude-haiku-4-5, and layers prompt-level accuracy rules with a programmatic validator. Because reporting drives funding and compliance decisions, accuracy, provenance, and auditability are first-class concerns throughout this document.

The subsystem is available to agency staff on the VS360 web app and the Workspace mobile app, gated behind the reports.pmt_reports permission. This PRD specifies the reporting suite end to end: functional behavior, step-by-step flows, data model, endpoints, integrations, RBAC, non-functional requirements, and the known accuracy risks that must be tracked as open questions.

3. Product Overview & Vision

3.1 Problem statement

Victim-services agencies live and die by their reporting. Funders require PMT/VOCA/VAWA submissions on funder-supplied Excel templates; boards want quarterly and annual impact summaries; program managers need grant-progress reports. Today advocates re-key case data into spreadsheets by hand, reconcile demographic counts across systems, and assemble narrative reports under deadline pressure. The work is slow, duplicative, and prone to transcription errors — and an error in a grant report can jeopardize funding. Agencies need reporting that draws directly from the case data they already capture, assists authoring with AI, and makes every number traceable to its source.

3.2 Product vision

The NECX AI Report Builder turns the case data agencies already collect into accurate, funder-ready reports with minimal manual effort. Grants define what funders need; linked questionnaires collect that data automatically as staff work cases; PMT rows capture demographics per case; and AI assists two ways — surgically filling funder spreadsheets under human approval, and generating complete, styled custom reports from live data and uploaded files. The north star is trustworthy automation: the platform accelerates reporting without ever fabricating numbers, and it surfaces provenance and accuracy checks so staff can verify before they submit.

3.3 Where this sits in the NECX ecosystem

NECX is a single unified backend serving four clients: the Victim mobile app, the Workspace staff mobile app, the VS360 agency web app, and the NECX Admin console. Reporting is an agency (VS360) capability. It consumes data produced across the platform — service claims (cases), PMT demographic rows, questionnaire answers, referrals, partnerships, chat/inquiry threads, and staff time entries — and it is authored on two clients: the VS360 web app (full reporting suite, Luckysheet editor, drag-drop Grant Report Builder, Custom Report Builder) and the Workspace mobile app (PMT/grants management plus an on-device AI custom-report builder with PDF export). All web AI calls are backend-routed with server-held provider keys; the current mobile AI builder calls providers directly from the device (flagged in Risks).

4. Goals, Objectives & Success Metrics

4.1 Goals & objectives

  • Reduce the time and manual effort required to produce PMT, grant, and board/program reports by drawing directly from live case and PMT data.
  • Provide two fit-for-purpose AI surfaces: a conservative, human-approved spreadsheet editor for funder templates, and a generative custom-report builder for narrative/analytical reports.
  • Guarantee numeric trustworthiness: the AI must never fabricate, round, or substitute numbers; uploaded-file values are authoritative; and a post-generation validator flags conflicts and possible hallucinations.
  • Make every reported datapoint traceable to a source (database, uploaded file, user input, or AI-generated) for audit and compliance.
  • Support the full grant lifecycle: define a grant + Excel template, link data-collection questionnaires, collect answers as cases progress, generate reports, edit them, and export.
  • Deliver reporting on both VS360 web and Workspace mobile, gated by a single reports.pmt_reports permission.

5. Scope

5.1 In Scope

  • PMT reports: paged, agency-scoped PMT demographic rows (pmt_report) with per-row editing; grant-filtered views.
  • Questionnaires: CRUD of data-collection questionnaires and questions; per-claim answer submission; the full question×case answer matrix that feeds the AI editor.
  • Grants: grant CRUD with Excel template upload/replace/download; questionnaire linkage; field-distribution analytics.
  • Generated reports: creation by copying a grant's Excel template; status management; Excel overwrite; deletion (cascading chat logs + file); download.
  • PMT AI spreadsheet editor: OpenAI gpt-4o surgical cell editor with confidence scoring, questions-before-edits, and human approval (requiresApproval).
  • Report Builder AI: lightweight advisory chat (gpt-4o-mini) for the drag-drop Grant Report Builder canvas.
  • NECX AI Report Builder (custom reports): JSON-schema report generation via custom-reports/generate-chat (gpt-4o / claude-haiku-4-5); agency-brain + live-data context; chat iteration; manual section editing; status (draft/final); duplication; chat history.
  • File upload + accuracy pipeline: server-side file parse (doc/docx/pdf/xlsx/xls/csv/txt), metric extraction with provenance, verified-metrics reference table injection, and the post-generation accuracy validator (conflicts/warnings) and data-source mapper.
  • Luckysheet grant editor and the drag-drop Grant Report Builder on VS360 web.
  • PDF export: server-side Puppeteer render (web) and client-side PDF (web print + mobile pdf/printing).
  • Workspace mobile reporting hub and on-device AI custom-report builder with PDF export.
  • RBAC via reports.pmt_reports; per-resource ownership scoping (grants/reports/questionnaires by service center; custom reports by user).

5.2 Out of Scope

  • Case management, intake, referrals, partnerships, notes-center, inquiries, hotline, and shelter features (covered by their own PRDs; referenced here only where they feed reporting data).
  • The Victim (client) mobile app — reporting is a staff-only capability.
  • NECX Admin console reporting/administration beyond platform AI-provider configuration.
  • Automated funder submission/transmission (e.g., direct upload to OAG/PMT portals) — reports are generated and exported; transmission is manual.
  • The "Case Management AI Agent" (named in mobile branches but not implemented — see Risks).
  • Offline reporting on mobile (no local cache is implemented).
  • Scanned/image PDF OCR for file uploads (unsupported; text-based files only).

7. System Context & Architecture

7.1 Tech stack

  • Backend: Node.js + Express (ESM), Objection.js/Knex over PostgreSQL, Socket.IO, Redis, BullMQ. knexSnakeCaseMappers maps snake_case columns to camelCase in JS. Reports live under src/apps/reports and are mounted at /web/reports.
  • VS360 web: React 18 + Vite, Mantine v7, Redux Toolkit + redux-persist, TanStack React Query v4, react-router-dom v6. Charts via ApexCharts/Recharts/@mantine/charts; maps via Leaflet. Spreadsheets via Luckysheet + LuckyExcel + ExcelJS. Drag-drop via @dnd-kit. Rich text via TipTap. Realtime via socket.io-client.
  • Workspace mobile: Flutter (Dart), Riverpod, GoRouter, Dio, Socket.IO client, fl_chart, and pdf/printing for report export.
  • AI providers: OpenAI (gpt-4o, gpt-4o-mini) via src/loaders/openai.js; Anthropic (claude-haiku-4-5-20251001) via raw axios to api.anthropic.com/v1/messages.
  • File/PDF: server-side parsers (word-extractor, mammoth, pdf-parse, ExcelJS, CSV), and Puppeteer (puppeteer-core + system Chromium) for server PDF export.

7.2 How clients talk to the backend

Every reporting call is issued to the unified API under /v2/web/reports/.... Requests carry Authorization: Bearer <access>, x-refresh-token, X-Netlify-Secret, and X-Environment. Response bodies are AES-encrypted by the encryption middleware and decrypted client-side. The entire /web/reports tree is gated by allowOnly('VS360') and rate-limited by rateLimiters.pmtApi (~15 requests / 5s). Services return { status, success, data|message }; controllers echo the status.

All AI provider keys are held server-side on the web path — the browser never sees an API key; the backend fetches the data, builds the prompt, calls the provider, and returns structured results. The Workspace mobile app currently calls Anthropic/OpenAI directly from the device using embedded keys (flagged in Risks as a security gap; the server AI chat endpoints exist and should supersede this).

7.3 Real-time, tenancy, and data lineage

  • Tenancy: agency-scoped resources (grants, generated reports, questionnaires, PMT rows) are filtered by issuer.serviceCenterInfo.id. Custom reports are owned by the creating user (custom_reports.userId) and scoped by user, not agency.
  • Data lineage: grants → linked questionnaires (by service category) → per-claim answers → the answer matrix + pmt_report demographic rows → the AI editor context and the custom-report live-data/agency-brain context.
  • Auditing: create/update/delete operations across the platform write ActionLogs rows for a full compliance trail.
  • Notifications: the reporting subsystem is primarily request/response; it does not emit dedicated socket/FCM report-ready events in the current implementation (see §14).

8. Functional Requirements

8.1 PMT reports

PMT reports are per-case demographic/service rows (pmt_report) created automatically when a claim is created and when a case is cloned by a referral. getPmtReport requires issuer.serviceCenterInfo, joins pmt_report → service_claims → services, scopes to serviceCenterId, and eager-loads claimInfo.[victimInfo, serviceInfo]. It supports page/pageSize, pageLess (returns all rows — used by the AI data services), startDate/endDate, and grantId (resolves the grant's linked questionnaire categories and filters claims to those service categories; returns nothing if the grant has no categories). updatePmtReport verifies the row's claim's serviceCenterId matches the issuer (403 otherwise) before patching.

PMT fields: isNew, raceEtnicity(+Other), gender(+Other), age, victimizationType(+Other), specialClassification(+Other), typeOfService(+Other), typeOfServiceSubCategory(+Other), serviceUnmet, surveyReceived, surveyCompleted, victimCompensation.

8.2 Questionnaires & answer matrix

Data-collection questionnaires drive PMT/grant data capture. Tables: pmt_questionnaires, pmt_questionnaire_questions, pmt_questionnaire_categories (M2M questionnaire↔service category), pmt_questionnaire_answers, pmt_grant_questionnaires (M2M grant↔questionnaire). CRUD is agency-scoped with soft-delete (is_active=false). Questions store questionText, questionType, options (JSON), questionOrder with types fill-in-the-blank / multiple-choice / true-false. Updates use a smart upsert that preserves answers for kept questions and only deletes answers for removed questions.

getQuestionnaireForClaim(claimId) finds active questionnaires whose category matches the claim's service category and which are linked to an active grant, returning a merged (composite) question list plus existing answers. submitQuestionnaireAnswers(claimId,{answers}) maps each questionId to its questionnaire, deletes prior answers for those questionnaires on that claim, and inserts new ones. getQuestionnaireAnswersMatrix(issuer) builds { questions[], cases[], answers{qId:{caseId:{answer}}}, applicability{qId:{caseId:bool}} } across the agency, including only questions linked to an active grant — this matrix is the primary data source for the PMT AI editor.

8.3 Grants

A grant = name + description + an Excel template file (.xlsx/.xls only) + linked questionnaires. Templates are uploaded to cloud storage (Azure Blob in prod/staging, MinIO in dev) via fileService, with metadata in file_uploads (context='GRANT'). CRUD is transactional and agency-scoped. 8.5 PMT AI spreadsheet editor (conservative, human-approved)

The PMT AI editor fills/corrects the Excel generated report rendered in Luckysheet, returning surgical cell edits — never a whole document. Provider: OpenAI gpt-4o (temperature 0.7, max_tokens 4096) via src/loaders/openai.js, 30s timeout, retry on 429 (exp backoff, max 2), requires OPENAI_API_KEY.

processAiChatMessage validates the key, saves the user message to pmt_report_chat_logs, builds context, calls OpenAI with [system, ...history, user], parses JSON, saves the assistant message, and returns {message, edits[], rowInsertions[], questions[], requiresApproval}. Context (fetchEnrichedContext) = the questionnaire answer matrix + per-case enriched details (status, new/returning client, service, victim demographics, referral status) + agency KPIs (fetchAgencyKpis: case/inquiry/referral stats via AgencyReferrals.getAgencyReferralStats, client demographics, top services, satisfaction), optionally tagged/highlighted cell ranges and current spreadsheet data, assembled by buildAiContext.

The system prompt is a large XML "EXPERT PMT DOCUMENT EDITOR" enforcing a zero-error / 80–100% confidence-only fill policy: never guess or infer beyond explicit data; protect formulas; match header + row-label + case-ID + case creation date (temporal placement into Q1/Q2/FY); questions-before-edits (if unclear, return empty edits/rowInsertions and populate questions); requiresApproval:true unless the user explicitly says "auto fill"/"automatically"; limited victimization inference (≥90% only when service name is explicit); no duplicate data across rows; ID unification (Case/Client/Claim IDs are synonymous). Every edit carries oldState (including formula preservation) for revert.

8.7 NECX AI Report Builder — custom reports

The flagship generator produces a structured JSON report (header + typed sections + footer) rendered as a publication-quality report and exportable to PDF.

Storage (CustomReport → custom_reports): id, uid, userId (owner), serviceCenterId, title, reportData (JSON — the full report), status (draft|final), organizationName, dateRange (JSON), isDeleted, timestamps. All service ops scope by userId. status='final' locks non-AI edits (423 unless aiAssisted:true, which may only change reportData). CRUD: list, create, get, update, soft-delete, duplicate ("(Copy)", resets to draft), updateReportStatus; chat history via getChatHistoryByReportId.

Report JSON schema: { header:{reportTitle, orgName, dateRange, style:{backgroundColor,color,accentColor}}, sections:[...], footer:{generatedDate, pageNumber} }. Section types: kpi, bar_chart, pie_chart/donut, line_chart, stacked_bar, data_table, narrative, section_divider, two_column, metric_comparison. Manually authored sections carry _userAdded:true + _id and must be preserved verbatim by the AI.

Generation (generateReportChat): providers are OpenAI gpt-4o by default (response_format: json_object, max_tokens 16384) or Anthropic claude-haiku-4-5-20251001 when AI_PROVIDER=claude (anthropic-version: 2023-06-01, max_tokens 16384). Keys stay server-side. Context assembly:

  1. Fetch chat history (last 6 turns) from custom_report_chat_logs; prepend.
  2. fetchReportContextData(serviceCenterId, start, end) — live agency data from PMT rows (getPmtReport pageLess): KPIs, demographics by race/gender/age/classification, service breakdown, inquiries (from chat_threads counts), staff time (from staff_time_entries); formatted by formatDataForPrompt.
  3. fetchAgencyBrain(serviceCenterId) — agency identity: profile+plan, active services+categories, staff roles/headcount, accepted-partnership count, active grants, all-time claim stats; formatted by formatAgencyBrainForPrompt ("AGENCY PROFILE").
  4. Load previously uploaded files for the report (reportFileUploadRepository) and any new files on incoming messages.

System prompt = INSTRUCTIONS + (file-override block if files present) + DATA_RULES + AGENCY PROFILE + COMPONENT_CATALOG + DESIGN_GUIDELINES + EDITING_GUIDELINES + SECTION_REFERENCE (skipped for large reports with files) + AVAILABLE SYSTEM DATA + VERIFIED METRICS TABLE. Large-report handling: if sections ≥ 40, the current report is reduced to a skeleton (buildReportSkeleton strips chart/table/narrative bodies but keeps structure and full _userAdded sections) and the AI repopulates from DB/file data. Output: parse JSON (with brace-matching fallback), save new user+assistant messages + file uploads, run accuracy validation, return { message, reportUpdate, changedSections[], detectedDateRange, dataDateLabel, extractedMetrics, validationResult }.

8.8 File upload, metric extraction & accuracy validation

Files arrive as pre-parsed text (browser converts Excel to a pipe-separated table) or as buffers. processFilesForMetricExtraction parses each (parseTextTableToStructure/parseExcelStructured/parseCSVStructured/parseDocxStructured), persists to report_file_uploads (with parsed structure), and extracts numeric metrics with {key, value, cellRef, sheetName, header, rowLabel} provenance. buildMetricsReferenceTable renders a "VERIFIED METRICS FROM UPLOADED FILES" ASCII table injected into the system prompt; the prompt mandates the AI use ONLY these values and never hallucinate/round/substitute.

After generation, validateReportAgainstMetrics(reportUpdate, extractedMetrics) extracts every numeric value from the produced report (KPIs, chart data, table cells) and compares to the extracted metrics, producing conflicts (value mismatch > 0.01, severity high) and warnings (values not found in files = possible hallucination). formatValidationReport renders a PASS/FAIL summary returned to the client. data-source-mapper.service.js provides per-datapoint source-audit records (source_type ∈ database|file|user_input|ai_generated, confidence 0–1). The server-side parse endpoint handles doc (word-extractor), docx (mammoth), pdf (pdf-parse), xlsx/xls, csv, and txt for formats the browser can't parse.

Accuracy posture. Two layers: (a) prompt-level — strong "trust is everything / never fabricate / match numbers exactly / show zeros / file data overrides system data" rules; (b) programmatic — post-generation numeric cross-check with conflict/hallucination flags. Validation is advisory (returned to the client, not enforced server-side); file values are designed to win over DB values; conflicts are reported but do not block saving.

8.10 Custom Report Builder UI (VS360 web) & PDF export

The Custom Report Builder has three views: landing (saved reports + templates), setup (guided form), builder (live preview + AI chat). Report types (REPORT_TYPES): Board Report, Grant Progress (VOCA/VAWA-aligned), Quarterly Program Summary, Annual Impact, Custom. 9. Detailed User & System Flows

9.1 Build a custom AI report end-to-end (VS360 web)

  1. A user with reports.pmt_reports opens Reports & Analytics and launches the Custom Report Builder (landing view).
  2. In setup, the user selects a report type (e.g., Board Report), enters title/organization/date range/audience, ticks the sections to include (each tagged live/db/upload), and adds free-text notes.
  3. The UI converts the form into a natural-language prompt and enters the builder (empty or template preview + chat).
  4. The client calls POST /reports/custom-reports/generate-chat with messages, currentReportData, dateRange, serviceCenterId, organizationName, and reportId.
  5. The backend fetches the last 6 chat turns, fetchReportContextData (live PMT/agency data), and fetchAgencyBrain (agency profile), loads any uploaded files, and assembles the system prompt (INSTRUCTIONS + DATA_RULES + AGENCY PROFILE + catalogs + AVAILABLE SYSTEM DATA + VERIFIED METRICS TABLE).
  6. If the current report has ≥ 40 sections, the backend skeletonizes it (preserving _userAdded sections) before calling the provider.
  7. The backend calls OpenAI gpt-4o (or Claude if AI_PROVIDER=claude) with response_format: json_object / max_tokens 16384.
  8. The backend parses the JSON report (with brace-matching fallback), persists the new user+assistant messages and file uploads, and runs the accuracy validator.
  9. The response returns { message, reportUpdate, changedSections, detectedDateRange, dataDateLabel, extractedMetrics, validationResult }.
  10. The builder renders the report JSON (KPIs/charts/tables/narrative), highlights and scrolls to changedSections, and shows the PASS/FAIL validation summary.
  11. The user iterates via chat, edits sections manually (preserved as _userAdded), and uses undo/redo as needed.
  12. The user saves the report (POST/PUT /reports/custom-reports[/:id]); drafts also autosave to localStorage every 30s.
  13. When satisfied, the user sets status final (PATCH /:id/status), which locks non-AI edits.

9.2 Upload a file for metric extraction

  1. In the builder's chat, the user attaches a file (.xlsx/.xls/.csv/.pdf/.doc/.docx/.txt).
  2. For .txt, the browser sends text directly; for other formats the client calls POST /reports/custom-reports/parse-file (multipart) and the server extracts text + structured tables (doc/docx/pdf/xlsx/csv).
  3. The parsed content is attached to the outgoing chat message to generate-chat.
  4. processFilesForMetricExtraction parses each file, persists it to report_file_uploads with parsed structure, and extracts numeric metrics with {key, value, cellRef, sheetName, header, rowLabel} provenance.
  5. buildMetricsReferenceTable renders the "VERIFIED METRICS FROM UPLOADED FILES" table and injects it (plus a file-override block) into the system prompt.
  6. The AI is instructed to use ONLY these values (no rounding, no substitution) and that file values override system/DB data.
  7. Generation proceeds as in §9.1; extracted metrics are returned in extractedMetrics for downstream validation.

9.3 Accuracy validation & human review

  1. After generation, validateReportAgainstMetrics(reportUpdate, extractedMetrics) extracts every numeric value from the produced report (KPIs, chart data, table cells).
  2. Each report value is compared to the extracted file metrics: a mismatch > 0.01 becomes a high-severity conflict; a value not found in any file becomes a warning (possible hallucination).
  3. formatValidationReport builds a PASS/FAIL summary; data-source-mapper records per-datapoint source type (database/file/user_input/ai_generated) with a confidence score.
  4. The validationResult is returned to the client and displayed alongside the report — but is advisory only and does not block saving.
  5. The reviewer inspects each conflict (report vs file value + provenance) and each warning, then fixes the report via chat or manual section edits.
  6. Process gate: the report should not be marked final until high-severity conflicts are resolved and warnings reviewed (organizational control, not a server enforcement).

9.4 Generate and fill a PMT/grant report (Luckysheet + AI editor)

  1. In Report Management, the user selects a Grant (remembered in localStorage).
  2. The user clicks New Report → POST /reports/generated-reports {reportName, grantId}; the backend clones the grant's Excel template into a new report (status='in_progress').
  3. The editor opens: LuckysheetEditor loads the report Excel (GET /reports/grants/:id/file → LuckyExcel.transformExcelToLucky); the AI ChatInterface loads history (GET /reports/ai/chat/:reportId/history).
  4. The user asks the AI to fill data (e.g., "populate Q2 victim counts"). The client posts POST /reports/ai/chat.
  5. The backend saves the user message, builds enriched context (answer matrix + per-case details + agency KPIs + optional tagged cell ranges + current sheet data), and calls OpenAI gpt-4o.
  6. The AI returns {message, edits[], rowInsertions[], questions[], requiresApproval}. If confidence is below threshold or data is ambiguous, edits/rowInsertions are empty and questions are populated.
  7. If questions are present, the user answers them in chat and the AI re-attempts.
  8. With requiresApproval:true, the user reviews each proposed edit (with oldState shown) and approves; the copilot applies edits to Luckysheet cells (setCellValue/setCellFormat, formulas protected, temporal placement by case creation date).
  9. The user saves the sheet → PUT /reports/generated-reports/:reportId/file (ExcelJS rebuild); background autosave also runs.
  10. When done, the user sets status completed (PATCH /reports/generated-reports/:reportId/status).
  11. Any misplaced edit can be reverted using the stored oldState.

9.5 Set up a grant and collect data (prerequisite flow)

  1. In PMT Grants, the user creates a grant: name, description, linked questionnaires, and the funder's Excel template (POST /reports/grants, multipart).
  2. Linked questionnaires (by service category) begin collecting data as staff work cases: getQuestionnaireForClaim surfaces the applicable questions on the case's PMT tab; answers are submitted via POST /reports/pmt-questionnaires/claim/:claimId/answers.
  3. PMT rows accrue automatically per case; the answer matrix (GET /reports/questionnaire-answers-matrix) aggregates question×case answers for reporting/AI context.
  4. When reporting time comes, the grant's template + collected data feed the generated-report and custom-report flows (§9.4, §9.1).

9.6 Export a report

  1. Custom report (web): the user clicks Export → the client either renders PDF locally (clone preview DOM, strip print CSS, group sections into page-break units, bake Recharts SVGs) via react-to-print, or calls POST /reports/custom-reports/export-pdf for a server-side Puppeteer render of the posted HTML.
  2. Custom report (mobile): pdf_export_service builds a PDF via pdf/printing for share/print.
  3. Generated (grant) report: the user downloads the Excel via GET /reports/generated-reports/:reportId/file (streamed).
  4. Grant template: downloadable via GET /reports/grants/:id/file.

9.7 Duplicate / finalize / delete a custom report

  1. Duplicate: POST /reports/custom-reports/:reportId/duplicate creates a "(Copy)" reset to draft.
  2. Finalize: PATCH /:reportId/status to final — subsequent non-AI edits return 423; only aiAssisted:true updates to reportData are allowed.
  3. Delete: DELETE /:reportId soft-deletes (isDeleted=true).

9.8 Provider selection & failure handling

  1. The custom-report generator uses OpenAI gpt-4o unless AI_PROVIDER=claude, in which case it calls Anthropic claude-haiku-4-5 via raw axios (anthropic-version: 2023-06-01).
  2. The PMT editor uses gpt-4o with a 30s timeout and retries on 429 (exp backoff, max 2).
  3. On missing key / provider error, the service returns an error envelope; the client surfaces a failure message and preserves the current report/sheet state.
  4. Malformed JSON from the model triggers a brace-matching fallback parse in the custom-report path before erroring.

13. Non-Functional Requirements

13.1 Security & privacy (victim safety)

  • Reporting operates on aggregated demographic/PMT data; reports and prompts must minimize exposure of individually identifying victim information. The prompt distinguishes staff-entered pmt_report demographics from app_users self-reported demographics.
  • All web AI calls are backend-routed; provider API keys are never exposed to the browser. AES response encryption applies to all reporting responses.
  • Mobile currently embeds provider keys and X-Netlify-Secret in source and calls providers directly from the device — a security gap to be closed by moving to backend-routed AI (see Risks / FR-MOB-04).
  • Agency isolation (CJIS-aligned) must hold: no cross-agency data must appear in a report; PMT edits are 403-guarded by service center.

13.2 Accuracy & data integrity

  • The AI must never fabricate, round, or substitute numbers; uploaded-file values are authoritative and override DB values; zeros must be shown as data, not omitted.
  • The PMT editor must operate at 80–100% confidence and ask questions rather than guess; every edit must be revertible via oldState.
  • The post-generation validator must flag high-severity conflicts (>0.01 mismatch) and possible hallucinations; results are surfaced to the reviewer.

13.3 Performance

  • Custom-report generation is bounded by provider latency; large reports (≥40 sections) are skeletonized to stay within token limits (max_tokens 16384).
  • The PMT editor enforces a 30s provider timeout with 429 retry (exp backoff, max 2).
  • The reporting tree is rate-limited (pmtApi ~15/5s); Luckysheet uses background autosave to avoid blocking edits.

13.4 Availability & resilience

  • Provider/model errors must degrade gracefully: current report/sheet state is preserved and the user sees a clear failure message; malformed JSON is recovered via brace-matching fallback where possible.
  • Puppeteer runs as a singleton browser for PDF export to bound resource use.

13.5 Auditability

  • Create/update/delete operations write ActionLogs rows (action, referenceType, description context). Custom-report and PMT-editor conversations are persisted (custom_report_chat_logs, pmt_report_chat_logs) for traceability. Per-datapoint provenance is recorded in ReportDataSourceMap.

13.6 Compliance & localization

  • Reporting must support VOCA/VAWA/OAG PMT program requirements (grant templates, questionnaires, PMT demographics). Grant deletion is blocked while dependent generated reports exist to protect the audit trail.
  • Reports are authored in English; the reporting UI is not localized (mobile language picker is a no-op).

13.7 Accessibility

  • Report previews and builder controls should meet standard web accessibility guidance; exported PDFs should preserve chart labels and readable contrast. Recharts SVGs are baked with viewBoxes for faithful PDF rendering.

14. Analytics, Reporting & Notifications

14.1 Reporting outputs

  • PMT/generated reports: funder Excel templates filled via Luckysheet + AI editor, exported as .xlsx.
  • Custom reports: publication-quality JSON reports (KPI/charts/tables/narrative) exported as PDF; templated types (Board, Grant Progress, Quarterly, Annual, Custom).
  • Grant dashboard: KPI cards + charts summarizing a grant's generated reports and per-questionnaire answer counts.
  • Field distribution: count/% for a questionnaire question or a whitelisted demographic field, used in the drag-drop builder.

14.2 Underlying analytics data sources

Custom-report live data is aggregated from PMT rows (getPmtReport pageLess): KPIs, demographics (race/gender/age/classification), service breakdown, inquiries (from chat_threads counts), and staff time (from staff_time_entries). The agency brain adds profile/plan, services/categories, staff headcount, partnership count, active grants, and all-time claim stats.

⚠ Placeholder data sources
Several KPIs in custom-report-data.service.js (hotlineCalls, satisfaction, shelter, financial, HR) are currently hard-coded to zero, yet the AI is told to present them as real data. Reports must not treat these as verified results until the sources are wired. See Open Questions.

14.3 Notifications

The reporting subsystem is request/response and does not emit dedicated report-ready socket/FCM notifications in the current implementation. Mobile in-app notifications are fetched by REST polling. Any future "report generated/shared" notifications would flow through the platform's standard notification delivery (DB row + Socket.IO + FCM + email). Chat-based iteration feedback (validation summary, changed sections) is delivered inline in the builder response.
