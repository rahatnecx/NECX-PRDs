2. Executive Summary

The NECX Hotline Management module is a full Twilio-backed telephony system that lets each victim-services agency operate a professional crisis and information hotline directly inside the NECX platform. Rather than asking agencies to procure and wire up their own phone system, NECX provisions a dedicated Twilio subaccount, phone number(s), and calling infrastructure per agency, then exposes a browser-based softphone and real-time "command center" so advocates can receive, route, transfer, and document calls without leaving the VS360 web app.

The module serves three distinct audiences. Agency staff (advocates, hotline coordinators, supervisors) use the VS360 command center to take inbound calls, place outbound callbacks, run live AI-assisted transcription and insight capture, and complete structured wrap-up documentation that ties a call to casework. NECX platform administrators use the Admin app to provision, migrate, connect, and cost-account the underlying Twilio infrastructure across every agency, and to configure platform-wide AI and telephony providers. Twilio itself is a first-class actor, driving the system through signed webhooks and a media-stream WebSocket.

This matters because the hotline is frequently the first point of contact between a victim/survivor of crime and the agencies that help them. Every design decision — simultaneous ring to all available advocates, conference-anchored calls that allow warm transfer, AI insights that surface safety concerns and immediate-action needs in real time, and disposition codes that flow the call into a case, inquiry, or partner referral — exists to shorten the time to a safe, competent human response and to make sure nothing said on the call is lost.

Because calls carry acutely sensitive information, the module treats privacy and safety as primary requirements: transcripts and insights are gated by per-agency toggles, socket payloads for hotline events are suppressed from debug logging to avoid leaking PII, confidential-caller handling is preserved, and provisioning supports SHAKEN/STIR verified caller identity so victims can trust that a callback genuinely originates from their agency.

3. Product Overview & Vision

3.1 Problem Statement

Victim-services agencies need a hotline but rarely have the telecom expertise or budget to run a resilient, multi-advocate call center. Manual phone trees drop calls, lack documentation, and cannot connect what a caller says to the agency's casework. Advocates handling crisis calls must simultaneously listen, de-escalate, take notes, assess safety, and remember to schedule follow-ups — a cognitive load that risks both caller safety and data quality. Supervisors have no unified visibility into call volume, outcomes, or cost.

3.2 Product Vision

Give every NECX agency a turnkey, AI-assisted hotline that is provisioned in minutes, answered from any browser, documented automatically, and fully accountable. Inbound calls ring every available advocate at once and anchor on a Twilio conference so a second advocate can join or take over mid-call. A live transcript and rolling AI insight panel surface the situation, safety concerns, children involved, and immediate-action needs while the call is still happening, then pre-fill the wrap-up form so structured documentation is a review-and-confirm step rather than a from-scratch chore. Calls end tied to a disposition that pushes them into a case, inquiry, partner referral, or scheduled callback.

3.3 Position in the NECX Ecosystem

The hotline spans three of the four NECX clients on top of the single unified backend:

  • NECX Admin (platform super-admin) provisions and operates the shared Twilio estate: master account, per-agency subaccounts, numbers, SHAKEN/STIR trust, cost analytics, and platform AI/telephony provider configuration.
  • VS360 web (agency staff) hosts the command center, softphone, live call screen, callbacks, full call log, analytics, and per-agency hotline settings.
  • Unified backend owns the Twilio webhook and media-stream endpoints, conference orchestration, transcription and insight AI pipelines, persistence, Socket.IO real-time fan-out, and Redis-backed staff availability.
  • Twilio is the telephony provider; the Victim/Workspace mobile apps are not direct hotline clients, though hotline dispositions create cases and inquiries that surface in those apps, and scheduled callbacks overlay the VS360 calendar.

4. Goals, Objectives & Success Metrics

4.1 Goals & Objectives

  • Provision a fully functional per-agency hotline (subaccount, API key, TwiML app, number, line) idempotently and resumably from the Admin app, with an option to connect to an existing/master account instead.
  • Let any available advocate answer an inbound call from the browser within seconds, with simultaneous ring, and support warm transfer and multi-advocate conferences.
  • Reduce documentation burden and improve data quality by auto-transcribing calls and extracting structured insights that pre-fill the wrap-up form.
  • Ensure no follow-up is dropped by capturing callbacks from wrap-up dispositions and surfacing them in the command center and calendar.
  • Give supervisors real-time and historical visibility (stats, analytics, full searchable call log with CSV export).
  • Give platform admins accurate per-agency and master-account Twilio cost accounting.
  • Preserve caller safety and privacy at every layer (verified caller ID via SHAKEN/STIR, gated AI, PII-safe logging).

5. Scope

5.1 In Scope

  • Per-agency hotline provisioning: setupHotlineForAgency (subaccount, API key pair, TwiML app, phone number, hotline line, optional SHAKEN/STIR), buy/release numbers, connect-to-existing/master, unlink, migrate master→subaccount, clear simulated data.
  • Browser softphone bootstrap (Twilio Voice access token) and real-time command center (staff board, call queue, recent calls, today's analytics).
  • Inbound call intake, simultaneous ring to all available staff, conference-anchored answering, incoming-call dialog, call-taken dismissal.
  • Outbound/callback calls from the browser.
  • Live AI transcription and rolling insight extraction over a Twilio media stream, with per-agency gating.
  • Wrap-up documentation with AI prefill; disposition codes that link calls to cases/inquiries/partners/callbacks.
  • Callbacks management (create/complete/cancel; overdue/today/upcoming/recent tabs; calendar overlay).
  • Manual call logging.
  • Full searchable, filterable, paginated call log with CSV export and a detail drawer.
  • Hotline analytics/stats and admin-side Twilio cost analytics (master, all, per-subaccount, per-agency).
  • Multi-advocate conference: add/remove participants, transfer primary control.
  • Per-agency hotline settings (lines, staff assignments, insight targets, AI toggles) and admin platform hotline settings (AI providers, master account profile, simulate toggles).
  • RBAC by hotline.* permission flags; Twilio webhook signature validation.

5.2 Out of Scope

  • Victim/Workspace mobile app as a direct hotline calling client (no in-app dialer). Mobile surfaces are limited to cases/inquiries created via disposition.
  • SMS/text-based hotline conversations (SMS is used elsewhere for OTP and notifications; text hotline is not part of this module).
  • IVR/menu trees, skills-based routing, or ACD queue prioritization beyond "ring all available staff."
  • Automated case/inquiry record creation logic itself — the hotline records the disposition and (for callbacks) auto-creates the callback; case/inquiry creation is owned by those modules.
  • The created_inquiry disposition from the web wrap-up (disabled — mobile-originated only).
  • Direct staff↔staff (client:→client:) bridge is a raw Twilio dial with no call record and no documentation surface.
  • Number porting from an agency's prior carrier.

Maria, hotline advocate. Maria sets her availability to "Available" at the start of her shift. When a call comes in, a floating dialog appears bottom-right with the caller's number and line. David provisions new advocates' line assignments, configures which insight fields the AI should capture, reviews the "Today's Analytics" panel each morning, and exports the prior week's call log to CSV for a grant report. Priya, NECX platform admin. When a new agency is onboarded, Priya provisions its hotline — either an isolated Twilio subaccount with its own number pool or a link to the master account — tracks each setup step, buys additional numbers, sets up SHAKEN/STIR for verified caller ID, and reviews master and per-agency Twilio cost analytics monthly. On staging she flips on "Simulate Twilio provisioning" to dry-run the whole flow without incurring charges.

7. System Context & Architecture

7.1 Tech Stack

  • Backend: Express (ESM) + Objection/Knex over PostgreSQL, Socket.IO, Redis, Twilio, and AI providers (OpenAI Whisper / GPT-4.1-mini, ElevenLabs Scribe) via an ai-provider.factory. Hotline code lives in src/apps/hotline-management/ (hotline.{routes,controller,service,validator,constants}.js), twilio/ (twilio.webhook.js, twilio.media-stream.js, conference.service.js), ai/ (insights.service.js, transcription.service.js), repository src/db/repositories/hotlineRepository.js, config src/config/hotline.config.js, and middleware src/middleware/twilio-signature.middleware.js. All routers mount under global prefix /v2.
  • VS360 web: src/pages/HotlineManagement/ (HotlineManagement.jsx, HotlineSettings.jsx, components/, hooks/, modals/, constants/hotline.constants.js), service wrapper src/services/hotlineService.js (base /hotlines), softphone via Twilio Voice SDK (@twilio/voice-sdk, src/services/twilioClient.js, src/hooks/useTwilioDevice.js).
  • NECX Admin: hotline wrappers in services/serviceCenter.js, services/adminSettings.js, services/platformSettings.js; surfaced in ServiceCenterDetails.jsx, CostAnalytics.jsx, and Settings.jsx.

7.2 Client-Backend Communication

VS360 staff routes are guarded by allowOnly('VS360') + checkPermission('hotline','view') under base /web/hotlines. Admin routes are guarded by allowOnly('ADMIN') under /admin/hotlines (plus /settings/hotline and /settings/ai-providers). Twilio webhook/media routes under /web/hotlines/twilio carry no session auth and are validated by Twilio signature (validateTwilioSignature). The authenticated principal is req.issuer; VSMS staff carry issuer.type='VSMS', issuer.serviceCenterInfo.{id,uid}, and issuer.vsmsUserInfo[0].id. Services return { status, msg?, data? }.

7.3 Real-Time & Notifications

A single Socket.IO connection (VS360 src/context/SocketContext.jsx) joins per-user room vs360-user-<uid> and agency room service-center-<id>. Hotline events (NOTIFICATION_EVENTS.HOTLINE) are registered on demand by the Hotline page via registerHotlineEvents and torn down on unmount; the debug logger suppresses hotline:* payloads to avoid leaking PII. Live audio flows from Twilio to the backend over the media-stream WebSocket /web/hotlines/twilio/media/:callId. Browser WebRTC audio flows via the Twilio Voice SDK.

7.4 Tenancy & State

Every hotline entity is scoped to a service_center_id (agency). Staff availability (available/break/offline/on_call/wrap_up) is not in the database — it lives in Redis (setStaffStatusInRedis/getStaffStatusRaw). hotline_calls.id is a UUID supplied by the caller (pre-generated at intake so the conference name hotline-<callId> and media-stream path can be formed before the DB row exists). On server startup terminateStaleCalls closes orphaned active calls.

8. Functional Requirements

8.1 Per-Agency Hotline Provisioning

Platform admins provision a hotline for an agency via POST /admin/hotlines/setup/:uid (service setupHotlineForAgency). The flow is step-tracked in hotline_configs.setupSteps (per-step {ok/fail, sid, …}), idempotent, and resumable — a retry re-runs only failed/incomplete steps. The admin selects which steps run via a checkbox modal; defaults are subaccount + TwiML app + buy number, with SHAKEN/STIR opt-in. The agency detail page renders a live SetupStepList: Twilio Subaccount → API Key Pair → TwiML App → Phone Number → Hotline Line → SHAKEN/STIR (optional), each showing success/error/pending, SIDs, and errors. Two models exist: Provision New Subaccount (isolated Twilio subaccount, own billing/number pool) and Connect to Existing (link to an existing profile or the master account via a search modal from GetTwilioAccounts).

A simulate mode (from platform hotline settings) fakes Twilio SIDs (optionally creating DB records) for dry runs without incurring charges; clearSimulatedProvisioning removes them. Agencies started on the master account can later be moved to an isolated subaccount via migrateToSubaccount (numbers change).

8.6 Inbound Call Handling

Twilio hits POST /web/hotlines/twilio/voice; handleVoice routes by From/To shape to handleVictimToHotlineCall. The active hotline_lines row is resolved by the dialed number; if none, TwiML answers "not active." All assigned staff who are Redis-available are collected (fallback: first assigned); if none, TwiML answers "all unavailable, recorded." A callId (UUID) and conference name hotline-<callId> are pre-generated; startCall creates the hotline_calls row (active, answeredByUserId = first available) and sets that staff Redis→on_call. For every available staff a browser leg (callClientIntoConference → client:userId) and, if configured, a phone leg (callPhoneIntoConference) is placed, each recorded as a hotline_call_participants row (primary, ringing) — all ring simultaneously. hotline:incoming is emitted to service-center-<id>. TwiML returns <Start><Stream> (both tracks, to the media WS) + <Dial><Conference> (startConferenceOnEnter=false, endConferenceOnExit=true) so the victim waits until staff join.

On the client, only staff with status available see the floating IncomingCallDialog (caller name/phone/line, pulsing, OS notification if tab hidden, auto-reject after 30 s). Answer accepts the WebRTC leg and opens LiveCallScreen; Reject calls PATCH /calls/:id/reject. When another staff answers first, hotline:call-taken closes all other dialogs.

8.8 Outbound & Callback Calls

handleStaffToVictimCall (From:client:X, To:+Y) finds the staff's active line, runs startCall (ringing), dials the victim into the conference (endConferenceOnExit=true, victim status callback /status?callId=…), records the staff browser leg, stores callerCallSid, and emits hotline:outbound. Victim answer → /status in-progress (or conference-join backup) flips ringing→active + hotline:call-answered. No-answer/busy/failed/completed-while-ringing → cancelUnansweredCall (completed, "No answer") + hotline:call-not-answered. On the client, the outbound/callback flow opens a ringing screen immediately (sentinel id -1), calls connectOutbound(phone), then reconciles the real DB call id from hotline:outbound; cancelling before connect disconnects the orphan. A direct staff↔staff (client:→client:) call is a raw <Dial><Client> bridge with no call record.

8.9 Live Transcription & AI Insights

Over the media WS, Twilio streams base64 mulaw 8kHz audio; the handler buffers ~1s (CHUNK_BUFFER_TARGET=50 × 20ms) per track. transcription.service.js decodes G.711 μ-law → PCM16 WAV in-process, skips sub-0.5s and silent chunks (SILENCE_RMS_THRESHOLD), drops Whisper prompt hallucinations, and delegates STT to a provider (OpenAI Whisper / ElevenLabs Scribe). Each segment persists to hotline_call_transcripts (appendTranscriptSegment) and emits hotline:transcript to the answering user. Every INSIGHTS_EXTRACTION_INTERVAL=5 segments a GPT-4.1-mini pass (extractInsightsFromTranscript) runs against the agency's configured insightTargets (or defaults: Situation, Safety Concern, Children Involved, Location, Services Needed, Perpetrator Info, Prior History, Emotional State, Immediate Action Required), upserts per target (other always appended) via upsertCallInsight, and emits hotline:insights. On audio_end a final pass runs, then hotline:insights-resolved {callId, filled, total}. All AI runs only when hotline_settings.transcriptEnabled/aiInsightsEnabled are on (insights require transcript).

8.10 Ending & Wrap-Up

endCall (PATCH /calls/:id/end or Twilio status callback) is idempotent: ringing→cancelUnansweredCall; phone-answered→completed ("Answered on staff phone", staff→available); browser-answered→wrap_up (staff→wrap_up). It terminates the Twilio conference (disconnecting the victim) and returns performed to dedupe concurrent end emits; hotline:ended fires to the agency room. completeCall (PATCH /calls/:id/complete) sets the wrap-up fields + completed, returns staff→available, and — when disposition='callback_scheduled' with a callbackDate — auto-creates a hotline_callbacks row (controller emits hotline:callback-updated). getWrapUpPrefill (GET /calls/:id/wrapup-prefill) re-runs extractWrapUpData over the full transcript to pre-fill the modal and returns collected insights.

Wrap-up fields: disposition, callCategory, callerMood, callSummary, servicesDiscussed, safety-plan + immediate-action flags, callbackDate/Time/Notes, quickTags. Disposition enum: created_case, created_inquiry (mobile-only, disabled on web), linked_to_existing_case, referred_to_partner, callback_scheduled, informational_only.

9. Detailed User & System Flows

9.1 Provision an Agency Hotline (Admin)

  1. Admin opens the agency detail page (ServiceCenterDetails.jsx) and clicks Provision New Subaccount (or Connect to Existing to link the master/another account).
  2. A checkbox modal presents the steps: subaccount, TwiML app, buy number, SHAKEN/STIR.
  3. Admin confirms; the client posts POST /admin/hotlines/setup/:uid { steps }; provisioning runs in the background.
  4. Server (setupHotlineForAgency), step-tracked in hotline_configs.setupSteps: (1) create subaccount via master api.accounts.create → store twilio_accounts row, link on hotline_configs.twilio_account_id; (2) subClient.newKeys.create → store apiKeySid/secret; (3) applications.create (voiceUrl /v2/web/hotlines/twilio/voice, statusCallback /status); (4) search a US local voice number → incomingPhoneNumbers.create bound to the TwiML app + webhooks; (5) create twilio_phone_numbers + hotline_lines (24/7, active); (6) optional non-blocking createShakenStirTrustProduct.
  5. If simulate mode is on, all Twilio calls are mocked (SIDs faked; DB records optional).
  6. The SetupStepList and status badge update on refetch, each step showing ok/fail + SID/error.
  7. If a step failed, the admin clicks Retry, which re-sends the same steps; completed steps are skipped server-side.
  8. Admin optionally buys more numbers (POST /numbers/buy/:uid), sets up SHAKEN/STIR (POST /shaken-stir/:uid), or later migrates master→subaccount (POST /migrate/:uid, typing "migrate").

9.2 Receive & Handle an Inbound Call (Advocate)

  1. Advocate sets availability to Available (PATCH /staff/my-status, stored in Redis, hotline:staff-status to the agency).
  2. Victim dials the agency number; Twilio POSTs /web/hotlines/twilio/voice; handleVoice→handleVictimToHotlineCall resolves the active line.
  3. Server collects Redis-available assigned staff (fallback first assigned); pre-generates callId + conference hotline-<callId>; startCall creates the hotline_calls row (active, first available as answeredByUserId, that staff Redis→on_call).
  4. Server places a browser leg (and phone leg if configured) for every available staff as primary/ringing participants; emits hotline:incoming to service-center-<id>.
  5. TwiML returns <Start><Stream> (media WS, both tracks) + <Dial><Conference> (startConferenceOnEnter=false, endConferenceOnExit=true); the victim waits.
  6. Each available advocate's browser shows the IncomingCallDialog (auto-reject 30 s; OS notification if hidden).
  7. Advocate clicks Answer → Voice SDK accepts the WebRTC leg → conference join → handleConference sets twilioConferenceSid, cancels all other ringing legs, sets callAnsweredVia, corrects answeredByUserId/Redis; emits hotline:call-taken (agency) + hotline:call-answered-browser (user). Other advocates' dialogs close.
  8. LiveCallScreen opens: elapsed timer, mute/hold, audio-device select, live transcript (hotline:transcript), rolling AI insights (hotline:insights), live notes (PATCH /calls/:id/notes), quick tags.
  9. If needed, advocate adds a participant (POST /calls/:id/participants) or transfers primary control (POST /calls/:id/transfer).
  10. Advocate hangs up → PATCH /calls/:id/end: browser-answered → wrap_up, conference terminated (victim disconnected), hotline:ended emitted, final insight pass → hotline:insights-resolved.
  11. Wrap-up modal (pre-filled via GET /calls/:id/wrapup-prefill): advocate confirms category, mood, summary, services, safety-plan/immediate-action flags, disposition.
  12. Complete (PATCH /calls/:id/complete) → completed, staff→available. If disposition is callback_scheduled + date, a callback is auto-created (hotline:callback-updated); created_case opens the case-create modal and links linkedCaseId.
  13. Edge cases: no available staff → TwiML "all unavailable, recorded"; inactive line → TwiML "not active"; advocate rejects → PATCH /calls/:id/reject (hotline:ended); answered on personal phone → read-only UI, auto-completed with no wrap-up.

9.3 Create & Resolve a Callback (Advocate)

  1. A callback is created either from a wrap-up (disposition='callback_scheduled' + callbackDate/Time/Notes, auto-created on completeCall) or manually via POST /callbacks (clientName, clientPhone, scheduledDate/Time, reason, assignedToUserId, originalCallId).
  2. hotline:callback-updated refreshes the agency's callbacks tab; the callback also appears in the command-center CallQueue (today/overdue) and as a read-only teal event on the VS360 calendar.
  3. At the scheduled time the assignee opens Callbacks (Overdue/Today/Upcoming/Recent) and clicks Call — the softphone (must be ready) opens a ringing screen (sentinel -1), connectOutbound(clientPhone) runs, and the real call id arrives via hotline:outbound.
  4. On victim answer (hotline:call-answered) the advocate documents the call as usual.
  5. Advocate clicks Complete (PATCH /callbacks/:id/complete, optionally completedCallId) — or Cancel (PATCH /callbacks/:id/cancel); either emits hotline:callback-updated.
  6. Edge case: victim no-answer → hotline:call-not-answered; the callback remains open for a later attempt.

9.4 Review Analytics & Call Log (Supervisor)

  1. Supervisor opens the command center; the "Today's Analytics" panel loads GET /calls/stats: stat cards (Total, Active, Avg Duration, Crisis, Cases, Safety Plans, Callbacks Today), calls-by-hour bar, category/disposition pies.
  2. For history, the supervisor opens Full Call Log (GET /calls paginated), applies filters (date range, staff, category, disposition, line, search), sorts, and toggles columns.
  3. Row click opens the detail drawer (overview, caller/staff, assessment, outcome incl. linked case → "View Case" /claims/{id}, summary, live notes).
  4. Supervisor exports visible columns to CSV for grant/PMT reporting.
  5. Admin (platform) opens Cost Analytics → Hotline: filters by account status, picks all vs. a single subaccount and a month; views the Master Account Invoice + selected breakdown (GET /hotlines/cost/{master,all,subaccount/:sid}, GET /hotlines/agencies?status=).

9.5 Simulate a Call / Provisioning (Staging)

  1. Platform admin (non-production) enables "Simulate Twilio provisioning" in Admin Settings → Hotline (PUT /settings/hotline {simulate:true, simulateCreateRecords}).
  2. Provisioning (POST /admin/hotlines/setup/:uid) now mocks all Twilio calls with fake SIDs; with simulateCreateRecords on, DB records (twilio_accounts, twilio_phone_numbers, hotline_lines) are created for UI testing.
  3. On the agency detail page the hotline detail shows a simulated flag; POST /clear-simulation/:uid removes the fake records afterward.
  4. To exercise the advocate UI without a real inbound call, an advocate with hotline.show_simulate_button clicks Simulate in the command center → POST /dev/simulate-inbound {lineId?, callerName?, callerPhone?} (dev-only; 404 in production) → a fake hotline:incoming drives the IncomingCallDialog and downstream flow.
  5. Edge case: in production the simulate button is hidden by RBAC and the dev endpoints return 404.

13. Non-Functional Requirements

  • Security: Twilio webhooks are validated by signature (twilio-signature.middleware.js); the media WS is keyed by pre-generated callId. Softphone tokens are scoped, short-lived (1 h), and identity-bound. Twilio secrets (authToken, apiKeySecret) and AI provider keys are stored server-side and masked in all UIs (blank input keeps existing). Dev/simulate endpoints return 404 in production.
  • Privacy & victim safety: Hotline socket payloads are suppressed from debug logging to avoid PII leakage. Transcription and AI insights are opt-in per agency (transcriptEnabled/aiInsightsEnabled). SHAKEN/STIR provides verified caller ID so victims can trust callbacks. Confidential-caller conventions from the wider platform are preserved. Insight extraction runs against a controlled target set; insights can be dismissed. Access is least-privilege via hotline.* flags.
  • Performance: Command-center data loads in parallel. Media is buffered ~1s per track; sub-0.5s and silent chunks are skipped; insight passes run every 5 segments (not per segment) to bound AI cost/latency. The call log uses bi-directional infinite scroll with server pagination.
  • Availability & resilience: Provisioning is idempotent/resumable with per-step retry. Call ending is idempotent across concurrent Twilio/user signals (returns performed). terminateStaleCalls closes orphaned active calls on startup. cancelUnansweredCall handles no-answer/busy/failed uniformly.
  • Auditability: Every call persists status transitions, participants, transcripts, insights, disposition, and linked casework; callbacks record originalCallId/completedCallId; provisioning steps persist SIDs and errors.
  • Cost governance: Per-agency and master cost analytics compute number rental + per-minute inbound/outbound + Twilio usage; buying a number warns if SHAKEN/STIR is unconfigured; release requires explicit "release" confirmation.
  • Localization: Lines carry a language; caller-facing TwiML and staff routing can be language-aware.
  • Accessibility: Command center and dialogs follow the VS360 web accessibility baseline (keyboard-operable controls, OS notifications for hidden tabs).
  • Compliance: Documentation fields (category, disposition, services discussed, safety plan) and CSV export support grant/PMT reporting; cost accounting supports platform financial reconciliation.
