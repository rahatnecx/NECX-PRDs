2. Executive Summary

The NECX E-Sign & Document Center gives agency staff a case-scoped place to attach documents to a victim's case, prepare those documents for signature by placing signature fields, send them for signature, and collect legally meaningful electronic signatures from victims/survivors and staff — all without leaving the NECX platform and without engaging a third-party e-signature vendor. Signatures are captured in-house (drawn on a canvas or typed in a script font, or reused from a saved signature image), stamped directly onto the PDF, and preserved with a per-signature audit trail (signer identity, signature type, timestamp, and IP address).

The feature serves two audiences from a single unified backend. Agency staff (advocates and caseworkers) work in the VS360 web app, inside the Case Management "Documents" tab of a claim/case. Victims/survivors work in the NECX mobile app, where documents awaiting their signature appear in the "Documents & Forms" area and via a home-screen e-sign banner. Because NECX serves victims and survivors of crime — a population for whom safety, privacy, and dignity are paramount — the signing experience deliberately keeps the victim inside the authenticated mobile app rather than exposing a public signing link in email.

This matters because case work generates consent forms, service agreements, releases, and acknowledgements that must be signed by the victim and/or the assigned staff member. Historically these were handled on paper or via disconnected tools, breaking the coordinated record of a case. By bringing document preparation, signing, stamping, and status tracking into the case record itself, NECX keeps the signed artifact, its status, and its audit trail attached to the claim, visible to both the agency and (for their own documents) the victim.

This document specifies the Document Center and E-Sign capability as implemented on the feature/necx-568 backend worktree and the feature/necx-607 victim-app branch (with the VS360 web surface on feature/necx-568-e-sign-and-document-center). None of these are merged to develop at the time of writing; scope, risks, and open questions reflect that pre-merge state.

3. Product Overview & Vision

3.1 Problem statement

Case work at victim-services agencies routinely requires signed documents: consent-to-serve forms, releases of information, service agreements, and acknowledgements. Without an integrated tool, staff resort to printing, wet-ink signing, scanning, and re-uploading, or to external e-signature products that live outside the case record. This fragments the record, adds friction for a vulnerable population, and leaves the signed artifact and its audit trail disconnected from the claim it belongs to. Victims, in particular, should not be pushed to a public web link in an email — both for safety (link exposure) and for a coherent, in-app experience.

3.2 Product vision

Provide an in-house, case-anchored electronic signature workflow: staff upload a document to a case, place signature fields for a victim and/or a staff signer directly on the PDF, and send it for signature. Each signer signs inside their own authenticated app — victims in the mobile app, staff in VS360 web. On each signature the system re-stamps the PDF from the pristine original, advances the document status, records an audit trail, and notifies the relevant parties. Every user may save one reusable signature image to sign faster. The signed PDF and its audit trail remain attached to the case.

3.3 Where this sits in the NECX ecosystem

E-Sign & Document Center is a feature of Case Management. It is consumed by two of the four NECX clients:

  • VS360 web (agency staff): the Document Center is the "Documents" tab of a claim/case detail page; staff upload, prepare, send, sign, view, and cancel documents, and manage their own saved signature.
  • Victim mobile app: victims see documents awaiting their signature, view the PDF, and sign; they manage their own saved signature in Settings.
  • Unified backend (necx-backend_v2): a single Express (ESM) + Objection/Knex (PostgreSQL) API exposes /web (staff) and /mobile (victim) routers for case documents and user signatures, backed by shared object storage (MinIO in dev, Azure Blob in staging/production) and a pdf-lib stamping service.
  • NECX Admin and the Workspace mobile app: no surface in this feature (no admin console or workspace-app entry point exists for e-sign).

This feature is adjacent to, but distinct from, NECX Drive (a Google-Drive-style agency file store, VS360-only, with direct-to-Azure SAS upload/download). The Document Center uses a different storage model (bytes proxied through Express) and a different identifier scheme, and is anchored to a case rather than to an agency file tree.

4. Goals, Objectives & Success Metrics

4.1 Goals & objectives

  • Let agency staff attach any relevant document to a case and manage those documents in one case-scoped list.
  • Let staff prepare a PDF for signature by visually placing per-role signature fields (victim / staff) at precise, resolution-independent positions.
  • Collect legally meaningful electronic signatures in-house from victims (mobile) and staff (web), with no external e-signature vendor and no public signing link.
  • Stamp collected signatures onto the PDF, regenerating from the pristine original each time so partial progress is visible and no artifacts compound.
  • Maintain a tamper-evident audit trail per signature (identity, type, timestamp, IP address) and per document (sent/completed timestamps).
  • Keep all parties informed via multi-channel notifications (real-time socket, in-app, FCM push, and email to the victim on request).
  • Let every user save one reusable signature image for faster signing.
  • Keep the victim experience safe: signing happens inside the authenticated app, not via an emailed link.

5. Scope

5.1 In Scope

  • Per-case Document Center: list, upload, view, and cancel documents attached to a claim/case (VS360 web); list and view documents awaiting the victim's signature (mobile).
  • Document preparation: place signature fields (victim / staff) on a PDF via a drag-and-drop editor with alignment guides; positions normalized 0..1; optional "sign with saved signature" for staff during preparation.
  • Send for signature: create signature-request records, derive required-signer flags, generate a placeholder-stamped preview, and notify signers (victim: socket + in-app + FCM + email; staff: implicit via case UI).
  • Signing: staff sign in VS360 web (draw / type / saved); victims sign in the mobile app (draw / type / saved). Role-matched pending record required; victim restricted to their own assigned signature record.
  • PDF stamping: incremental re-stamping from the original on each signature, embedding signed signatures plus dashed placeholders for still-pending positioned fields; unpositioned signatures land on an appended "Signatures" page.
  • Saved "My signature": each staff user and each victim may store/replace/remove one reusable signature image.
  • Status & audit: document lifecycle statuses; per-signature audit trail (identity, type, timestamp, IP); document sent_at/completed_at.
  • Storage per case: originals, signed copies, and signature images stored under case-namespaced object keys; bytes streamed through the backend.
  • Completion notifications: victim notified on full completion or when staff has signed and the victim is still needed; staff notified in the VS360 notification room on completion.

5.2 Out of Scope

  • Third-party / vendor e-signature (DocuSign, Adobe Sign, etc.) — signing is entirely in-house.
  • Public / email signing links or tokens — the signature-request email directs the victim to the app; there is no tokenized web signing link (contrast with NECX Drive share links).
  • External signer role (external) — the data model reserves a signer_role='external', but no upload/prepare/sign path targets external (non-victim, non-staff) signers in this release.
  • Non-PDF signature stamping — placement and stamping target PDFs; non-PDF uploads can be stored and viewed but are not field-stamped (positioned stamping is PDF-only).
  • Workspace mobile app surface — no entry point.
  • NECX Admin surface — no admin console management of case documents.
  • Storage quota / SAS direct upload for case documents — none (that model belongs to NECX Drive); case-document bytes are proxied via Express.
  • Trash / restore for documents — documents are soft-deleted via cancel only; there is no restore or empty-trash flow (contrast with Drive).
  • Decline-to-sign UI — the model supports a declined status for a signature and the document, but a first-class victim/staff "decline" action is not specified in this release (flagged in Risks / Open Questions).

7. System Context & Architecture

7.1 Tech stack

  • Backend: Node.js + Express (ESM), Objection/Knex over PostgreSQL, Socket.IO, BullMQ (email queue), Handlebars email templates. Case documents live in src/apps/case-documents/ and saved signatures in src/apps/user-signature/. PDF stamping is src/services/v2/shared/pdf/signatureStamper.js (pdf-lib). Storage is the shared src/services/v2/shared/storage/fileService.js (MinIO in dev, Azure Blob in staging/production, selected by ENV).
  • VS360 web: React/Vite + Mantine + TanStack Query. The Documents tab (DocumentsManagement.jsx), the preparation editor (PrepareSignatureModal.jsx, using react-pdf/pdf.js), the signature capture (SignatureModal.jsx, HTML canvas), and the saved-signature card (AgencySignatureCard.jsx). API wrappers in services/caseDocuments.js and services/userSignature.js; the shared axios factory prefixes /web.
  • Victim mobile app: Flutter + GetX. CaseDocumentsController / UserSignatureController, CaseDocumentsService / UserSignatureService, the "Documents & Forms" screen (signed_documents_screen.dart), the PDF viewer (document_pdf_viewer_screen.dart, Syncfusion), and signature settings (signature_settings_screen.dart). Typed signatures render in cursive fonts (GreatVibes, DancingScript, Sacramento, Allura, Parisienne, AlexBrush).

7.2 Request model & tenancy

Both routers are mounted behind the platform auth layer (populating req.issuer) and behind rateLimiter(rateLimiters.standard). The staff router is wrapped in allowOnly('VS360') (agency context); the victim (mobile) router has no allowOnly guard and relies on auth plus the per-signer ownership check. Case documents are keyed by claim_id; service_center_id is captured on the row for provenance, but read/sign paths (getDocument, streamFile, applySignature) look up purely by document uid and lean on route-level context guards and (for victims) the signerAppUserId === issuer.id check. This thin tenant re-check on read paths is flagged for security review (see Risks).

7.3 Storage model

Unlike NECX Drive (direct-to-Azure via SAS), case-document bytes are proxied through Express. Uploads arrive via upload.single('file') (multer, in-memory buffer); the service writes the buffer to a temp file and pushes it through fileService.uploadFile (temp cleaned in finally). Downloads/views stream through the backend (streamFile), setting res.locals.skipEncryption=true so the raw file bytes are not response-encrypted. There is no SAS URL and no storage quota in this feature.

7.4 Real-time & notifications

Notifications combine a DB notification row, a Socket.IO emit to the target room, and (for victims) an FCM push, plus a templated email on the signature request. Victim events use the app notification room and are routed in-app by NotificationNavigator (document-signature-request → the document sign screen). Staff completion is emitted to vs360-notification-room as case-document-signed-{claimUid}. The VS360 web UI itself surfaces mostly local toasts and TanStack Query refetches (it does not subscribe to sockets for this feature).

8. Functional Requirements

8.1 Per-case Document Center

Staff open the Document Center as the "Documents" tab of a claim/case (DocumentsManagement.jsx, keyed on claim.uid). It lists documents for the case, shows status badges, and launches prepare/sign/view actions. Each document exposes uid, title, fileName, mime, createdAt, status, signatures[], with a per-doc signer summary line (signatures.map(s => "role: status")). Victims see a parallel, filtered list in the mobile app of documents that have a victim signature record for their own account.

Status badges: draft (gray), pending_signature ("Awaiting signature", yellow), partially_signed (blue), signed (green), declined (red), cancelled (gray).

8.2 Preparing a document for signature (field placement)

Staff prepare a draft PDF in PrepareSignatureModal.jsx. The PDF is loaded via the authenticated proxy (getCaseDocumentFile) into an object URL and rendered with react-pdf/pdf.js (page width ~560px, with prev/next controls). Toolbar buttons "Victim field" and "Staff field" add draggable fields (default size ~30% × 7% of the page, staggered so repeats do not stack), each carrying {id, role, page, x, y, w, h} with coordinates normalized 0..1 and a top-left origin. Dragging shows Figma-style alignment guides that snap (~6px) to page edges/center and to other fields; each field has a remove control.

8.3 Sending a signature request

Sending (sendForSignature, POST /web/case-documents/:uid/send) supports two placement modes. In the positioned mode the body is fields[] (each {role, page, x, y, w, h, signWithSaved?}), which the backend converts into document_signatures rows (status pending, normalized coordinates). In the fallback boolean mode the body carries requireVictim/requireStaff with no coordinates, producing appended-page signatures; at least one must be required, else 400. The current VS360 UI always sends the field-array form. On send, the backend deletes any prior pending rows (clean re-send), inserts the new rows, patches the document to pending_signature with the derived requiresVictim/StaffSignature and sent_at, generates a placeholder-stamped preview, and notifies the victim.

8.4 Signer viewing & signing

Both audiences sign through applySignature (POST /:uid/sign on the respective router). The signature payload is signatureImage (data-URL data:image/png;base64,...) with signatureType (drawn|typed) and signerName, or useSaved:true to pull the signer's saved signature. Staff sign in SignatureModal.jsx (draw on a 460×160 canvas, or type a name in a script font); victims sign in the mobile app (draw/type/saved). The backend finds the caller-role pending record, authorizes it, resolves the image, uploads it, marks the record signed with signed_at and ip_address, then recomputes status and re-stamps.

8.5 Signature stamping onto the PDF

Stamping is centralized in recomputeStatus + stampSignaturesOnPdf (pdf-lib). Status is recomputed from the records: declined if any declined; signed if all non-declined are signed (sets completed_at); partially_signed if some signed; else pending_signature. The signed PDF is always regenerated from the pristine original (idempotent, no compounding artifacts), embedding all signed signatures in place plus dashed placeholders for still-pending positioned fields; unpositioned signatures land on an appended "Signatures" page. Output is saved as {uid}-signed.pdf and the document's signed_file_url/signed_storage_key are patched. Stamping failures are swallowed so they never block recording a signature.

9. Detailed User & System Flows

9.1 Prepare a document from a case (staff, VS360)

  1. Staff opens the claim/case detail and selects the Documents tab (DocumentsManagement.jsx), which loads GET /web/case-documents/claim/:claimUid.
  2. Staff clicks Add Document, enters a Title, and selects a file (PDF, image, or Word doc) → POST /web/case-documents/claim/:claimUid (multipart file, title).
  3. Backend createDocument: validates a file is present (400 if missing); looks up the claim by claimUid (404 if missing); computes storageKey = case-documents/{claim.id}/{uuid10}-{sanitizedName}; writes the multer buffer to a temp file and pushes it via fileService.uploadFile (temp cleaned in finally); inserts a case_documents row in status draft capturing service_center_id, uploaded_by_user_id, mime, size_bytes.
  4. The new document appears in the list with a gray draft badge and a Prepare & send action.
  5. Staff clicks Prepare & send → PrepareSignatureModal loads the PDF via the authenticated proxy (getCaseDocumentFile) and renders it with react-pdf.
  6. Staff adds fields with Victim field / Staff field, drags each into position (alignment guides snap to edges/center and to other fields), and optionally removes fields via their X control. Each field is {id, role, page, x, y, w, h} with normalized 0..1 coordinates.
  7. (Optional) If the staff user has a saved signature, they drag the signature chip onto the page to create a staff field flagged signWithSaved:true, signing themselves in place.

9.2 Send a signature request (staff, VS360)

  1. In PrepareSignatureModal, staff clicks Send (enabled only once ≥1 field is placed) → POST /web/case-documents/:uid/send with { fields:[{role,page,x,y,w,h,signWithSaved?}] }.
  2. Backend sendForSignature loads the document (with claimInfo.victimInfo) and resolves the victim's app-user id.
  3. It deletes any prior pending signature records (clean re-send) and inserts new document_signatures rows (status pending, normalized coordinates). For any staff field with signWithSaved:true, it fetches the staff's saved signature, copies it to sig-{uid}.png, and creates that row already signed with signed_at set.
  4. It derives requiresVictimSignature/requiresStaffSignature from the rows and patches the document: status='pending_signature', the requirement flags, and sent_at.
  5. Preview stamping: if the document is a PDF with positioned rows and nothing was auto-signed, it stamps a copy showing each field as a dashed placeholder and saves it as {uid}-signed.pdf (so "View document" reveals placement before signing). If a staff field was auto-signed, it instead calls recomputeStatus to do real stamping.
  6. Notify victim (if a victim signature is required and the victim is present): sendDocumentSignatureRequestNotification delivers a socket + in-app + FCM notification, and, if the victim has an email, an email of type DOCUMENT_SIGNATURE_REQUEST is queued with recipientName and documentTitle.
  7. VS360 shows a "Document sent for signature" toast, refetches the list (status now yellow "Awaiting signature"), and closes the modal.
  8. Edge case: if neither positioned fields nor requireVictim/requireStaff are provided, the send is rejected with 400.

9.3 Victim receives the request and signs (mobile app)

  1. The victim receives an FCM push (and, if they have an email, an email) that a document needs their signature. The email directs them to the app (no signing link/token).
  2. Tapping the push routes via NotificationNavigator (type document-signature-request) to the document sign screen; alternatively the victim opens Documents & Forms (signed_documents_screen.dart) or taps the home-screen e-sign banner.
  3. The app lists documents via GET /mobile/case-documents/ — those in pending_signature/partially_signed/signed that have a victim signature record for the logged-in issuer.id, each carrying needsMySignature and claimUid.
  4. The victim opens the document → GET /mobile/case-documents/:uid for detail and GET /mobile/case-documents/:uid/file to stream the PDF into the Syncfusion viewer (the signed/preview copy is served when present, so placeholders show where to sign).
  5. The victim signs: draws on the canvas, types their name (rendered in a cursive font), or chooses use saved → POST /mobile/case-documents/:uid/sign with {signatureType, signatureImage(dataURL), signerName} or {useSaved:true}.
  6. Backend applySignature finds the victim-role pending record (400 if none), verifies signer_app_user_id === issuer.id (403 otherwise), resolves the image (data-URL decode, or fetch saved — 400 if none), uploads it to case-documents/{claimId}/sig-{sigUid}.png, and patches the record: signer id/name/type/url/key, status='signed', signed_at, and ip_address.
  7. recomputeStatus runs (see 9.5); the app refetches and reflects the new status.

9.4 Staff signs in the web app (VS360)

  1. In the Documents tab, when a staff signature record is pending (staffCanSign), the document shows a Sign action → opens SignatureModal.
  2. Staff enters their Full legal name and picks a mode: Draw (freehand on a 460×160 canvas, with Clear/eraser) or Type (name rendered in a script font onto the canvas).
  3. On submit, the canvas is exported (toDataURL('image/png')) and POST /web/case-documents/:uid/sign is called with {signatureType:'drawn'|'typed', signatureImage(dataURL), signerName}.
  4. Backend applySignature finds the staff-role pending record, uploads the image, and patches the record (signed, signed_at, ip_address) exactly as for the victim (without the victim ownership check; staff authorization is via allowOnly('VS360')).
  5. recomputeStatus runs; VS360 shows a success toast and refetches. Status advances to partially_signed or signed.

9.5 Signature is stamped onto the PDF (system, on every signature)

  1. recomputeStatus loads the document's signature records and computes the new status: declined if any is declined; signed if all non-declined records are signed (also sets completed_at); partially_signed if at least one is signed; otherwise pending_signature.
  2. It re-stamps by calling stampSignaturesOnPdf against the pristine original (never the prior signed copy), embedding every signed signature in place and a dashed placeholder for each still-pending positioned field.
  3. For positioned signatures, normalized coordinates convert to PDF points with a bottom-origin transform (boxBottom = height − posY*height − boxH); the PNG is scaled into the field, drawn with a baseline and a caption name · signedAt, using role accent colors (victim blue, staff purple).
  4. Signatures without a position are drawn onto an appended "Signatures" page.
  5. The output is written to {uid}-signed.pdf and the document's signed_file_url/signed_storage_key are patched, so subsequent views/downloads prefer the signed copy.
  6. If stamping throws, the error is swallowed: the signature record and status change still persist (the PDF just isn't re-rendered this pass).

9.6 Completion, audit trail & completion notifications (system)

  1. When recomputeStatus finds all required signatures collected, it sets the document status to signed and stamps completed_at.
  2. The audit trail is preserved per signature: signer_name, signer_user_id/signer_app_user_id, signature_type, signed_at, and ip_address; the document carries sent_at and completed_at.
  3. Victim notifications: on full completion the victim gets a DOCUMENT_SIGNED notification ("…is now fully signed."); if instead staff just signed and the victim is still pending, the victim gets a DOCUMENT_SIGNATURE_REQUEST notification ("Staff signed… Your signature is still needed."). Both go via socket + in-app + FCM.
  4. Staff notification: on full completion, the backend emits io.to('vs360-notification-room').emit('case-document-signed-{claimUid}', …).
  5. Any party can now view the completed document; the file endpoint serves the signed copy with a signed- filename prefix and inline disposition.

9.7 Cancel / withdraw a document (staff, VS360)

  1. Staff selects Remove on a document → confirm modal → DELETE /web/case-documents/:uid.
  2. Backend deleteDocument soft-deletes: sets status='cancelled' and deleted_at.
  3. If a victim was a signer, a DOCUMENT_REMOVED notification is pushed so the document drops from the victim's app list.
  4. The document no longer appears in the case's active list (there is no restore/trash flow).

9.8 Upload a personal signature ("My signature")

  1. Staff: in Agency Profile / user settings, AgencySignatureCard calls GET /web/user-signature to show the current signature (as a data URL) if any. Victim: in the app's signature_settings_screen, GET /mobile/user-signature does the same.
  2. The user uploads an image → POST /web|mobile/user-signature (multipart file). Backend uploadSignature accepts the file if its content-type is an image or its magic bytes sniff to PNG/JPEG/GIF/WEBP (tolerating application/octet-stream).
  3. The image is stored at a versioned key user-signatures/{staff|victim}-{id}-{uuid10}.png; the principal row (users or app_users) is patched with signature_image_url/signature_storage_key; the previous image is best-effort deleted.
  4. getSignature thereafter returns the saved image as a base64 data URL (fetched from storage to avoid internal-host reachability).
  5. Remove → DELETE /web|mobile/user-signature deletes the stored object (best-effort) and nulls the columns.
  6. The saved signature is then usable via useSaved:true at sign time and, for staff, as the drag-to-sign chip in PrepareSignatureModal.

9.9 Edge cases & branches

  1. No matching pending record on sign → 400 (e.g., role already signed, or role not requested).
  2. Victim signing another victim's record → 403 (signerAppUserId !== issuer.id).
  3. useSaved:true with no saved signature → 400.
  4. Re-send after placement error → prior pending records are deleted and replaced; already-signed records are retained; status returns to pending_signature.
  5. Non-PDF document sent for signature → stored and viewable, but positioned stamping does not apply (fields/placeholders are PDF-only).
  6. Stamping failure → non-fatal; the signature is still recorded and status still advances; the signed copy simply isn't refreshed this pass.
  7. Missing file on upload → 400; missing claim → 404.

13. Non-Functional Requirements

13.1 Legal validity & tamper-evidence

  • Audit trail per signature: signer identity (name + staff/app user id), signature type (drawn/typed), signed_at, and ip_address; document-level sent_at and completed_at. These constitute the evidentiary record for an in-house electronic signature.
  • Tamper-evidence via regeneration: the signed PDF is always re-generated from the pristine original with all current signatures, so the artifact is deterministic from the recorded signature data; there is no incremental editing of a prior signed copy that could accumulate hidden state.
  • Intent & attribution: signing requires an explicit action (draw/type/use-saved) plus the signer's full legal name; captions on the stamped signature show name · signedAt.

13.2 Security & privacy (victim safety)

  • No public signing link: victims sign only inside the authenticated mobile app; the request email carries no token/link — reducing exposure risk for a vulnerable population.
  • Per-record authorization: victims can act only on their own signature record (signerAppUserId === issuer.id).
  • Context isolation: staff surface behind allowOnly('VS360'); victim surface behind platform auth.
  • Byte handling: files are proxied through Express with res.locals.skipEncryption=true for raw file streaming; uploads validated by content-type and, for signatures, magic-byte sniffing.
  • Known gap: read/sign paths look up by document uid without an explicit tenant/claim-ownership re-check in the service; access control leans on the route guards and the victim ownership check. This warrants a security review before merge (see Risks).

13.3 Performance & availability

  • Document, signature-image, and saved-signature payloads are small; PDFs are bounded by typical case-document sizes. Byte streaming is proxied, so large documents consume backend bandwidth (acceptable at expected volumes).
  • Stamping runs synchronously on send/sign but is failure-tolerant (non-fatal), so a stamping fault never blocks recording a signature or advancing status.

13.4 Auditability, compliance & reporting

  • The signature records plus document timestamps provide a self-contained audit trail suitable for compliance review; consider also emitting platform ActionLogs entries for upload/send/sign/cancel to align with the platform-wide audit pattern (see Risks).

13.5 Accessibility & localization

  • Typed signatures use script/cursive fonts (web script font; mobile cursive font family). Victim app strings are localizable (en, es, bn, fr, ar). Signature capture supports mouse and touch on both platforms.
